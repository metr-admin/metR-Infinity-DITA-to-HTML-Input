Using the Section Screens

Using the Section Screens

Press the Section button to show detailed information about the measurement data of a section. You can quickly change to another section by pressing the Section arrow buttons.

Press on a bottle image to show only that bottle and related information. Press again on the bottle image to show all bottles again. Press on a bottle to see more data about a specific cavity.
Press on a graph to zoom on the image. Press again on the graph to return to the normal view.

Press on the up and down arrow keys to change the Region. Press on the arrow keys < > to go back and forward in time.

Press the Squares Icon to expand the view and show the bottles above each other.
Press the Filter Icon to filter the graph data. The screens below show no filter (left) and one filter (right).

Section Screen Distribution Screen

The Distribution screen shows glass distribution trend graphs for each region.

Use this screen to see changes in the glass distribution, such as when you change timing settings for the IS machine.

Section Screen  Symmetry Screen

The Symmetry screen shows the trend graphs for the symmetry.

The symmetry of a region is the difference in the amount of infrared radiation (= glass) between the left (L) and the right (R) part.

Symmetry = abs ((L-R) / (L+R)) * 100%. For example:
  • Left radiation = L = 60;
  • Right radiation = R = 40;
  • Symmetry = abs ((60-40)/(60+40))*100% = 20%

Section Screen Lean Screen

The Lean screen shows the trend graph of the lean / verticality.

The lean is displayed in mm, but it can also be shown as a percentage. This can only be changed by Cortex.

Section Screen Temperature Screen

The Temperature Screen shows the trend graph of the calculated bottle temperature and the relative temperature of the bottle.

The temperature is calculated using the radiation coming from the bottle. The emissivity is a fixed setting and cannot be changed. That means the temperature is not an absolute glass temperature, but just shows the trend of the temperature for the complete bottle.

The relative temperature is the temperature difference between the measured temperature and the temperature that it is expected the bottle should have. The expected temperature is calculated by using the cooling of the bottle on the conveyor.

Section Screen Tuning Screen

The Tuning screen shows the amount of glass in eight automatically defined regions. A point to the right of the center means more glass, and a point to the left means less glass.

The blue bars show variation over the last 5 minutes. (The time period is set at the Engineer level.)
