Safety Hazards

Respirable Crystalline Silica

Crystalline silica has been classified as a human lung carcinogen. Additionally, breathing crystalline silica dust can cause silicosis, which in severe cases can be disabling, or even fatal.

Overview of the Compressor

This section describes the major compressor components. See the vendor’s manual for more details.

Components and Subsystems

Air-End: A pair of rotor assemblies rotates while the timing gear keeps a precise clearance between the male and female rotors. This non-contacting, high speed rotation of the rotor assemblies compresses the intake air. There is no oil injection into the rotor assemblies during the compression process, allowing the DSP to discharge oil free air.
  • Rotor Assembly: The surfaces of the rotors are finished with a special heat-resistant PTFE- Free coating, designed for a precise limit of clearances between the male and female rotors.
  • Bearings: The heavier male rotors are supported by bearings that are larger in diameter, so as to provide a longer service life.
  • Case: Oil jackets are provided on the case to absorb the heat of compression.
  • Timing Gears: A pair of precision finished timing gears is mounted on the ends of the rotor shafts to keep a precise clearance between the rotors and prevent contact during rotation.
  • Shaft Seal: The sealing consists of two mechanisms
    • Air seals prevent the compressed air from leaking out of the compression chamber.
    • Visco-type seals prevent the oil in the gear case from entering in the compression chamber.

  • Compressor Drive Train (Motor): The compressor employs a highly efficient, 4-pole, totally enclosed, fan cooled (TEFC) motor. The motor speed is increased by the speed up gears, and assist 1st and 2nd stage air-ends to increase rotation level properly at rated level in the gear case.

    Capacity Control System: When demand of compressed air is enough, the suction throttle valve is closed by capacity control system and the compressed air is purged into the atmosphere at the same time, for unloading the DSP air compressor.

    Inter-Cooler: The air-cooled inter-cooler is made of aluminum. The intake air is compressed up to 2.0 bar (29 psi) (and consequently generates heat in this process) by the 1st stage air-end. The intercooler cools the heated air to an atmospheric temperature +15℃ (27℉ ) and separates the moisture from the compressed air before entering the 2nd stage air-end.

    After Cooler: The air-cooled after-cooler is a corrugated fin structure with aluminum slit fin. It is located downstream of the check valve and cools the compressed air, coming from the 2nd stage air-end, down to an atmospheric temperature +15℃ (27℉) and also separates the moisture from the compressed air from the 2nd stage air-end.

    Hi-Precooler: The air-cooled Hi-precooler is located between the 2nd stage air-end and the aftercooler, and primarily cools the hot compressed air discharged by the air-end.

    Check Valve: The check valve is located downstream of the 2nd stage air-end and prevents backflow of the discharge air.

    Lubricating Subsystem: The lubricating subsystem consists of the oil pump, oil strainer, oil filter, air-cooled oil cooler and relief valve. The oil pump circulates the oil in the gear case, oil sump, through the lubrication subsystem, keeping the bearings, gears, etc. lubricated.

    Air Intake Filter: The air intake filter is a dry type, for easy cleaning or replacement.

    Cooling Fan: The low noise cooling fan cools the coolers, and exhausts the hot air from the enclosure. It also exhausts the internal compressed air that is released to the atmosphere during the unloading process.

Air/Oil Flow

Intake air is compressed and generates heat by the 1st stage air-end, and pressure builds up to approximately 2.0 bar (29 psi).

The heated air is cooled down to atmospheric temperature +15 ℃ (27 ℉) by the inter-cooler and then further compressed and heated again by the 2nd stage air-end as pressure builds up to the rated pressure level of the DSP air compressor.

The re-heated air passes through the check valve to the after-cooler, where the compressed air is cooled down to atmospheric temperature +15 ℃ (27 ℉), eventually being supplied to the plant air piping.

The oil sump is located in the bottom of the gear case. Oil is sucked through an oil strainer (oil strainer has a 120-mesh filter screen) by the oil pump and is hydraulically fed to the oil cooler where it is cooled. During the cooling process, oil passes through the high- precision oil filter and is then injected onto the bearings of the 1st and 2nd stage air-ends as well as the step-up gears. Eventually oil returns to the oil sump, where it will continue to circulate in the lubricating system.

Cooling air is inhaled from the right and left panels of the enclosure and removes the heat from the air-cooled oil cooler, inter-cooler, after-cooler and Hi-precooler. Eventually, this exchanged heat air is exhaled from the grill on the top of the enclosure.

Oil Mist Remover Subsystem

The incorporated ejector uses the compressed air to generate a vacuum pressure, which maintains a negative pressure on the oil mist remover subsystem. This prevents the gear case internal pressure from rising due to a resistance of the element.

By installing the oil mist remover:
  • There is no need to extend a gear case vent pipe outdoors, avoiding any type of trouble that may be caused by incorrect installation of the gear case vent pipe.
  • Oil mists are prevented from venting from the DSP gear case to the atmosphere, keeping the DSP compressor room/area clean.
  • Oil expenses and labor costs to add extra oil are lowered because the oil is automatically recovered in the gear case.
