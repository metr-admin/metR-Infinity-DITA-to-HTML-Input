Replacing Mixing Arms and Outer Scrapper Arms

SBS-MR-031B

Revised: 2022-10-01

Purpose/Application

Defines the procedures for replacing the mixing arms and the outer scrapper arms for the pan mixer.

PPE Required

General

Bump cap, safety glasses, hearing protection, long sleeves, steel toed safety shoes.

Have available a respirator with certified cartridges, or a PAPR, in case of a silica release or toxic batch material spill.

Safety Hazards

Respirable Crystalline Silica

Crystalline silica has been classified as a human lung carcinogen. Additionally, breathing crystalline silica dust can cause silicosis, which in severe cases can be disabling, or even fatal.

Safety Controls

  • Lockout-tagout-verify the mixer before performing maintenance work. Follow plant procedures.
  • The Hazard Communication Standard (HCS) requires chemicals to have Safety Data Sheets (SDS), please refer to the SDS for chemicals listed in this SOP for additional hazards, first aid measures, toxicological information, accidental release measures, and handling/storage of the chemical.
  • Review the Emergency Action Plan before performing work.

Equipment Needed

  • Access to the mixer control panel or the SCADA control station.
  • Hand tools as determined by the maintenance personnel.

Comments

  • Read a section thoroughly before starting the work. Personnel performing the tasks in this document must have completed the required training.
  • For additional information, refer to:
    • SBS-ET-031 Troubleshooting the Pan Mixer
    • SBS-OP-031 Operating the Pan Mixer
    • Vendor manual in the Resources library

Replacing Mixing Arms and Outer Scrapper Arms

  1. Replacing the top part of the mixing arm and/or the outer scraper arm:
    • Unscrew the adjustment screws (6 & 11) far enough for the spring (8) and plug bolt (4) to be pulled out.
    • Remove the insertable sheet (10).
    • Remove the feather key (7) by moving the adjusting rings (5 & 9) to the side.
    • Pull the top part of the mixing arm (14) out of the rotor case (12).
    • To reassemble, complete the steps in reverse order.
  2. If replacing only the bottom part of the mixing arm (5 in image on the right):
    • Remove the screws (3, 4), and remove the threaded plate (1).
    • The arm should be free.
    Note:

    Re-tighten the connections between the top part of the mixing arm and the bottom part of the mixing arm after 5 hours of operating time.

  3. Replacing the mixing arm bearings:
    • The mixing arm bearings (1) are bushings that are inserted into the drilled holes of the rotor, and into which the mixing arms are inserted.
    • The mixing arm bearings can only be replaced after the mixing arms have been removed.
  4. Replacing the mixing arm scrapers:
    • The scraper (2) is a sealing ring that is inserted into the same drilled hole in the rotor into which the mixing arm is inserted. (See image in step 3 above.)
    • The scrapers can only be replaced after the mixing arms have been removed.
  5. Replacing the mixing paddles:
    • Undo the screws (1), and remove the paddles (2).
    • Before installing the new mixing paddles, soak an intermediate textile or paper gasket layer (3) in water.
    • Place the paper layer between the mounting part and the mixing paddle.
    • Ensure that the mixing paddles (2) are attached so that their top edge overlaps the mounting part.
    • Readjust the paddle height using the slotted holes.
    • Before tightening up the fastening screws, make sure that there is a parallel gap between the bottom of the paddle and the trough floor wear liner (1-2 mm, or 0.04 to 0.08 inch).
    Note:

    Re-tighten theconnection between the mixing paddles and the scraper paddles after five hours of operating time.

    Note:

    Inspect all mixing paddles at regular interval s for wear and damage. Replace if necessary. Using worn or damaged paddles can lead to serious damage to the mixer.
