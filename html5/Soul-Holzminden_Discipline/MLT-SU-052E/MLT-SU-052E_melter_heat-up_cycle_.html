Melter Heat-Up Cycle

MLT-SU-052E

2023-05-25

Purpose/Application

Defines the procedure to heat up the melter and light the floor burners.

PPE Required

Bump cap, safety glasses, hearing protection, long sleeves, steel-toed safety shoes, heat-resistant gloves.

Safety Hazards

Safety Controls

  • Hot surfaces will be present in areas where hot molten glass is being produced. Use caution and proper hand and body protection while working in areas where hot molten glass is being produced.
  • Air monitoring device, ventilation (when in confined space).
  • Confined space authorization.
  • Review the Emergency Action Plan before performing work.

Equipment Needed

Access to DFC software.

Comments

  • Use the buddy system.
  • Some sections in this document require specific PPE and tools/equipment to perform tasks, and may also have specific safety hazards. Read a section thoroughly before starting the work. Personnel performing the tasks in this document must have completed the required training.
  • Personnel must have valid certification in Operation and Maintenance of Gas Systems (up to 5 bar) by the appropriate company before working on gas skids.

Melter Heat-Up Cycle

Follow this procedure to heat up the melter and light the floor burners:

  1. Select primary gas and oxygen SSOVs.
  2. Once the top thermocouple readings exceed 750 °C (1382 °F), press the Acknowledge button on the Melter HMI Safety tab.
    • The Auto Ignition Top Temperature indicators will turn green.
  3. Verify that the gas and oxygen SSOVs are open.
  4. Once the top thermocouple readings exceed 800 °C (1472 °F), use the switches on the Melter Combustion Skids to light floor burners.
    • Start with burner 1.
    • Turn the switch to the START position.
    • If the burner has successfully lit, the light above the switch will be on.
    • Turn the switch to the on position.
    • If the light doesn’t turn on, turn the switch back to OFF. Inspect the SSOVs for issues and repeat the ignition attempt.
    • Wait for 30 seconds before lighting the next burner.
    Note:

    Do not light burners 2, 3, and 4 until after the heat-up burner is removed (in step 10).

  5. In DFC, go to the Melter H3F screen and click the H3F Melter Energy from the dropdown menu.
    • Click the Main Burners Startup tab.
    • Check the Burner Bypass boxes for burners that have not been started yet.
    • Click the Main Burners to Normal Operation button.
    Note:

    If the Normal Operation button is not functional, lower the Main Burner Ramping Target Temperature setpoint below the current melter temperature.

  6. Click the Melter Energy (BRN 1-15) and Melter Energy (BRN 16-24) tabs.
    • Set the burners that are running to Remote mode.
  7. Increase the gas flow to each burner until the melter temperature reaches 875 °C
  8. Once the melter temperature is at or above 875 °C (1607 °F), turn off the heat-up burner.
    • Go to the heat-up skid.
    • Press the Brenner Aus button.
    • Wait 10 seconds and press the Ventilator Aus button.
    • Lock out the heat-up skid gas supply.
  9. Note:
    Remove the heat-up burner.
    • Verify that the heat-up burner door jackbolts are completely flush with the inner surface of the door.
    • Close the heat-up burner door and secure the yellow clamps around the perimeter.
    • Set the heat-up burner down so it is not contacting any part of the moving structure.
  10. Uncheck the Burner Bypass boxes for burners 2, 3, and 4.
    • Turn on burners 2, 3, and 4.
    • Set all burners to Normal Operation and Automatic Remote.
