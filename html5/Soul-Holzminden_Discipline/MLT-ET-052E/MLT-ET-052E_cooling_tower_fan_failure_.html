Cooling Tower Fan Failure

MLT-ET-052E

Revised: 2021-06-25

Purpose/Application

Defines a reaction plan for a cooling town fan failure.

PPE Required

General

Bump cap, safety glasses, hearing protection, long sleeves, steel-toed safety shoes.

Required for Specific Sections

Aluminized apron and chaps, face shield with shaded lens for optical radiation hazard, heat resistant balaclava, heat resistant gloves, cut resistant gloves, face shield, ear plugs/ear muffs.

Safety Hazard

Safety Controls

  • Exposure to heat can cause illness and death. The most serious heat illness is heat stroke. Other heat illnesses include heat exhaustion, heat cramps, and heat rash. Precautions should be taken to avoid heat illness any time temperatures are high, and the job involves physical work.
  • If a task causes exposure to hot molten glass, the appropriate shaded eyewear must be worn to decrease exposure to infrared radiation.
  • Hot surfaces will be present in areas where hot molten glass is being produced. Use caution and proper hand and body protection while working in areas where hot molten glass is being produced.
  • Review the Emergency Action Plan before performing work.

Tools & Equipment

  • Air hammer, long pry bar, flex-tape, adjustable wrench, neoprene sheets, measuring tape, utility knife, zip ties, locking tape, locking pliers, water lance.
  • Access to DFC software.

Comments

  • Use the buddy system.
  • Stay hydrated.
  • Some sections in this document require specific PPE and tools/equipment to perform tasks, and may also have specific safety hazards. Read a section thoroughly before starting the work. Personnel performing the tasks in this document must have completed the required training.

Cooling Tower Fan Failure

Follow this procedure in the event of a cooling tower fan failure

  1. Inspect the PVAG control panel for alarms.
    • Inspect the alarm log.
  2. Inspect the system for physical signs of equipment failure.
    • Damaged belts on the fan, smoking or overheating motors, etc.
    • Inspect the fan VFD for alarms.
  3. Call maintenance immediately to make the necessary repairs.
    • Reset the VFD to Auto mode once repairs are complete.
  4. If the fan cannot be repaired or the closed loop water temperature reaches the High alarm setpoint, a controlled drain is required.
    • Follow Steps 5.1–5.6 to drain the melter.
    • If cooling water supply temperature is within 5 °C (41 °F) of the High-High alarm setpoint during the drain, close the secondary exit and perform an E-Stop of the melter.
    • Refer to document MLT-MR-051-DE, General Melter Maintenance, if needed.
    Note:

    Reduce the melter temperature to slow the rate of the water temperature rise.

  5. Note:

    PPE Required: Aluminized apron and chaps, face shield with shaded lens for optical radiation hazard, heat resistant balaclava, heat resistant gloves.

    Note:

    Use the buddy system. Stay hydrated.

    1. In DFC, go to the Melter H3F screen, and click the H3F Melter Energy tab on the dropdown menu.
      • Verify the oxygen-gas ratio is 2.30.
      • Lower Gas Flow SP in increments of 1.5 Nm³/h (0.056 MSCFH) to keep melter temperature below 1400 °C (2552 °F).
      • Click the Apply to All Burners button.
    2. Go to the Melter H3F screen and click the Control Overview tab.
      • Click the Charger Profile button and check the Suppress checkbox to stop batch charging.
    3. Start the melter scraper.
    4. Note:

      PPE Required: heat resistant gloves, heat resistant balaclava, aluminized apron and chaps, face shield with shaded lens for optical radiation hazard.

      Use caution.
      Turn on cullet water to the drain chute.
    5. Note:

      PPE Required: heat resistant gloves, aluminized apron and chaps, face shield with shaded lens for optical radiation hazard, ear plugs/ear muffs.

      Use caution.

      High noise level hazard.

      Tools Required: Air hammer, long pry bar.

      Raise the secondary exit door by using the switch located on a nearby column.
      • Use the air hammer or long pry bar to break through the frozen layer of glass if needed.
      • Glass should stream out.
      Note:

      Adjust the height on the secondary exit door as needed.

      • Use the long pry bar to maintain a controlled flow of glass through the opening, and move big chunks of glass into the water flow to cool down.
      • Close the secondary exit door once the melter is empty.
    6. Go to the burner skids.
      • Use the burner lever switches to turn off the melter burners one at a time.
      Note:

      Nitrogen will automatically flow through the burners until the melter temperature falls below 500 °C (932 °F).
