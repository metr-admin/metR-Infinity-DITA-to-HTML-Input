Servicing the Cooling Water Filters

MLT-MR-052A

Revised: 2021-07-15

Purpose/Application

Defines procedures for servicing the cooling water filters.

PPE Required

Bump cap, safety glasses, hearing protection, long sleeves, steel-toed safety shoes, cut-resistant gloves.

Servicing the Cooling Water Filters

Follow this procedure to service cooling water filters:

  1. If the open-circuit or closed-circuit cooling water flow rates are low, the filters may be plugged.
    • There is one water filter on the open circuit, and one water filter on the closed circuit.
    • Go to the basement and inspect the differential pressure gauge on the affected filter.
  2. If the closed circuit filter pressure drop is above the high limit:
    • Open the filter bypass valve.
    • Close the filter inlet and outlet valves.
  3. Note:

    Caution: Wet floor.
    Open the flush valves to backflush the filter.
    • Activate the cleaning cycle on the controller.
  4. To switch the filter back online:
    • Close the flush valves.
    • Open the filter inlet and outlet valves.
    • Close the bypass valve.
    • If the pressure drop is still above the high limit, there may be an issue with the filter element or cleaning mechanism.
    Note:

    If the filter housing is opened, verify that the sealing surfaces are clean, and the gasket is seated properly.

  5. The open circuit filter flushes automatically.
    • If the open circuit filter pressure drop is above the high limit, there may be an issue with the controller or automatic flush valves.
    • Bypass the filter and contact maintenance to service the filter.
