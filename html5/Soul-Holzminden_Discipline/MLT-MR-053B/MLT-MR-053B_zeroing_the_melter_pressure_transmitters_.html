Zeroing the Melter Pressure Transmitters

MLT-MR-053B

Revised: 2023-04-13

Purpose/Application

Processes for zeroing the melter pressure transmitters.

PPE Required

General

Bump cap, safety glasses, hearing protection, long sleeves, steel-toed safety shoes.

Required for Specific Sections

Respirator or PAPR, cotton forming gloves, heat-resistant gloves, face shield with shaded lens for optical radiation hazard.

Safety Hazards

Respirable Crystalline Silica

Crystalline silica has been classified as a human lung carcinogen. Additionally, breathing crystalline silica dust can cause silicosis, which in severe cases can be disabling, or even fatal.

Safety Controls

  • The Hazard Communication Standard (HCS) requires chemicals to have Safety Data Sheets (SDS), please refer to the SDS for chemicals listed in this SOP for additional hazards, first aid measures, toxicological information, accidental release measures, and handling/storage of the chemical
  • Exposure to heat can cause illness and death. The most serious heat illness is heat stroke. Other heat illnesses include heat exhaustion, heat cramps, and heat rash. Precautions should be taken to avoid heat illness any time temperatures are high, and the job involves physical work
  • If a task causes exposure to hot molten glass, the appropriate shaded eyewear must be worn to decrease exposure to infrared radiation.
  • Hot surfaces will be present in areas where hot molten glass is being produced. Use caution and proper hand and body protection while working in areas where hot molten glass is being produced.
  • Follow donning and doffing procedure.
  • Air monitoring device, ventilation (when in confined space).
  • Review the Emergency Action Plan before performing work.

Tools & Equipment

  • HEPA vacuum, long pry bar, mallet, reinforced shovel, air chisel, MSA air monitor.
  • Access to DFC software.

Comments

Some sections in this document require specific PPE and tools/equipment to perform tasks, and may also have specific safety hazards. Read a section thoroughly before starting the work. Personnel performing the tasks in this document must have completed the required training.

Zeroing the Melter Pressure Transmitters

Follow this procedure to zero the melter pressure transmitters:

  1. Note:

    PPE Required: Cotton forming gloves.

    Caution: Hot surfaces.
    Go to the melter pressure transmitter.
    • Close the process valve.
    • Open the equalizing valve.
  2. Note:

    PPE Required: Cotton forming gloves.

    Caution: Hot surfaces.
    If the reading is 0 kPa (0 in H₂O):
    • Close the equalizing valve.
    • Open the process valve.
  3. If the reading is not 0 kPa (0 in H₂O):
    • Verify that the process valve is closed.
    • Verify that the equalizing valve is open.
    • Zero the pressure transmitter.
  4. Note:

    PPE Required: Cotton forming gloves.

    Caution: Hot surfaces.
    Remove the cap from the front of the pressure transmitter.
    • Press E to enter the main menu.
    • Press + until Quick Setup is displayed.
    • Press E to enter the Position Zero Adjust menu.
  5. Note:

    PPE Required: Cotton forming gloves.

    Caution: Hot surfaces.
    Perform a Position Zero Adjust using the –, +, and E buttons.
    • Verify that the measured value goes to 0 mm H₂O (0 in H₂O).
    • Press the – and + buttons simultaneously twice to return to the main screen.
    • Close the equalizing valve.
    • Open the process valve.
