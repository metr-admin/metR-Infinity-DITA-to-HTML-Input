Normal Operation

In normal operation the rectifier supplies the inverter and charges the battery. The inverter supplies the load.

In normal operation the inverter supplies the load.

The rectifier mains input feeds the phase angle controlled rectifier via a matching transformer and converts the AC to DC. The rectifier maintains a constant DC by compensating for both variations in the input voltage and load.

The inverter converts the DC to AC with optimized sine wave pulse width control (PWM).

For systems with a bypass: If the inverter is unavailable or overloaded (by default, load at 150% for more than 1 minute or load at 125% for more than 10 minutes), the system will change to bypass operation. For more information see chapter Bypass Operation (Static Bypass).

For systems with manual bypass switches: When the load is supplied in normal operation the manual bypass switch is always in Auto position.
