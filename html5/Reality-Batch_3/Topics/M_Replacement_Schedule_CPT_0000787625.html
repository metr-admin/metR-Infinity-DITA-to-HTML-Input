Replacement Schedule

As part of the standard replacement schedule the following parts need to be replaced.

Table 1. Standard Replacement Parts
Part Name Replacement Cycle Notes
Rectifier modules Every 8-10 years
Inverter modules Every 5-6 years
DC/DC Converter modules Every 8-10 years
Cabinet fans Every 5-6 years Not all systems have cabinet fans, see Parts List or Drawings.
Fan monitoring card Every 8-10 years Not all systems have a fan monitoring card, only systems with cabinet fans contain a fan monitoring card. See Parts List or Drawings.
Surge arrester Every 8-10 years The replacement cycle depends on quality of the mains. If the alarm “Surge voltage prot. tripped” is active replace the tripped input surge arrester immediately.
Fuses Every 8-10 years Not all systems have fuses, see Parts List. Replace immediately when a fuse has blown or is damaged.
Power supply units (PSU) Every 8-10 years
Controller Every 8-10 years Includes integrated communication card.
Display Every 8-10 years
Battery See documentation from the battery manufacturer

As part of an extended replacement schedule it is recommended to replace the following parts, if applicable.

Table 2. Extended Replacement Parts
Part Name Replacement Cycle Notes
Static switch modules Every 8-10 years
Input/output card Every 8-10 years
DC measurement card Every 8-10 years
AC measurement card Every 8-10 years
Battery monitoring card Every 8-10 years
Insulation monitoring card Every 8-10 years
