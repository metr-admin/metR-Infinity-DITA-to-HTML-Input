Voltage Dropper

Notice
TOO HIGH OUTPUT VOLTAGE
Do not change any settings on the voltage dropper PLC. It is programed and tested in factory for the specific system.

The voltage dropper is a controlled voltage stabilizing device. It is used for maintaining the output voltage within a given range, for example Â±5% of the nominal voltage. The voltage dropper is used if the voltage range of the connected battery is larger than the voltage tolerance on the output.

The voltage dropper can have a maximum of four diode stages. Each stage can contain multiple diodes. One diode will reduce the voltage with about 0.8 V, so the total reduction of a stage can be multiples of about 0.8V. The stages can contain different amounts of diodes.

A PLC is used to monitor the output voltage. The PLC can deactivate a diode stage to increase the output voltage or activate a diode stage to decrease the output voltage. A stage is deactivated by closing a contactor to create a bridge over the stage. A switching hysteresis prevents constant switch-over if the output voltage is near a threshold.

Note: Press OK once on the PLC to illuminate the PLC display for about 30 seconds.

If the natural cooling inside the system is not sufficient to remove the heat generated by the voltage dropper additional fans are installed below the voltage dropper.

There are two optional alarms for the voltage dropper that can be displayed on the front panel. One alarm in case the output after the voltage dropper is out of tolerance and one alarm if the voltage dropper fans are inoperable. For more information see Alarm Panel.
