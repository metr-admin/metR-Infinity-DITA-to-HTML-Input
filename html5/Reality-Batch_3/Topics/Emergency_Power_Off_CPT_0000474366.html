Emergency Power Off

Warning
ENERGIZED BY external power sources
When emergency power off (EPO) is activated the load is no longer supplied by the system. The system is still energized by external input power sources and/or by the batteries.

According to UPS safety standard IEC 62040-1 (2017), a UPS with an emergency switch device should be able to prevent further supply of the load. The emergency switching device can interrupt the load internally, or via a terminal to an externally connected switching device.

The emergency power off (EPO) is integrated on the external connection board A025. To use the EPO J3 needs to be open on A025.

There are two types of the EPO:

  • EPO with internal power supply (J2 closed). Activates when X107:1 and X107:2 is open.

  • EPO with external power supply (J2 open), voltage range: 9-24 V DC. Activates when X107:1 and X107:2 is open or when the external power supply to X107:3 and X107:4 is off.

    Note: The EPO is fail-safe in both options.

With EPO the load is de-energized by turning off the system (Standby), the rectifier, the inverter and the static switch(es).
