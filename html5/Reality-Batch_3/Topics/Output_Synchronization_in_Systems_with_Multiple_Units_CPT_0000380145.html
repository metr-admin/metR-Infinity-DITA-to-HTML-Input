Output Synchronization

When units work in parallel the outputs must be synchronized. The parallel communication shares the synchronization signal between the inverters.

Note: The bypass mains needs to come from the same source even if the units have individual bypass lines.

Output synchronization:

  • The bypass synchronization signal is measured separately by each inverter.

  • One of the two inverters will operate as the primary (inverter 1) and send the bypass synchronization signal to the internal communication bus.

  • The secondary (inverter 2) will synchronize to the internal communication bus so that the two inverter outputs are synchronized with the bypass.

In addition:

  • The secondary inverter will immediately become primary if the primary inverter is not able to synchronize to the bypass, if the secondary inverter can synchronize to the bypass.

  • Each inverter has an internal oscillator. If the synchronization signal from the bypass is missing or out of tolerance for both inverters, the internal oscillator of the primary will take over.
