General Safety Precautions

DANGER
HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, OR ARC FLASH
  • All safety information in this document must be read, understood and followed.
  • Only qualified personnel are allowed to install, operate and perform maintenance on the system.
  • Hazardous voltages are present as soon as the cabinet door is opened. Only qualified electrical personnel are allowed to open the cabinet.
  • Isolate all power supplies (including the battery) before working on or inside the system.
  • Always use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE).
  • Reinstall all parts and covers, and close the doors before turning on any AC power supply or connecting a DC power source to the system.
  • Follow safe electrical work practices. See NFPA 70E or CSA Z462.
DANGER
HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, OR ARC FLASH
Always wait 5 minutes after turning off the system and isolating all the power supplies (including the battery). The system contains DC capacitors with long discharge time.
DANGER
high leakage current
The system must be connected to protective earth. Always connect the system to protective earth before connecting any power supply.
DANGER
HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, OR ARC FLASH
  • Never drill or cut holes for cables or conduits with the gland plates installed.
  • Never drill or cut holes near the system.
Warning
Hazardous vapors
Fire inside the system can produce hazardous vapors that should not be inhaled.
Important: Only use fire extinguishers with CO2 to put out fires in or close to the systems.
CAUTION
unintended Equipment operation
Never connect the system output to regenerative loads, for example, photovoltaic systems or speed drives.
Notice
damaged BAtteries
If the load is not automatically disconnected when the low DC shutdown signal is activated, the connection between the battery bank and the load must be manual opened to avoid permanent damage to the batteries.
