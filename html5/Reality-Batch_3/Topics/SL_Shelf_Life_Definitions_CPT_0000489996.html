Shelf Life Definitions

Reality defines shelf life as follows:

“The time from manufacturing or assembly that an electronic component or mechanical part can be stored at the defined storage conditions and is usable without testing for the full design life.“

To clarify:

  • From when: Manufacturing or assembly date.

  • To when: Manufacturing or assembly date + shelf life.

  • Storage condition: Defined storage conditions and packing requirements.

  • Status after storage: Can be used for the full design/operation lifetime if the spare part has been stored correctly.

The manufacturing or assembly date is found on a label or as a marking on the component.
