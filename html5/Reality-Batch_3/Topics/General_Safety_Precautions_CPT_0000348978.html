Safety Precautions for Maintenance

DANGER
HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, OR ARC FLASH
Before performing any maintenance inside the system:
  • Read the safety information
  • Use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE)
  • Isolate all the power supplies (including the battery) from the system and use lockout-tagout (LOTO)
  • Wait 5 minutes after isolating the system to let the DC capacitors discharge
  • Check that any removable (NC) earth link is closed
  • Check that the parts, capacitors, terminal and protective earth are volt free with a properly rated voltage sensing device
  • Check that protective covers are installed on any energized parts
Note: Only open the removable earth link when troubleshooting an earth fault.
DANGER
HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, OR ARC FLASH
Always use isolated tools when removing the protection covers inside the cabinet (metal sheets and plastic covers).
DANGER
HAZARDOUS VOLTAGE
Never perform maintenance on the system alone. While working on systems with hazardous voltages always have a second person close in case of emergency.
DANGER
HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, OR ARC FLASH
When working on batteries always use:
  • Eye protection
  • Protective rubber gloves
  • Protective rubber apron
  • Protective rubber boots
  • Insulated tools
When working on batteries:
  • Always disconnect the charging source
  • Always remove from earth if inadvertently earthed
  • Always remove watches, rings, or other metal objects
  • Never place tools or metal objects on top of batteries
DANGER
HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, OR ARC FLASH
When replacing batteries, always replace with the same type and number of batteries or battery packs.
