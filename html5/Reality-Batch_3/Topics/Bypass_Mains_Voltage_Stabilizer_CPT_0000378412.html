Bypass Mains Voltage Stabilizer

The bypass mains stabilizer T090 is a combination of a variable auto transformer T2, a motor M1, a booster transformer T1 and an electronic controller N1.

  • (M1) Motor
  • (N1) Electronic controller
  • (T1) Variable auto transformer
  • (T2) Booster transformer

The electronic controller N1 compares the output voltage with the adjusted set-point value. Depending on the difference between the measured and set-point value, the motor M1 will adjust the variable auto transformer T2.

The adjusted voltage of the variable auto transformer T2 is transferred to the primary winding of the booster transformer T1, which is rated for the maximum bypass mains voltage deviation. The secondary winding of the booster transformer T1 is placed in the line branch of the bypass mains to compensate for voltage variations.

With the bypass mains stabilizer T090 the actual value of the output voltage will be observed with an accuracy of ± 1 % at a deviation of the incoming bypass mains voltage of ±10 / ±15 / ±20 % of the set-point value (depending on type of transformer). A change of the load has no influence on the accuracy of the output voltage.
