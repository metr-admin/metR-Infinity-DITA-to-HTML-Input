Semi-Annual Battery Maintenance

DANGER
HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, OR ARC FLASH
When working on batteries always use:
  • Eye protection
  • Protective rubber gloves
  • Protective rubber apron
  • Protective rubber boots
  • Insulated tools
When working on batteries:
  • Always disconnect the charging source
  • Always remove from earth if inadvertently earthed
  • Always remove watches, rings, or other metal objects
  • Never place tools or metal objects on top of batteries
DANGER
HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, OR ARC FLASH
When replacing batteries, always replace with the same type and number of batteries or battery packs.

It is important with regular battery maintenance to ensure that the batteries are in good condition. To help monitor and maintain the condition of the batteries the Reality battery management system (G.BMS) can be used. The G.BMS actively balances and continuously monitors important parameters of each battery block. For more information please refer to the G.BMS Reality Web Page.

  1. Clean the battery cell covers with damp lint-free cloth. Check the terminals for any corrosion and if necessary, clean and slightly grease the poles and links.
    Note: Only use water to dampen the lint-free cloth. Never use any solvents or scouring powders.

  2. Check the tightening torque of the pole screws, see the documentation from the battery manufacture for torque specifications.
  3. Check the battery with the rectifier switched on. Measure and record:

    • The battery temperature.

    • The string voltage.

    • Float current.

    • Inter-cell resistance.

    • Internal ohmic measurements (impedance), if applicable.

    • The voltage of each cell.

    Note: The measurements need to be recorded manually or done with the G.BMS (or other battery management system).
  4. Any other battery maintenance recommended by the manufacturer of the battery. For more information refer to documentation from the battery manufacture.
