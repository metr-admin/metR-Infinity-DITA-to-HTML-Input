Fasten the Cabinet to the Floor Using Inside Access

DANGER
HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, OR ARC FLASH
  • Make sure that the input power supply and any batteries have not yet been connected.
  • Before working inside the cabinet always use a properly rated voltage sensing device to check for hazardous voltage between all terminals, including the protective earth (PE).
  1. Remove the screws (E) and washers (F) attaching the cover plates at the bottom.
    Note: Depending on the system configuration it might be necessary to remove more than one cover for access.
  2. Remove the cover plate (G).
  3. Unscrew and remove the clips (H) on both sides of the gland plates (C). Then remove the gland plates (C).
    Note: Depending on the system configuration the number and position of the gland plates (C) might be different. For more information see the Drawings.
  4. Fasten each corner of the plinth to the floor at the location (B).
  5. Place the gland plates (C) and attach the clips (H) on both sides of the gland plates (C). Tighten the screws in the clips (H) to a torque of 2.5 Nm (22.12 lbf-in).
  6. Attach the cover plate (G).
  7. Attach and tighten the screws (E) and washers (F).
