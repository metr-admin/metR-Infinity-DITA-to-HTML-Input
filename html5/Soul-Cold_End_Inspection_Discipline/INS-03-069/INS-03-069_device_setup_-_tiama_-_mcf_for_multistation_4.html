Device Setup - Tiama - MCF for MultiStation 4

INS-03-069

Revised: 2017-01-23

Purpose and Application

Purpose

To define a work instruction for proper setup of the MCF on Multi Station 4.

Application

This work instruction applies to all Soul manufacturing facilities

Safety

Observe Soul standard safety requirements and procedures defined by the Global Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) organization, as well as all applicable local, regional, and national requirements. Safety equipment and instructions specific to the completion of this work instruction are detailed in the Instructions Section.

Instructions

  1. Before starting the work instruction activity, perform the following steps:
    1. Do not begin these tasks without confirmation that personnel directly involved have completed the required training associated with the work instruction activity and have reviewed this document.
    2. Gather necessary tools and equipment as identified below in the Equipment Section.
    3. Wear and use additional Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safety equipment required for this specific work instruction.
    4. Obey all applicable safety requirements and procedures.
    5. Notify appropriate personnel of the activity and estimated the time needed to complete the tasks.
    6. Clearly identify, mark off, and secure the designated area defined by the work instruction activity.
    7. Follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures when work instruction activity requires isolating energy in the designated area. Verify energy is isolated before starting work.
    8. Only after energy is verified to be isolated, can the protective guarding be removed and safety interlock gates are opened.
  2. For proper setup, perform the following steps:
    1. If required, perform CSBC LO work instruction before completing this device setup.
    2. Obtain quality approved challenge samples for all base defects.
    3. Stop the flow of containers into the machine.
    4. Using the key operated switch, place the machine into manual mode.
    5. Jog challenge sample under the MCF camera.
    6. Click on Detections –> MCF (Base inspection) on the User interface screen.
    7. Click Adjustments – Optical Setup.
    8. Click Permanent to turn on light source.
  3. For proper camera setup, perform the following steps:
    1. Select the image alone button. Verify the proper lens is installed according to MSDR INS-07-038 Parts List.
    2. Use back and forth switch on the front of the machine to move the challenge sample container under the camera. View the setup screen to center the container.
    3. Move the camera close to the container by using the adjustment crank.
      1. For a narrow neck – set camera approximately 5 mm above the finish.
      2. For a wide mouth – Adjust height to get a good picture on the screen that fills the whole screen.
      3. Base stress camera is mounted to the same device and moves up and down with the MCF camera – It is important to confirm clearance for this camera as well.
      4. The further the source is from the base of the container, the more contrast the image has. So the source needs to be further away to highlight defects with low contrast.
      5. Center the light source underneath the container.
    4. If job was loaded from history, confirm parameters.
  4. To adjust the focus of the camera, perform the following steps:
    1. Begin focusing the camera using the adjustment ring of the camera (further away from the camera), while looking visually at the setup screen.
    2. Oscilloscope will be on by default – turn on and off using the O icon.
    3. Open the Oscilloscope screen.
    4. View the defect in the bottom of the setup container.
    5. Click the dot (defect) to center the measurement line over the dot of the setup sample.
      1. By default, measurement line is located at the center of the container.
    6. Adjust the focus to maximize the peak at the location of the defect.
  5. To adjust the brightness setting, perform the following steps:
    1. Adjust the brightness settings using the slider so that the bright parts of the bottom of the container (the area inside the knurling) show on the oscilloscope reading at 50-75%.
    2. Use Sensor offset to align image to center of screen if camera is not perfectly aligned
    3. You may need to adjust the zones of the light sources to create a homogeneous/consistent level of light intensity.
    4. Press on Expert Mode to adjust light levels if necessary
    5. There are 4 LED zones – Zones can be turned off or adjusted in intensity from 0 to 100%.
    6. Work with zones and brightness adjustment on the camera to achieve the most consistent brightness level possible across all zones.
    7. The light source can also be adjusted if there is poor contrast. Typically it should be about 20 mm from the container.
    8. Click next.
    9. Click on the save button.
  6. To set the distance setting, perform the following steps:
    1. Close the doors to the machine.
    2. Change the machine to Automatic Mode using the switch on the front of the machine.
    3. Turn on the conveyor
    4. Pass a container through the machine
    5. Press the Distance button.
    6. Use the slider to adjust where the image will be seen – crosshairs should be in the center of the container.
      1. The station distances have been pre-set in the Configuration/Distance menu.
    7. Use the slider on the distance screen to correctly center the image.
    8. Pass the container again to confirm it is centered.
    9. Repeat process until container is centered.
    10. Exit distance mode by clicking Next. Click save if required.
  7. To set zones and defect rejection parameters, perform the following steps:
    1. Open the Zone and Defect menu.
    2. Set Channels according the MSDR INS-07-032 Minimum Inspection Channels on Tiama Equipment.
    3. Zone 0 will show already in place
    4. Click drawing icon to adjust Zone 0 already in place.
      1. To adjust the size of the zone, click and drag the green squares.
      2. To move the zone, click the yellow square in the center of the zone.
      3. Zone 0 should be set just outside of the knurling
      4. Zone 0 surrounds all the other zones subsequently created.
    5. Click + to create the second zone inside zone 0.
      1. Choose shape
      2. Click centering icon to automatically center zone to the same as zone 0.
      3. Adjust the size using blue squares to just inside the knurling.
    6. Add additional zones as needed – 6 zones are available. Repeat process to create as many zones as are needed for this container
      1. Guidelines - Create a zone for the knurling area, Create a separate zone for a push-up area, create a zone around a mould number, etc.
      2. Avoid placing the zone too close to a shadow.
    7. Exit drawing mode by clicking Next. Click save if required.
  8. To set reject thresholds for each zone and defect, perform the following steps:
    1. While the zone is selected, press Dynamic Zone.
      1. Once a dynamic zone is set, select the defect to assign.
    2. Set parameters according to MSDR INS-07-033 Inspection Device Setup Parameters for Tiama Equipment.
    3. Run challenge samples and several commercial containers through the machine and analyze results for each defect.
      1. Defect samples must be available for each defect parameter that will be set.
    4. Select the zone and defect screen.
    5. Choose the defect type on the left side of the screen
    6. Select the object with the mouse.
      1. The object will display green if not considered a defect.
      2. The object will display red if considered a defect.
    7. Adjust the reject parameters for each defect type by moving the sliders so that the challenge sample will be rejected.
      1. Compact and drawn compact.
      2. Surface – adjust the size of the defect to be rejected in pixels.
      3. Contrast – adjust how well the defect is seen based on how dark the image is seen vs the rest of the base.
      4. Set the reject threshold slightly above the largest defect (from a challenge sample) to be rejected.
    8. Set Reject settings for all zones by repeating the above process.
  9. If the container has a specific element, such as an orientation notch, perform the following steps: (Skip if no orientation notch.)
    1. Press the zone and defect screen.
    2. Select the zone using the dropdown.
      1. If they overlap over two or more zones, the learning process must be performed in zone 0 (in which case all the elements in the image are taken into account).
    3. Select the number of containers.
    4. Press the learn button to start the learning process.
      1. Detected elements are marked in green.
    5. Run the container with notches through as many times as specified.
      1. Elements detected will show in Blue.
      2. A popup will appear after each container. Press Yes if the detection is correct.
    6. After the last container is run, a popup will appear. Press Yes if the detection is correct.
  10. To inspect the Knurling area, perform the following steps:
    1. Click Adjust – parameters
      1. Turn on “stipling” for zone 0
    2. Click adjust zones and defects
    3. Stiplings will show up in pink
    4. A defect in the stipling area will show up in red.
      1. Click Learning to identify the stiples
      2. Width should typically be left at the default value – between 7 and 10.
      3. The sensitivity of drawn objects will typically be left at the default value.
      4. Adjust the threshold by looking at the graph on the bottom and top of the image
        1. Stiplings should be almost no peaks, and the defect should show up as a larger peak in the graph view
        2. Threshold line will show up as a horizontal line on the graph, peaks above this line will be rejected.
    5. Density is a setting for total dark pixels seen in the zone
      1. This can be used to reject dirty or contaminated bottoms
      2. Set to the appropriate level based on a number of pixels for each zone.
    6. Pass many production containers; analyze rejects and confirm proper rejection and passing of commercial ware.
      1. Make adjustments to thresholds based on actual production ware.
  11. Upon completion of tasks, perform the following steps:
    1. Replace protective guarding and close safety interlock gates.
    2. Confirm the designated work area is clear and communicate to personnel the intent to restart energy.
    3. After the designated work area is confirmed clear and personnel is aware of the pending restart, follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures to restart energy supplied to the designated area. Verify energy is active.
    4. Remove signs and markings from the designated work area.
    5. Perform basic housekeeping duties. Clean up the work space, tools and equipment. Dispose of trash. Put tools and equipment in assigned area.
    6. Notify appropriate personnel that the work instruction activity has been completed.
    7. Record and report findings and results.
    8. Follow appropriate instructions for notification of findings and results relating to specifications, targets, and/or reaction limits.

Required Training

Before performing this work instruction, applicable personnel must be trained by an authorized trainer on the required training contents listed in this document.
