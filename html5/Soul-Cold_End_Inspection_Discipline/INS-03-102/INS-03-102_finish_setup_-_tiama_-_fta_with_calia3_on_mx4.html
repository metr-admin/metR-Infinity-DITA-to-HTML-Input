Finish Setup - Tiama - FTA with CALIA3 on MX4

INS-03-102

Revised: 2021-10-12

Purpose and Application

Purpose

To define a work instruction for proper setup of the Finish Top Analysis inspection device on a fully integrated MX4 Machine.

Application

This work instruction applies to all Soul manufacturing facilities.

Safety

Observe Soul standard safety requirements and procedures defined by the Global Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) organization, as well as all applicable local, regional, and national requirements. Safety equipment and instructions specific to the completion of this work instruction are detailed in the Instructions Section.

Instructions

  1. Before starting the work instruction activity, perform the following steps:
    1. Do not begin these tasks without confirmation that personnel directly involved have completed the required training associated with the work instruction activity and have reviewed this document.
    2. Gather necessary tools and equipment as identified below in the Equipment Section.
    3. Wear and use additional Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safety equipment required for this specific work instruction.
    4. Obey all applicable safety requirements and procedures.
    5. Notify appropriate personnel of the activity and estimated time needed to complete the tasks.
    6. Clearly identify, mark-off, and secure the designated area defined by the work instruction activity.
    7. Follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures when work instruction activity requires isolating energy in the designated area. Verify energy is isolated before starting work.
    8. After verifying that energy is isolated, remove the protective guarding and open the safety interlock gates.
  2. Prerequisites
    1. Obtain a commercial quality container with a known height value measured and approved by the quality team.
    2. Monitor the production flow to ensure that the machine is properly set up in accordance with Work Instruction INS-03-100 Container Handling Setup - Tiama - MX4.
  3. To set up the motor-driven movable frame, perform the following steps:
    1. Confirm the motor-driven movable frame height adjustment in accordance with Work Instruction INS-03-100 Container Handling Setup - Tiama - MX4.
    2. Jog a commercial quality container of the type to be inspected into station 5 or other station where FTA is installed on the machine.
      1. The MX4 should be in Manual mode .
      2. Move the container using the selector.
    3. Verify that the gauge light is on.
      1. The gauge signal is shown on the status bar. When the container can be inspected, the gauge light changes from gray to green.
  4. For the mechanical setup of the FTA Head Assembly, perform the following steps:
    1. Select Detections > FTA from the Main Menu.
    2. Select Settings > Optics from the Main Menu.
    3. Display the cross hairs on the finish surface image.
      1. The MX4 should be in PAUSE mode .
      2. Activate the Permanent button .
      3. Press the Cross Hairs button to aid in setup .
    4. Select the finish surface view and enable the laser.
      1. Activate the laser by clicking on the button in the FTA Laser Source Settings menu.
      2. The right image must show the finish surface with the cross hairs and laser.
    5. If the container is not appearing on the screen, use the up/down and in/out adjustments until an image appears.
      1. Locking Handle (in/out) [1].
      2. Adjustment Knob (in/out) [2].
      3. Locking Screw (up/down) [3].
      4. Adjustment Knob (up/down) [4].
    6. While monitoring the Scope Screen, slightly loosen the Locking Screws and adjust the position of the Head Assembly left/right using the Adjustment Knob until the sealing surface image is horizontal as in the image shown. When finished, tighten the Locking Screws.
      1. Locking Screws (left/right) [1].
      2. Adjustment Knob (left/right) [2].
    7. While monitoring the Scope Screen, loosen the Locking Handle and adjust the position of the Head Assembly in/out using the Adjustment Knob until the sealing surface image is vertically centered as in the image shown. When finished, adjust the Locking Handle.
      1. Locking Handle (in/out) [1].
      2. Adjustment Knob (in/out) [2].
    8. While monitoring the Scope Screen, slightly loosen the Locking Screw and adjust the position of the Head Assembly up/down using the Adjustment Knob until the vertical Wire Edge laser line is centered as in the image shown. When finished, tighten the Locking Screw.
      1. Locking Screw (up/down) [1].
      2. Adjustment Knob (up/down) [2].
    9. When the laser is centered on the crosshairs, confirm that the sealing surface of the container is in focus. If it is not, the FTA Head requires realignment or repair.
      Note:

      The previous adjustment steps may need to be repeated until the image is centered on the crosshairs.

    10. Tilt adjustments (if installed).
      1. Tilt adjustment knob [1].
      2. Tilt adjustment blocking screw [2].
      3. Tilt adjustment default position pin [3].
  5. To adjust the FTA light and laser settings, perform the following steps:
    1. Adjust the Sealing Surface Intensity slider until the Avg. Intensity reads between 80 and 90. (Ideal intensity is 100).
      1. The MX4 should be in Manual mode and the article should not rotate.
      2. If the Sealing Surface Intensity value must be adjusted to near 100 to obtain the desired measured intensity, this may indicate that the FTA Head needs servicing.
    2. Adjust the Sealing Surface Balance slider to obtain an even contrast throughout the image from top to bottom.
    3. Adjust the Laser Intensity.
      1. Activate the laser by clicking on the button in the FTA Laser Source Settings menu.
      2. Move the Laser Intensity slider until the Avg. Spot Width slider turns to green.
      3. The ideal Avg. Spot Width is 4.0.
  6. For the software setup of the FTA, perform the following steps:
    1. Spin the container:
      1. The machine should be in Manual mode [1].
      2. Press the Green button [2].
    2. Select Settings > FTA Defect Adjustment.
      1. The MX4 should be in Pause mode .
    3. Click on the button to show the finish edges on the trapped image.
      1. Select the Sealing Surface image.
      2. Select one of the 64 frames.
      3. Verify the green top and blue bottom arcs match with the finish edges.
    4. Learn the Top and Bottom finish limits.
      1. Click on the button.
      2. Drag the Top and Bottom blue lines to the Top and Bottom limits of the Finish surface as shown.
      3. Press the Learn button.
    5. Verify the Article Type.
      1. Click on the button.
      2. Choose the proper Article Type as needed.
        1. Normal: Used for most containers. It should always be used for initial setup regardless of the actual bottle type.
        2. Rough: Selected when finish edges are rough (due to a dirty finish) or blurred due to the curvature of a crown finish.
        3. No Step: Basically used for Blow & Blow process if Normal finish is not working (false rejects for Overpress or Wire Edge).
      Note:

      Changing the Article type will restore the parameters values back to their defaults.

    6. Select the Image Contrast and Luminosity.
      1. Click on the button.
      2. Move the Contrast and Luminosity sliders as required to better see the surface image.
      3. This affects the displayed image only. It does not change the optical or light setup done previously.
    7. Show the Over height/Under height values.
      1. Click on the button.
      2. Click on the Sealing Surface button.
      3. The bottom image will show a green dot graphic with the values measured from the frames. The red lines are the threshold values defined.
    8. Show the Wire Edge height values.
      1. Click on the Wire Edge Height button.
      2. The bottom image will show a green dot graphic with the values measured from the frames. The red line is the threshold value defined.
      3. Negative values mean Overpress.
    9. Set up the revolutions per gauge.
      1. Select Settings/Advanced Parameters.
      2. Confirm the Rev Per Gauge value is set to 1.50.
  7. To verify there is no stray light coming from nearby stations (NCI, NCL or others), perform the following steps:
    1. Jog 3 containers into the machine:
      1. One (1) in the station after FTA.
      2. One (1) in the FTA position.
      3. One (1) in the station before FTA.
    2. Spin the containers.
      1. The MX4 should be on MANU mode.
      2. Press the ON button.
    3. Trap the next 10 images.
    4. Check for stray light on the Sealing Surface, Wire Edge and Crizzle images.

      Possible solutions:

      1. Mask the NCL light source.
      2. Change the position of the NCI lamp.
    5. If there is no possibility to reduce stray light on the left side of the image by changing the position of the NCI lamp or masking the NCL lamp, set the WE Offset Left Column to 0.
      1. Enter to Settings/Advanced Parameters.
      2. Select the Left Column Offset parameter.
      3. Modify the value to 0.
  8. To review the Pockets screen of the FTA, perform the following steps:
    1. Select Settings > Pockets from the Main Menu.
    2. Clear out all the totals by pressing the Reset Values button .
    3. Start the machine (ensure production ware is available at the infeed).
      1. The numbers shown are the median values obtained during the 15 minute inspection interval.
      2. As the containers travel through the pockets, each number will turn black or red when a sufficient number of containers have passed.
      3. The numbers will be displayed in black when acceptable (>80% of set threshold), or red when below the 80% of the threshold. The FTA Head is properly adjusted when all these numbers are black.
    4. Setting thresholds.
      1. Click on the Settings button .
      2. Select Alarm configuration .
      3. Click on the Threshold option .
      4. Set the threshold values.
  9. To confirm the FTA inspection and the appropriate inspection channels are enabled.
    1. Enable the FTA channels.
      1. Select Settings > Advanced adjustments.
      2. Confirm the appropriate inspection channels are enabled (in orange) according to the FTA section of MSDR INS-03-030 Minimum Inspection Channels on Soul Equipment.
    2. Verify the Reject Parameter settings are within the ranges specified in the FTA section of MSDR INS-07-031 Inspection Device Setup Parameters for Soul Equipment.
      1. Go to Settings > FTA Defect Adjustment.
      2. Select each Reject Parameter to modify the desired settings/thresholds.
    3. Verify that the FTA inspection is enabled.
      1. The MX4 should be in Manual Mode.
      2. Select Configuration > Detector Presence to display the Presence Detections display.
      3. Click on the station 5 (or the station where the FTA is installed) button.
      4. Click on the FTA inspection to display the FTA verdict activation screen.
    4. Verify the FTA inspection IS NOT inhibited.
      1. Select Utilization > Inhibition.
      2. Confirm the FTA inspection IS NOT in orange. (Orange means the inspection is inhibited.)
    5. Verify the defect mapping function in the MX4 software.
      1. Select Utilization > Rejects Table to display the Rejects table screen.
      2. Click on the FTA Box.
  10. Upon completion of tasks, perform the following steps:
    1. Replace protective guarding and close safety interlock gates.
    2. Confirm the designated work area is clear and communicate to personnel the intent to restart energy.
    3. After the designated work area is confirmed clear and personnel are aware of the pending restart, follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures to restart energy supplied to the designated area. Verify energy is active.
    4. Remove signs and markings from the designated work area.
    5. Perform basic housekeeping duties. Clean up the work space, tools and equipment, dispose of trash, put tools and equipment in assigned area.
    6. Notify appropriate personnel that the work instruction activity has been completed.

Required Training

Before performing this work instruction, applicable personnel must be trained by an authorized trainer on the required training contents listed in this document.
