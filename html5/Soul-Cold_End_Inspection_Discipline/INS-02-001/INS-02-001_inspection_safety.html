Inspection Safety

INS-02-001

Revised: 2019-09-25

Purpose

To define a standard set of minimum safety requirements and procedures that apply to all inspection personnel, defined as anyone working in the inspection areas.

Procedure

  1. Plant leadership shall be accountable and responsible for providing the safety devices, equipment and prerequisite programs described in this document.
  2. PPE: All personnel working on inspection equipment shall wear Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), as specified in MSDR INS-07-001 Inspection Safety Equipment.
    1. Cut resistant gloves shall be carried at all times and worn when handling glass.
  3. Local safety requirements through contractual agreements or local legislation shall be followed. Plant shall address how contractors and visitors will be informed of, and comply with, the plant’s safety requirements.
  4. All personnel working on inspection equipment shall have a safety training plan and fully documented training records in accordance with Soul regional, national, and local regulations – as well as others defined by Global Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S). This includes, but is not limited to, the following:
    1. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).
    2. Lockout Tagout, including adherence to the following machine specific profiles:
      1. INS-02-001-F001Lockout Tagout Tryout Machine Profile - FP
      2. INS-02-001-F002Lockout Tagout Tryout Machine Profile - FPX
      3. INS-02-001-F003Lockout Tagout Tryout Machine Profile - FPX2
      4. INS-02-001-F004Lockout Tagout Tryout Machine Profile - ICK-CE
      5. INS-02-001-F005Lockout Tagout Tryout Machine Profile - ICK-non-CE
    3. Job-specific training.
    4. Mobile equipment as required by specific job duties, such as forklifts or scissor lifts.
  5. Safety warnings on machines shall be in the local language or a universally accepted safety symbol.
    1. English warnings should also be displayed on machines.
  6. A glass breaking device shall be available at each production line.
    1. All containers within the machine and the immediate area must be rejected when a container needs to be broken to protect from contamination.
    2. A carbide tipped glass breaking device is a recommended option.
  7. Safety upgrades identified in MSDR INS-07-001 Inspection Safety Equipment shall be performed.
  8. Cables shall be properly installed and managed to prevent trips and electrical hazards.
  9. Safety programs shall be implemented and practiced for all Inspection Personnel to include, but not be limited to, the following:
    1. A qualified first aid responder and a first aid kit must be located on site and available at all times.
    2. The area and floor around the inspection machine is kept clean, orderly, and free of glass and oil. The floor should not be slippery.
    3. No loose clothing, long or dangling jewelry, or hair extending beyond the shoulder. Long hair shall be tucked under the helmet/cap, or covered in a bandana.
      1. Hairnets may be worn in the inspection area according to plant requirements.
    4. Lighting in the inspection production area must be in working order, covered, protected, and clean.
    5. Authorized personnel performing an elevated task must wear the proper fall protection equipment as required by local regulations.
    6. Cold end housekeeping program is implemented.
    7. Follow all local regulations for lifting tasks.
  10. Injuries, incidents, and near misses shall be reported in a timely manner according to the established plant procedure.
  11. Personnel interacting with operational inspection equipment shall follow local procedures for conducting sampling safely according to Procedure INS-02-040 Inspection Equipment Validation.
  12. All machine enclosures, guarding and safety devices shall follow local regulations and be properly installed and functional for personal protection.
    1. Machine doors and electrical cabinets must remain closed and require a key or tool to prevent unauthorized access.
    2. Guard all drives and infeed / outfeed conveyors to manufacturer’s design or better.
    3. Machine guards must require a tool for removal or interlock to prevent unauthorized access.
    4. Machine safety interlocks must be utilized unless following an approved safety interlock bypass defined within a work instruction.
    5. Inspection Personnel must not remove, inhibit, or otherwise bypass any safety devices or guarding unless equipment is properly locked out and tagged out according to local regulations and Soul requirements.
    6. E-Stops for each machine must meet local requirements.
