Device Setup - DimPix - MCAL 4

INS-03-063

Revised: 2017-07-21

Purpose and Application

Purpose

To define a work instruction for proper setup of the DimPix inspection device on the MCAL 4.

Application

This work instruction applies to all O-I manufacturing facilities.

Safety

Observe O-I standard safety requirements and procedures defined by the Global Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) organization, as well as all applicable local, regional, and national requirements. Safety equipment and instructions specific to the completion of this work instruction are detailed in the Instructions Section.

Instructions

  1. Before starting the work instruction activity, perform the following steps:
    1. Do not begin these tasks without confirmation that personnel directly involved have completed the required training associated with the work instruction activity and have reviewed this document.
    2. Gather necessary tools and equipment as identified below in the Equipment Section.
    3. Wear and use additional Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safety equipment required for this specific work instruction.
    4. Obey all applicable safety requirements and procedures.
  2. Prerequisite
    1. Prior to this work instruction, ensure container handling was properly set up according to INS-03-012 Container Handling – Tiama – MCAL 4.
    2. Prepare a setup sample with best possible diameter and height of the new job.
  3. For proper setup, perform the following steps:
    1. The HMI screen of the MCAL 4 is located on the front right door of the machine.
    2. Stop the flow of containers into the machine using whatever container spacing device is installed prior to the machine.
  4. For proper setup, perform the following steps:
    1. Open DimPix from the Detections menu.
    2. Open the measurements window from the adjustments menu.
    3. Select the drawing icon.
    4. On the image, move the black reference line down to the top of the container.
      1. Review the six body camera images.
    5. Drag the finish "band" line just below the finish.
    6. Drag the shoulder band line to the upper contact area.
    7. Drag the bottom band line to the lower contact area.
    8. Select additional location bands and drag the corresponding line to the appropriate area as needed.
      1. Typically for narrow neck, a line is located at each contact point and an additional measurement in the neck area.
      2. Typically for wide mouth, an additional line is located at the finish.
    9. Press the copy icon to copy the diameters to all cameras.
    10. Select each camera image and verify the diameter information has copied over. Adjust as necessary.
    11. Select the type to specify how the measurement will be taken within the band.
    12. For Flint type, adjust thickness to 10-15.
    13. Enter Diameter for each band point from the container drawing in the Expected column.
  5. To adjust the height, perform the following steps:
    1. Enter settings for sensitivity, thickness, and expected similar to diameter setup.
      1. Enter the exact height measurement from the container drawing.
      2. The Type does not include Flint as an option.
    2. Select which area of the container to be measured.
    3. Any line that has been moved will turn purple. If any line is purple, you must click Learn to confirm the new location.
    4. Review all camera views and repeat previous step for any purple lines.
    5. Turn off drawing mode by pressing the drawing icon.
    6. Review measurements in Tolerance and Measure column.
  6. To adjust the verticality, perform the following steps:
    1. Select the drawing tool.
    2. Select the container verticality.
    3. The container verticality: the difference between the perfect vertical axis, and the measured axis between the center of "bottom" gauge diameter and the center of "finish" gauge diameter.
    4. Select the Body verticality.
    5. The body verticality: the difference between the perfect vertical axis, and the measured axis between the center of "bottom" gauge diameter and the center of "shoulder" gauge diameter.
    6. Select the Neck verticality.
    7. The neck verticality: the difference between the neck axe (should-finish) and the body axe (bottom-Shoulder).
    8. Turn off drawing mode by pressing the drawing icon.
      1. If the Verticality is within tolerance the number in the Measure column will be green.
      2. If the Verticality is out of tolerance the number in the Measure column will be red.
  7. To adjust the threshold parameters, perform the following steps:
    1. Choose the specific zone parameters according to MSDR INS-07-033 Inspection Device Setup Parameters for Tiama Equipment.
    2. Verify parameters and gauges have been copied to all cameras and adjust gauges if necessary.
    3. Press the green check to return to the main menu.
    4. Put the machine into operation mode.
    5. Pass a challenge sample through the machine and verify results.
    6. Adjust parameters as necessary and repeat the previous step until proper rejection.
      1. If the Measurement is within tolerance for Height or Diameter, the corresponding lines will show up green on the image and the number will show up green in the Measure Column.
    7. If the Measurement is outside of the tolerance for Height or Diameter, the corresponding lines will show up red on the image and the number will show up red in the Measure Column.
    8. Repeat steps for each gauge.
    9. Ensure all challenge samples are rejected.
    10. Pass 500 containers through the machine to learn the average.
      1. If differences are seen with actual production, the machine automatically will adapt to these settings.
    11. A container image will appear in the top left of the screen and will disappear at the end of the calibration process.
    12. Pass a challenge sample through the machine.
    13. Verify challenge sample is rejected and good container is not rejected.
  8. Upon completion of tasks, perform the following steps:
    1. Perform basic housekeeping duties. Clean up the work space, tools and equipment, dispose of trash, put tools and equipment in assigned area.
    2. Notify appropriate personnel that the work instruction activity has been completed.
    3. Record and report findings and results.
    4. Follow appropriate instructions for notification of findings and results relating to specifications, targets and/or reaction limits.

Required Training

Before performing this work instruction, applicable personnel must be trained by an authorized trainer on the required training contents listed in this document.
