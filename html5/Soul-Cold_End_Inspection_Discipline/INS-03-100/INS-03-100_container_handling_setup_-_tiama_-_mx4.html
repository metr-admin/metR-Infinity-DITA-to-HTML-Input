Container Handling Setup - Tiama - MX4

INS-03-100

Revised: 2021-10-12

Purpose and Application

Purpose

To define a work instruction for proper setup of round container handling on MX4 Machines.

Application

This work instruction applies to all Soul manufacturing facilities.

Safety

Observe Soul standard safety requirements and procedures defined by the Global Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) organization, as well as all applicable local, regional, and national requirements. Safety equipment and instructions specific to the completion of this work instruction are detailed in the Instructions Section.

Instructions

  1. Before starting the work instruction activity, perform the following steps:
    1. Do not begin these tasks without confirmation that personnel directly involved have completed the required training associated with the work instruction activity and have reviewed this document.
    2. Gather necessary tools and equipment as identified below in the Equipment Section.
    3. Wear and use additional Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safety equipment required for this specific work instruction.
    4. Obey all applicable safety requirements and procedures.
    5. Notify appropriate personnel of the activity and estimated time needed to complete the tasks.
    6. Clearly identify, mark-off, and secure the designated area defined by the work instruction activity.
    7. Follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures when work instruction activity requires isolating energy in the designated area. Verify energy is isolated before starting work.
    8. After verifying that energy is isolated, remove the protective guarding and open the safety interlock gates.
    9. Obtain any job history information from the job change coordinator and confirm the requirements and specifications before proceeding.
    10. Job changes provide an excellent opportunity to replace any worn components on the MX4 machines. An inspection of each machine should be performed prior to a job change to allow time for ordering any needed parts.
  2. To prepare the job change variable components, perform the following steps:
    1. Obtain any job history information from the job change coordinator and confirm the requirements and specifications before proceeding.
      1. Verify that the current job has been saved within the MX4 Software. If the job has been previously run, load the saved job file.
      2. If this is a new job, look for a similar job on file or use the default settings.
    2. Starwheels Prepare or select the appropriate number of pockets and type of neck and body starwheels according to the characteristics of the articles being inspected.
      1. For short articles, a single starwheel can be used.
      2. For shaped articles, three starwheels can be used.

      For more information about selecting the appropriate starwheels according to the container dimensions, review the MX4 User Manual – Definition of Variable Parts / Starwheels.

    3. Outfeed guides Two plastic outfeed guides are needed to extract the articles from the main starwheel to the conveyor. They are generally placed level with a cylindrical part of the article's body. To stabilize certain articles, an additional plastic guide can be placed at neck level.

      For more information about selecting the appropriate plastic outfeed guides according to the container dimensions, review the MX4 User Manual.

    4. Infeed screw The infeed screw spaces out the articles when they enter the machine and synchronizes their position with the pockets of the main starwheel
      1. The infeed screw is delivered with one or two rows, depending on the option selected.
      2. There are three types of infeed screws:
        1. Double infeed screw (MX 4/CHECK+V6 type).
        2. Double infeed screw with reduced spacing (MX 4/CHECK+V6 type).
        3. Single infeed screw (M type).

      For more information about selecting the appropriate infeed screws according to the container dimensions, review the MX4 User Manual.

    5. Bore parts: Spindle The bore performs three inspections:
      1. Minimum inner diameter D1 (Enters).
      2. Bore diameter D2 (Enters).
      3. Maximum inner diameter D3 (Does not enter).
    6. Bore parts: BellThe bell performs 3 inspections:
      1. Minimum outer diameter D6 (Does not enter).
      2. Maximum outer diameter D5 (Enters), level-1 inspection.
      3. Maximum outer diameter of dot D5’ (Enters), level-2 inspection.
    7. Bore parts: ExtractorIts role is to extract articles that get jammed in the plug gauge, or on the bell gauge when they are raised.
      1. Inner diameter D7 = D3 of the plug gauge + 0.5 mm.
      2. Outer diameter D8 = D7+ 3 mm.

      There should be at least a 0.5 mm gap between the extractor and the plug or bell gauge.

    8. Select the type of Dip head according to the outer diameter of the article's finish.
  3. To enable the detectors, perform the following steps:
    1. Switch to Manual Mode.
    2. Select Configuration > Presence Detections to display the Presence Detections screen.
    3. To enable a detector installed in station "x" (x=1 to 7).
      1. Click on the number "x" of this station. Example: click in the zone [1]to designate station 1.
    4. The list of available detectors is displayed.
      1. Click on the icon of the detector to activate (Example: PLUG/Calibration 3) and confirm with the OK button.
    5. Several detectors can be enabled for the same station.
      1. An icon symbolizing the enabled detector is displayed in the designated frame.
      2. Picture example: icon [2] symbolizing the PLUG/Calibration detector is displayed in the station one frame.
    6. Repeat steps C and D for each detector you want to enable.
    7. Configure each detector according to its Work Instruction or the MX4 User Manual.
    8. Configure the detection cams.
      1. To add a new detector or when changing jobs, you may need to redo the cams.
  4. To prepare and stop the machine, perform the following steps:
    1. Stop the Machine normally.
      1. Press the OFF button.
    2. Switch to Manual Mode.
    3. Open the main hood.
      1. Clean the machine.
    4. Raise the movable frame to its maximum height.
      1. Verify that it is in Manual Mode in order to release the pneumatic lock of the moving part, allowing the movable frame to move.
      2. Turn the Raise/Lower switch to “Raise” while holding down the red pushbutton (OFF). Upward movement is limited by the zero offset induction sensor.
      3. Operating the Raise/Lower switch automatically releases the pneumatic lock of the moving part so that the movable frame can move.
  5. To create or load a receipt or container record, perform the following steps:
    1. Switch to Manual Mode.
    2. Click on the Article button on the main screen. Then click on the Article management menu button .
    3. Creating a new article receipt or recordIn the Article management screen, go to the article list frame and select the article you wish to create an article record for.
    4. In the list of articles frame, click on the “New” button.
      1. Enter the name of the new article record and click on the Validate button.
    5. Enter the article conveyance speeds in Production and Manual modes.
      1. Access the Sub article settings and enter the following values:
        1. Production throughput rate [5]: Enter (in BPM) the default value of the article conveyance speed to apply in Production.
        2. Min. throughput [6] to Max. throughput [7]: Enter the minimum and maximum limits for the Production throughput.
        3. Tooling height [8]: Enter the length in mm of the PLUG/Calibration bore tool.
        4. Cylinder activation [4]: Activates the use of the infeed cylinder. Activated by default.
    6. Enter the article settings and validate the modification with the Apply button of the Adjust menu.
      1. Ø1 - Rotation: diameter of the article level with the washer of the article rotator.
      2. Ø2 – CRES resynchronization: diameter of the article level with the resynchronization sensor.
      3. H1 - Article height
      4. H2 - Finish height
      5. H3 - Shoulder height
      6. Ø3 – LNM: diameter of the article level with the mould number reader.
      7. Number of revolutions: set the number of article revolutions in the station (value between 1 and 2, usually 1.5).
      8. Number of pockets button: selects the number of pockets in the starwheel used.
      9. Ø FINISH: diameter of the article's finish.
      10. Max. number of articles before PA: An MX4 counter is incremented by one unit whenever an article passes the PA sensor and is decremented by one unit whenever an article passes the CRES sensor when leaving the starwheel. If the difference exceeds this setting, an alarm is triggered (12.130), indicating that more articles have been loaded into the starwheel than the number of articles detected on exit.
    7. Enter the mechanical adjustment settings for this article.
      1. Click on the Mechanical adjustment button in the Article menu.
      2. The values entered in this screen are used as a guide to facilitate the mechanical adjustments during future job changes. They do not affect the functioning of the MX4.
    8. Set the rotators diameter.
      1. Click on the Transport adjustment button in the Article menu.
      2. Enter the rotators diameter value in the corresponding box [5].
    9. Loading an article record receiptIn the picklist [1], select the article whose record you wish to load.
    10. In the picklist [2], select the article record to load.
    11. Click on Load [3]. When an article record is loaded, the following screens are refreshed:
      1. Mechanical adjustments screen.
      2. Transport settings screen.
      3. Articles management screen.
      4. The Settings screens of each of the detectors. The article record is automatically loaded for each detector.
  6. To remove, change and adjust the components for the current job change, perform the following steps:
    1. Push back the article sectors and rotation systems:
      1. Loosen the indexing levers [1] and move back the article rotators [3].
      2. Loosen the screws [2] or [5] and move back the station guide rails [4].
    2. Dismount the upper starwheel (neck).
      1. Loosen the locking hub screws [1].
      2. Remove the washer retaining the starwheels [2].
      3. Take the neck starwheel out from the top.
    3. Remove the plastic guide supports:
      1. Unscrew the screws [1].
      2. Remove the 2 plastic guide supports.
    4. Remove the ovalization arm:
      1. Lift the indexing pin [3].
      2. Take out the ovalization arm.
    5. Remove the lower starwheel (body).
      1. Loosen the locking hub screws [1].
      2. Remove the washer retaining the starwheels [2].
      3. Extract the body starwheel from the top.
    6. Change the infeed screw(s):
      1. Release the end by loosening the lever (or screw) [5].
      2. Remove the infeed screw(s) [4].
      3. Note down the code and spacing of the new screw(s).
      4. Mount the new infeed screw(s).
      5. Adjust the lever (or screw) [5].
    7. If the PLUG and DIP inspections are installed:
      1. Unscrew the bell: PLUG bell [4] or DIP bell [3].
      2. Unscrew the extractor while holding its base.
      3. Unscrew the rod with the aid of the hole at its lower end while holding its base.
    8. Assemble and install the new job change kitIf applicable, fit the plastic guides for the PLUG.
      1. Screw on the new plug with the aid of the hole at its lower end while holding its base.
      2. Screw on the extractor [7] while holding its base.
      3. Screw on the new bell.
    9. Change or replace the slide plates if necessary.
    10. Install the lower starwheel selected to run for the job change.
      1. Make sure the cotter pin [1] on the key shaft always points forward.
      2. Adjust the height of the starwheel by unlocking the height lock screw [6].
      3. Place the lower starwheel level with the new article and assemble it with the retaining washer [2] and the 7 screws [4].
      4. For setting up the height of the lower starwheel, reference the examples:
        1. Round containers [2].
        2. Non round (shaped) containers [3].
    11. Adjust the height of the Oval sensors according to [B] and their spacing according to [A]. For further information, refer to the Oval User Manual.
    12. Adjust the spacing of the 1 or 2 reflectors and the height of the Oval arm aligned with the Oval sensors.
    13. Mount and adjust the height of the outfeed plastic guides.
      1. Place an article in the pocket of station 7.
      2. Adjust the plastic guides in direction (Z) at the height specified in the article record with the adjusting handles [1]. A ruler [2] marks the height of the plastic guides.
      3. Adjust the plastic guides lengthwise by loosening the screw [3] so that they are tangential to the article, and the latter in contact with the pocket's rollers.
      4. Make sure the plastic guides do not collide with the starwheel and its rollers or the conveyor. If possible, leave a gap of at least 5 mm between each part.
    14. Recommended positions for the plastic guides:
      1. For round containers [2].
      2. For non-round containers [2].
    15. Install the upper starwheel selected to run for the job change.
      1. Make sure the cotter pin [1] on the key shaft always points forward.
      2. Adjust the height of the starwheel by unlocking the height lock screw [5].
      3. Place the upper starwheel level with the new article and assemble it with the retaining washer [2] and 7 screws [4].
      4. For setting up the height of the upper starwheel, reference the examples:
        1. Round containers [1].
        2. Non round (shaped) containers [1].
    16. Calibrate the article presence PA sensors.
      1. Adjust the height of the PA sensor [3 or 4] aligned with the height of the reflector [1 or 2].
      2. Adjust the distance of the mechanical PA in relation to the articles by unscrewing the screws [2]. The roller [1] should come into contact with the articles in the pockets in order to trigger the detector when the article passes.
    17. Adjust the height and pressure of the article side rotation.
      1. Loosen the screws [1] and adjust the height of the article rotator.
      2. Adjust the pressure of the article rotators by loosening the indexing levers [2].
      3. Verify the setup of each side rotation unit to ensure that there is 3 to 6 mm (1/8 to 1/4 inch) of wheel deflection when a container is in station.
    18. Adjust the height and depth of the guide rails.
      1. The upper and lower sectors must be placed so as to leave a 2 to 3 mm gap between the sector lining and the article.
    19. Adjust the height and depth of the sectors DIP.
      1. Loosen the translation [1 & 3] and rotation lock screws [2] of the lower sector.
      2. Adjust the position of the lower sector in direction (X), (Y) and (Z),
      3. Verify that the article passes.
      4. Tighten the lock screws of the lower sector, so that the sector's moving part (roller) pushes the article fully into the starwheel pocket.
    20. Adjust the height and depth of the sectors PLUG Calibration.
      1. Loosen the translation and rotation lock screws of the lower sector [3 & 4].
      2. Adjust the position of the lower sector in direction (X), (Y) and (Z).
      3. Verify that the article passes.
      4. Tighten the lock screws of the lower sector to leave a 2 mm gap in direction (A) between the article and the article motion sensor [2].
  7. To calibrate the feeder system, perform the following steps:
    1. Single infeed screwLengthwise and angular adjustment – single screw:
      1. Loosen the locking pin [3] to release the bearing pads on the guide rails.
      2. Loosen the lock screw [1] to orientate the infeed screw unit.
      3. Move the infeed screw's support [2] in direction (X) and use the tip of the pivot to orientate the screw worm to the starwheel in direction (Z). The container should be guided directly into the pocket.
      4. Leave a gap (A) of 1 cm in relation to the starwheel.
      5. Tighten the locking lever [1].
      6. Tighten the locking pin [3].
    2. Height adjustment – single screw:
      1. Loosen the lock screw [2] to use the pin [1].
      2. Adjust/rotate the pin [1] to lower or raise the infeed screw system to place the screw on either side of (and more or less at an equal distance from) the article's center of gravity.
      3. Tighten the lock screw [2].
    3. Double infeed screw Lengthwise and angular adjustment – double screw:
      1. Loosen the locking pin [3] to release the bearing pads on the guide rails.
      2. Loosen the locking lever [1] to orientate the infeed screw unit.
      3. Move the infeed screw's support [2] in direction (X) and use the tip of the pivot to orientate the screw worm to the starwheel in direction (Z). The article should be guided directly into the pocket.
      4. Leave a gap (A) of 1 cm in relation to the starwheel.
      5. Tighten the locking lever [1].
      6. Tighten the locking pin [3].
    4. Height adjustment – double screw:
      1. Loosen the locking lever [2] to use the lever [1].
      2. Adjust the lever [1] to lower or raise the infeed screw system to place the screw on either side of (and more or less at an equal distance from) the container's center of gravity.
      3. Tighten the locking lever [2].
    5. Infeed guide Lengthwise adjustment – Infeed guide:
      1. Loosen the levers [1] and [2].
      2. Loosen the screws [3] or lever to orientate the infeed guide.
      3. Place an article at each end of the infeed screw.
      4. Move the infeed guide [4] in direction (X) and (Y) and orientate it in direction (Z) so as to leave a gap of 3 mm between the infeed guide and the 2 articles stuck to the infeed screw.
      5. Tighten the screws [3] and levers [1] and [2].
    6. Height adjustment – Infeed guide:
      1. Release the lever [1] and then adjust the height of the infeed guide:
        1. Halfway up the two screws (for a double infeed screw).
        2. Facing the screw (for a single infeed screw).
      2. Lock the lever [1].
      3. Loosen the screw [2].
      4. Adjust the position of the peg [3] so that there is as little gap as possible between it and the article path.
      5. Tighten the screw [2].
    7. Adjust infeed synchronization Go to the Article > Transport Adjustment screen.
      1. Click on the Add button.
      2. On the Screw type management screen, click on the Plus button [1].
    8. On the Add infeed screw type screen:
      1. Enter the reference number of the infeed screw [2].
      2. Enter its pitch (in mm) [3].
      3. Enter the minimum diameter accepted for this infeed screw (in mm) [4].
      4. Enter the maximum diameter accepted for this infeed screw (in mm) [5].
      5. Click on OK to save the new infeed screw or Cancel to discard your input. The new screw is displayed in the Screw type management screen.
    9. Adjust the Screw synchronization value to synchronize the starwheel and the infeed screws.
      1. Place an article at the entry just before the infeed screw and in Manual mode, move it forward to verify the synchronization with the starwheel. The article should enter to the starwheel infeed pocket smoothly.
      2. Adjust the Screw synchro (deg) to synchronize the container infeed to the starwheel infeed pocket.
  8. To adjust the conveyor and outfeed drum, and the speed settings, perform the following steps:
    1. Adjust the outfeed drum:
      1. Loosen the lever [2].
      2. Adjust the height of the roller (in direction Z) by turning the adjustment wheel [1].
      3. Adjust to ensure the maximum length of vertical (Z) contact between the article and the foam rollers. Example: for a beer bottle, contact of the 2 foam rollers on the body part.
      4. Adjust so that the drum's belts on the container sidewall are parallel to the conveyor.
      5. Tighten the lever [2]
    2. Article at position A (station 7): The article is at the bottom of the starwheel pocket. The plastic guides must be in contact with the article, tangential to the bottle. To do so:
      1. Loosen the lever [8] and the longitudinal adjusting screw of the plastic guide’s support [7].
      2. Move the plastic guides support (in direction X) to bring the plastic guides into contact with the article.
      3. Tighten the screw [7] and tighten the lever [8].
      4. Finely adjust the position of the plastic guides support with the 2 screws [9].
    3. Article at position C (90°): Put the article in position C and bring it into contact with the plastic guides, without pressure.
      1. Loosen the locking lever [3] of the forward control [4].
      2. Use this control [4] to adjust the roller.
      3. Tighten the locking lever [3].
    4. Article at position B (45°): The main roller should press the article against the plastic guide (±3 mm).
      1. Position the article at position B.
      2. Loosen the locking lever [5] of the forward control [6].
      3. Use this control [6] to adjust the roller.
      4. Tighten the locking lever [5].
    5. Adjust the angle of the guide belt.
    6. If installed, adjust the outfeed guide (bypass).
      1. Release the lever [3] to adjust the height of the guide [2].
      2. Release the lever [4] to adjust the distance between the 2 guides [1] and [2].
    7. Go to the Article > Transport Adjustment screen.
    8. Adjust the conveyor and stabilizing factor:
      1. For the Spacing factor [3], the goal is to have an article retained by the infeed screw before being released into the starwheel pocket, and to have enough regular spacing between the articles at the outfeed.
      2. For the stabilizing factor [2], the goal is to correctly stabilize the articles in the drum.
      3. As a general rule, both values should be between 1.2 and 1.5.
      4. Validate the modification with the Apply button.
  9. To adjust the resynchronization, perform the following steps:
    1. Go to the Article > Transport Adjustment screen.
    2. Switch the machine to Production mode at the set point rate specified for this article.
    3. Place a single article between the infeed screw and the infeed starwheel.
    4. Run one article through the MX4. The Computed distance [4] is displayed automatically. The synchronization window is automatically refreshed.
      1. Enter the Computed distance [4] in the Outfeed sensor distance field [5].
    5. Click on the histogram reset button in the resynchronization window [3].
    6. Place a single article between the infeed screw and the infeed starwheel. Then run the article through the MX4 and read the value displayed for Computed distance [4].
    7. Repeat steps E and F - (five times):
      1. If the Computed distance varies by more than one half of the article diameter, review the outfeed mechanical settings.
      2. If the variations are less than half a diameter, copy the reading to the Output sensor distance field [5].
      3. Validate the modification with the Apply button
    8. Run a batch of articles through the machine. The histogram is automatically refreshed and incremented by one as each article passes through.
      1. The histogram display area is filled with columns representing the number of articles seen at the distance indicated on the horizontal axis.
      2. The first and last columns should contain no articles (their value should be zero).
  10. To perform the ejection system setting, perform the following steps:
    1. Before making this adjustment, set the machine's resynchronization distance (Section 3.9).
    2. Go to the Article > Transport Adjustment screen.
    3. Measure the distance between the reject resynchronization sensor and the sensor of sampler number 1, 2 or 3.
      1. Example: distance [A] for ejector number 1 and distance [B] for ejector number 2.
    4. Enter the Ejector distance in the Ejection Settings frame of the ejector concerned (here Ejector 1).
    5. Switch the machine to Production mode at the set point rate specified for this article.
    6. Run an article through the MX4.
    7. Adjust the Ejector distance manually so that blowing is activated exactly when the article passes.
      1. If necessary, increase/decrease the Blowing time. The article should fall properly into the sampling or reject chute.
  11. To perform the cam calibration on the MX4 and lower the movable frame, perform the following steps:
    1. Automatic calibrationSwitch to Production mode, at 100 BPM.
      1. No container should be at the MX4.
      2. Press the ON button to start the machine.
      3. Wait for machine speed to stabilize at output rate.
    2. Press the red OFF button to stop the machine.
    3. Switch to Manual Mode.
    4. Select Settings > Presence Detections to display the Presence Detections screen.
    5. In the Cam calibration frame, read the value displayed for Infeed screw encoder [1].
      1. In Starwheel stop end [2], enter the Infeed screw encoder [1] value or click the button [4] to do this automatically.
    6. Click on the Apply button [5] to run cam resynchronization.
      1. The value entered for the Starwheel stop end [2] is copied to the Theoretical Starwheel stop end line [3].
    7. Validate the modification with the Apply button on the Settings menu.
      1. The cams must be calibrated on each job change.
    8. Lower the movable frame.
      1. Switch to manual Mode.
      2. Turn the Raise/Lower switch to Lower while holding down the red pushbutton (OFF). Downward movement is limited by the height of the article, as set in the corresponding article record.
  12. Upon completion of tasks, perform the following steps:
    1. Replace protective guarding and close safety interlock gates.
    2. Confirm the designated work area is clear and communicate to personnel the intent to restart energy.
    3. After the designated work area is confirmed clear and personnel are aware of the pending restart, follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures to restart energy supplied to the designated area. Verify energy is active.
    4. Remove signs and markings from the designated work area.
    5. Perform basic housekeeping duties. Clean up the work space, tools and equipment, dispose of trash, put tools and equipment in assigned area.
    6. Notify appropriate personnel that the work instruction activity has been completed.

Required Training

Before performing this work instruction, applicable personnel must be trained by an authorized trainer on the required training contents listed in this document.
