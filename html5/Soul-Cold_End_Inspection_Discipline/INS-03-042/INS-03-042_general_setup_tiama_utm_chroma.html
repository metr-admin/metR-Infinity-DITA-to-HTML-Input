General Setup - Tiama - UTM Chroma

INS-03-042

Revised: 2021-09-30

Purpose and Application

Purpose

To define a work instruction for proper setup of the UTM Chroma inspection device on MX4 Machines.

Application

This work instruction applies to all O-I manufacturing facilities.

Safety

Observe O-I standard safety requirements and procedures defined by the Global Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) organization, as well as all applicable local, regional, and national requirements. Safety equipment and instructions specific to the completion of this work instruction are detailed in the Instructions Section.

Instructions

  1. Before starting the work instruction activity, perform the following steps:
    1. Do not begin these tasks without confirmation that personnel directly involved have completed the required training associated with the work instruction activity and have reviewed this document.
    2. Gather necessary tools and equipment as identified below in the Equipment Section.
    3. Wear and use additional Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safety equipment required for this specific work instruction.
    4. Obey all applicable safety requirements and procedures.
    5. Notify appropriate personnel of the activity and estimated time needed to complete the tasks.
    6. Clearly identify, mark-off, and secure the designated area defined by the work instruction activity.
    7. Follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures when work instruction activity requires isolating energy in the designated area. Verify energy is isolated before starting work.
    8. After verifying that energy is isolated, remove the protective guarding and open the safety interlock gates.
  2. To create a setup sample, perform the following steps:
    1. The setup container must have one indicator line for each required probe position.
      1. Attach pieces of tape to the container and draw lines at the exact inspection heights.
      2. Position the tape so the lines marking the inspection heights can be centered. This will allow the laser beam to be visible on the tape above and below each line during probe alignment.
  3. To set up the UTM Chroma probe(s), perform the following steps:
    1. Select the appropriate head according to the container specifications:

      Chroma

      1. 20mm: Recommended or often used for non-round containers.
      2. 8 mm: More accurate. Not recommended for non-round containers.

      For additional information, review the Tiama UTM Chroma Manual.

    2. Verify the UTM configuration on the MX4 in order to perform the setup correctly.
    3. Login to the CMX Application:
    4. Click on the Login button. A dialogue box opens to let you enter the password.
    5. Enter the Password and click OK. The Log out button is displayed.
    6. Jog a setup container into the station where the UTM Chroma probes are installed (typically station . The MX4 should be in Manual mode.
    7. OPTICAL SETTINGS Click on Detections/UTM Chroma to calibrate and then the Settings/Optical Settings button.
    8. In the Camera Settings frame, click on the Permanent button and then OK.
      1. The setup container should NOT rotate.
    9. MECHANICAL SETTINGS Use the height adjustment lever to position the axis of each probe at the height of the required measurement.
    10. Adjust the position of each probe so they are perpendicular to the glass surface.
      1. Loosen the 2 screws [1].
      2. Tilt the head (A) so that the beam is perpendicular (5) to the article wall.
    11. Adjust the position of each probe horizontally (direction A) so they are centered on the article wall.
      1. Loosen the adjusting screw [2].
      2. Turn the adjustment button [1] to the left or right.
    12. Adjust the focus point of each probe using the advance adjustment knob [1].
      1. Review UTM Chroma User Manual to calculate the optimal focal length of the UTM Chroma head.
    13. OPTICAL CALIBRATION After the mechanical calibration is performed, the optical screen should show 2 white horizontal lines (1 above and 1 below the horizontal red line).
      1. If the red line does not appear, repeat Steps 8, 9 and 10 as needed.
    14. Adjust the Brightness value to have 2 clear white lines.
      1. Enter a high value (around 50) for dark glass.
      2. Enter an average value (around 30) for standard inspections.
      3. Enter a low value (around 10) for clear glass.
    15. Resize the optical screen image.
      1. Click the button to display the optical screen in a full window mode.
      2. Drag both (top and bottom) blue lines close to the white horizontal lines to resize the UTM optical screen.
    16. Exit the optical calibration.
      1. Click on the button. It should stay gray.
  4. To set the defect threshold values, perform the following steps
    1. Select Settings and then Defect Adjustments to display the Defect Settings screen.
      1. The MX4 should be in Manual mode.
      2. Rotate the article by pressing the ON button .
    2. Adjust the Sensitivity.
      1. Set the sensitivity between 10 to 15% as a base starting point.
      2. This value represents the detection value. A peak below this value will not be identified as a thickness measurement.
      3. If the noise signal is too high (similar to the peak signal), try to recalibrate the mechanical system.
    3. Set the “Minimum thickness” limits according to the “quality approved reject value” (thresholds in 0.01 mm). The measured value for the article being processed appears in the frame above the gauge. [See blue arrow.]
      1. Measurement verdict: green for a good verdict, red for a defect.
    4. Set the “Total Losses” threshold in increments of 1%. The measured value for the article being processed appears in the frame above the gauge.
      1. Total losses include continuous and non- continuous dots (pixels) of each signal line.
    5. Set the “Continuous spot loss” threshold in increments of 1%. The measured value for the article being processed appears in the frame above the gauge.
      1. Continuous spot loss is the maximum continuous dots loss admissible on a signal line.
  5. To perform a thickness calibration using a sample container, perform the following steps:
    1. Adjusting the UTM Chroma probes Click the UTM Chroma button , then the Settings button and then the Advanced Settings screen to display the Thickness Calibration screen.
      1. Set the “Isolated points filter size” [1] and “ Thickness filter size” [2] to 3.0 as a base starting point.
      2. Set the “Offset” [3] to 0.00 as a base starting point.
      3. Deactivate (gray status) the algorithms [4] not needed for the actual UTM Chroma head.
    2. Select Settings and then Defect Settings to display the Defect Settings screen.
    3. Run a challenge sample container through the machine several times and record the thickness reading. It is recommended to calculate the average of at least 5 readings.
    4. If the reading value is not equal to the actual value measured in the lab, select the Settings/Advance Settings screen to display the Thickness Calibration screen.
      1. Modify the Offset value accordingly to match with the actual thickness value of the challenge sample container.
  6. To rename the UTM Chroma heads, perform the following steps:
    1. On the MX4 status bar, make sure the detection is set on the desired UTM Chroma head.
    2. Go to the Help menu and select the Customized translation option.
    3. Double click on the current translation column that needs to be updated.
      1. Enter the new name of the UTM Chroma head.
      2. Click on the Ok button.
  7. Upon completion of tasks, perform the following steps:
    1. Replace protective guarding and close safety interlock gates.
    2. Confirm the designated work area is clear and communicate to personnel the intent to restart energy.
    3. After the designated work area is confirmed clear and personnel are aware of the pending restart, follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures to restart energy supplied to the designated area. Verify energy is active.
    4. Remove signs and markings from the designated work area.
    5. Perform basic housekeeping duties. Clean up the work space, tools and equipment, dispose of trash, put tools and equipment in assigned area.
    6. Notify appropriate personnel that the work instruction activity has been completed.

Required Training

Before performing this work instruction, applicable personnel must be trained by an authorized trainer on the required training contents listed in this document.
