Device Setup - Sidewall - MCAL 4

INS-03-052

Revised: 2017-01-23

Purpose and Application

Purpose

To define a work instruction for proper setup of the sidewall inspection device on the MCAL 4.

Application

This work instruction applies to all O-I manufacturing facilities.

Safety

Observe O-I standard safety requirements and procedures defined by the Global Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) organization, as well as all applicable local, regional, and national requirements. Safety equipment and instructions specific to the completion of this work instruction are detailed in the Instructions Section.

Instructions

  1. Before starting the work instruction activity, perform the following steps:
    1. Do not begin these tasks without confirmation that personnel directly involved have completed the required training associated with the work instruction activity and have reviewed this document.
    2. Gather necessary tools and equipment as identified below in the Equipment Section.
    3. Wear and use additional Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safety equipment required for this specific work instruction.
    4. Obey all applicable safety requirements and procedures.
  2. Prerequisite:
    1. Prior to this work instruction, ensure container handling was properly set up according to INS-03-012 Container Handling – Tiama – MCAL 4.
    2. Prepare a setup container by applying a 0.76-1.27 mm (0.030 - 0.050) inch black dot vertically centered on the sidewall of the container.Prepare a setup container by applying a 0.76-1.27 mm (0.030 - 0.050) inch black dot vertically centered on the sidewall of the container.
  3. For proper setup, perform the following steps:
    1. The HMI screen of the MCAL 4 is located on the front right door of the machine.
    2. Stop the flow of containers into the machine using whatever container spacing device is installed prior to the machine.
  4. For proper setup, perform the following steps:
    1. From the main screen, click on the Log in button and login to the software using job change a password.
    2. Open the Article - Management – this contains a list of all saved jobs.
    3. Choose an existing job, or a current job to be modified or a new job.
      1. Click New > Enter Name, Neck Diameter, body diameter, and height.
      2. Ensure the body diameter and height are correct. (Recommend adding 5mm for each measurement).
      3. Load the new job file.
    4. You can also open an existing job file and save as a new job, then adjust the settings and parameters for the new job.
    5. Exit the Article menu.
    6. Open sidewall from the Detections Menu.
    7. Press Optical Settings from the Adjustments Menu.
    8. Select Body.
    9. Place a container on the conveyor in front of the camera’s flashing light source.
    10. Adjust the brightness of the body camera using the oscilloscope to 300µs.
      1. Individually adjust each LED line (1 to 20) in order to set the blue vertical line as close as possible to the middle of the two 50% and 75% lines.
    11. Click on the Shoulder button.
    12. Adjust the height of the cameras using the adjustment wheel so the container is displayed in the center of the image.
    13. Enter the camera height number that is displayed on the adjustment wheel from the above step.
    14. Open Distances from the Adjustments Menu.
    15. Close the doors of the machine and start the conveyor.
    16. Pass a container upstream to the spacer to pass through the machine.
    17. Adjust the centering by moving the center blue vertical lines in the middle of the container in views #2 and #5.
      1. The container must be centered inside each image.
    18. Continue to pass containers and adjust until the containers are centered in views #2 and #5.
    19. Adjust the number of gauges to 5.
    20. Group all the horizontal gauges of all the views onto the neck of the article with the aid of the mouse or the screen.
    21. Repeat until all horizontal gauges are in line with the container.
      1. Check the color of the horizontal gauges (they should be green) and the correct positioning of the blue dots on the edges of the article.
      2. If gauges are unable to become aligned, repeat the optical setup.
  5. For proper zone setup, perform the following steps:
    1. Open the Zones and Defects from the Adjustments Menu.
      1. The left half of the screen will have sensitivity parameters, while the right will show the image.
    2. Select the drawing icon (7th icon from the top) to create zones.
    3. Select Body.
    4. Move the horizontal lines to the top of the finish and on the base under the punt.
    5. The lower line must be positioned just at the lower inspect-able point of the bottom (not above the punt).
    6. The upper line must include the totality of the finish. Otherwise, some parts of the bottle are not inspected.
    7. Press the Find Outline icon and select ok to all.
    8. Review and adjust zones for each camera and adjust as needed.
      1. A box appears as zone “0”. This must entrap the entire container.
      2. Can have up to 7 adjustment zones.
      3. Always use a minimum of 2 inspection zones.
      4. The zone with the highest number will be the active zone.
        1. The active zone will turn red when selected.
    9. Press the + icon to add zones and select the location of the zones.
      1. Any area outside the zone will not be inspected.
    10. Select Finish and adjust the size of the zone to include the finish area.
      1. A dark blue rectangle is displayed.
    11. Select Bottom and adjust the size of the zone to include the bottom area.
      1. A dark blue rectangle is displayed.
    12. Select the Shoulder.
      1. Repeat creating zones as with finish and bottom.
    13. Repeat for any additional zones to ensure the entire container is inspected properly.
      1. Extra zones may be needed for engravings, punt, container shape, and finish.
    14. Press the Copy Icon to copy zones to all cameras.
    15. Switch from one camera to another using the images at the bottom of the screen and adjust zones as needed for each camera.
      1. Select zone 0 to move all zones within.
      2. Select individual zones to move them.
    16. Deselect the drawing icon to exit zones.
  6. To set up reject parameter settings, perform the following steps:
    1. Select each image at the bottom of the screen to view what defects (if any) the machine detected.
    2. Choose the specific zone parameters according to MSDR INS-07-033 Inspection Device Setup Parameters for Tiama Equipment.
    3. To put the machine into operation mode, press the operation mode button.
    4. Pass a setup sample through the machine to trap four images to confirm proper rejection.
      1. A setup container could be either a good or defect container.
      2. If multiple machines are present on a production line, the same setup container must be used for each machine to ensure consistency across machines.
    5. Restart the container spacer.
    6. Review inspection results during production and repeat procedure as necessary to ensure proper rejection, minimal commercial ware loss, and proper camera balance.
  7. Upon completion of tasks, perform the following steps:
    1. Perform basic housekeeping duties. Clean up the work space, tools and equipment, dispose of trash, put tools and equipment in assigned area.
    2. Notify appropriate personnel that the work instruction activity has been completed.
    3. Record and report findings and results.
    4. Follow appropriate instructions for notification of findings and results relating to specifications, targets and/or reaction limits.

Required Training

Before performing this work instruction, applicable personnel must be trained by an authorized trainer on the required training contents listed in this document.
