Transmission Oil & Oil Filter - Change

Warning:

Hot oil and components can cause personal injury. Do not allow hot oil or components to contact skin.

Park the lift truck level, with the forks lowered, parking brake engaged, transmission in NEUTRAL and the engine stopped.

  1. Open the oil drain plug and drain the used oil.
    Notice:

    Careless disposal of waste oil can harm the environment and can be dangerous to persons. Always dispose of waste oil to authorised and licensed personnel only.

  2. Clean all drain plug with magnetic insert and surface on the housing and install it again with O- ring.
  3. Rotate the filter counter-clockwise to remove it.
  4. Put a small amount of clean oil on the gasket.
  5. Clean the filter head.
    Note:

    Transmission Oil Filter should be replaced First 100 Service Hours and Every 1000 Service Hours with the change of the Transmission Oil. The filter differential pressure valve (bypass valve) is equipped with a maintenance-switch which is informing the operator about the contamination of the filter. At the lighting-up of the symbol on Transmission Display, the filter must be replaced.

  6. Screw the filter and tighten it by hand about 1/3 to 1/2 turn.
  7. Loosen the oil dipstick counter-clockwise and remove it. Fill the compartment with oil. See “Refill Capacities”. Install the dipstick.
  8. Start the engine.
  9. With the service brake applied and engine at low idle, shift the transmission to forward 1st, 2nd, 3rd and reverse 1st, 2nd, 3rd to fill the clutches.
  10. Shift the transmission into NEUTRAL. Engage the parking brake.
  11. Loosen the oil dipstick counter-clockwise, remove and clear it. Insert the oil dipstick slowly into the oil level tube until contact is obtained, and pull the dipstick out again.
  12. When the oil temperature is around 40°C, the marking on the oil dipstick must be lying above the cold start mark, “COLD”. When the oil temperature is around 80°C, the oil level must be lying in the zone “HOT”.
  13. Install the oil dipstick again and tighten it clockwise.
  14. Check for oil leaks at the filter and drain plug.
  15. Stop the engine.
