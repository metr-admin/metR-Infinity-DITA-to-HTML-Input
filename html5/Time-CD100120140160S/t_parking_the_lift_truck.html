Parking the Lift Truck

Typical Example

Park the lift truck level, with the forks lowered and the mast tilted forward until the fork tips touch the floor.

Block the drive wheels when parking on an incline.

  1. Park in authorised area only. Do not block traffic.
  2. Place the transmission controls in NEUTRAL.
  3. Engage the parking brake (push the parking brake button).

    Typical Example

  4. Lower the forks to the ground.
    Warning:

    Blocking the wheels will prevent unexpected lift truck movement, which could cause personal injury.

    Note:

    Always run the engine at low idle for 5 minutes before turning off the engine.

  5. Turn the ignition key switch to the OFF position and remove the key.
  6. Actuate each loading lever several times to remove the residual pressure in the respective cylinders and hoses.
  7. Block the drive wheels if parking on an incline.
