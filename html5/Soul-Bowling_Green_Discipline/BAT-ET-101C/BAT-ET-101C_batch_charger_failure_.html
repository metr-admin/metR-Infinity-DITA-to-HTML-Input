Batch Charger Failure

BAT-ET-101C

Revised: 2023-02-26

Purpose/Application

Defines a reaction plan for a charger failure in the batch system.

PPE Required

General

Bump cap / hard hat, safety glasses with foam liner, proper hearing protection, fire-resistant long-sleeved shirt, safety toe boots.

Safety Hazards

Safety Controls

  • Exposure to heat can cause illness and death. The most serious heat illness is heat stroke. Other heat illnesses include heat exhaustion, heat cramps, and heat rash. Take precautions to avoid heat illness any time temperatures are high, and the job involves physical work.
  • The Hazard Communication Standard (HCS) requires chemicals to have Safety Data Sheets (SDS). Use the SDS for chemicals listed in this SOP for additional hazards, first aid measures, toxicological information, accidental release measures, and handling/storage of the chemical.
  • Work/rest ratios are based on the heat index inside/outside the plant. Notify leadership immediately if someone is showing signs of heat stress.
  • Hot surfaces are present in areas where molten glass is produced. Use caution and proper hand and body protection.
  • If a task causes exposure to hot molten glass, wear the appropriate shaded eyewear to decrease exposure to infrared radiation.
  • Use the buddy system.
  • Stay hydrated.
  • Some tasks in this document require additional PPE and tools/equipment to perform tasks and may have specific safety hazards.
  • Review the Emergency Action Plan before performing work.

Melter SCADA Operator Station

Batch Charger Failure

See the following to diagnose and resolve a batch charger failure:

  1. Common symptoms of charger failure:
    • A sudden decrease in melter weight
    • A sudden increase in melter temperature

    The charger can fail in multiple ways:

    • Motor failure
    • Gearbox failure
    • Drive chain failure
    • Drive shaft failure
    • Bearing failure
    • Screw failure
    • Water jacket failure
    • VFD fault or deviation
  2. Note:

    PPE Required: Respirator or PAPR.

    Dust hazard.

    Toxic materials.

    Pinch hazard.

    Fall hazard. Utilize 3 points of contact on ladders.
    Inspect the charger surge hopper.
    If the surge hopper is empty,
    • Check the SBS for alarms or faults.
    • Clean buildup off the sides of the surge hopper. Buildup can interfere with the surge hopper level sensor.

    If the surge hopper is full,

    • There might be an issue with the charger or the batch inlet could be clogged.
    Note:

    Do not damage nearby instruments such as the moisture analyzer.

  3. Note:

    PPE Required: Respirator or PAPR, fire-resistant jacket, heat-resistant gloves, face shield with IR shade 5.0 lens.

    Dust hazard.

    Caution: Hot surfaces.

    Bright molten glass.

    Trip hazard. Use caution near pipes and wires.

    Falling hot objects.

    Heat Stress. Use the buddy system.
    Inspect the inlet tube.
    • Remove the outer top cover from the inlet tube.
    • The cover weighs approximately 7 kg (15 lbs) and has a handle.
    • Leave the inner perforated cover on to contain any splashing glass.
  4. If the inlet tube is dark:
    • The batch inlet is clogged.
    • Complete all the steps in BAT-ET-101A Clearning Plugs from the Batch System.

    If the inlet tube is bright:

    • The batch inlet is clear.
    • There might be an issue with the charger.
  5. At the operator station, go to the Overview tab.
    • Check if CH1SPD is operating at the expected speed.
  6. Physically inspect the Batch Charger VFD.
    Check the current reading, displayed at the bottom of the screen.
    • If the current is 0, the motor fuses might be blown.
    • If the VFD shows an overcurrent fault, the charger might be jammed.
    • If the VFD shows that the motor is running at a normal current (between 1-7 amps), the drive chain might be broken.
    Note:

    The issue must be resolved before resetting any faults.

  7. If the VFD values are not the same as the operator station values:
    • Run the charger off of the VFD.
    • Tell the Tech 5 there is a discrepancy between the operator station and VFD.
  8. Note:

    Lockout tagout.

    Electric shock.

    Rotating parts.

    Pinch hazard.
    If the VFD shows 0 “zero” current or an overcurrent fault:
    • LO/TO the charger.
    • Remove any large obstructions in the discharge of the charger surge hopper.
    • Contact the line leader.
    • The Tech 5 can briefly reverse the screw to clear small clogs.
    • The Tech 5 replaces fuses if necessary.
  9. If the VFD shows that the motor is running at a normal current, physically inspect the charger.
    • Check if the fan of the motor is spinning.
    • Check if the drive chain is broken.

    If the fan of the motor is spinning but the drive chain is not moving

    • The gearbox might have failed.

    If the fan of the motor is spinning and the drive chain is moving:

    • The screw might have failed.
  10. If the obstruction cannot be removed from the charger or the motor, gearbox, drive chain, or the screw has failed:
    • Inform the Tech 5 that the charger requires service.
    • The melter needs to be put in a Hot Hold until the fault can be resolved.
  11. Complete all steps in MLT-OP-101H to set the melter to a Hot Hold.
  12. To confirm that the screw has failed:
    • Remove the heat shield wrap around the charger barrel.
    • Loosen and remove any bolts if needed.
    • Remove any straps if needed.

    PPE Required: Respirator or PAPR.

    Lockout tagout.

    Dust hazard.

    Toxic materials

    Pinch hazard.

    Fall hazard. Utilize 3 points of contact on ladders.
    Tools Required: Rubber mallet, pry bar, vacuum, screwdriver.

    • LO/TO the charger.
    • Disconnect the charger from the chopper.
    • Remove the retaining nut on the front left leg of the charger.
    Note:

    Do not damage nearby instruments, such as the moisture analyzer.
