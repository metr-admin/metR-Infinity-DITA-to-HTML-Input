Overview of the Belt Conveyor

SBS-OP-138A

Revised: 2024-02-15

Purpose/Application

Provides an overview of the belt conveyor.

PPE Required

General

Bump cap/hard hat, safety glasses with foam liner, proper hearing protection, fire-resistant long-sleeved shirt, safety toe boots. Respirator or PAPR in case of a toxic batch material spill.

Safety Hazards

Respirable Crystalline Silica

Crystalline silica has been classified as a human lung carcinogen. Breathing crystalline silica dust can cause silicosis, which in severe cases can be disabling, or even fatal.

Safety Controls

The Hazard Communication Standard (HCS) requires chemicals to have Safety Data Sheets (SDS). Refer to the SDS for chemicals listed in this SOP for additional hazards, first aid measures, toxicological information, accidental release measures, and handling/storage of the chemical.

Review the Emergency Action Plan before performing work.

Have a fire extinguisher nearby

Introduction

The conveyor belt is used to transport bulk material. This continuous conveyor runs automatically. A control system performs the centralized acquisition of information, and controls and monitors all machine functions.

The belt conveyor consists of a belt frame (1) and a support structure (2). The drive station (3), tensioning station (4), drive unit (5) and a number of roller stations (6) are fastened to the belt frame. A conveyor belt (7) is tensioned between the drive and tensioning pulleys.

The conveyor belt is guided over the tensioning pulley and drive pulley and transports the bulk material. Feed units at the loading point transport the bulk material to the conveyor belt. The conveyor belt transports the bulk material to the delivery point.

The guide gutters (8) guide the bulk material on the conveyor belt. The guide gutters are equipped with rubber seals which prevent the bulk material from falling off the conveyor belt. Idler rollers (9) support the conveyor belt together with the bulk material.

A belt cleaner (e.g. belt scraper) (10) is located after the delivery point. This cleans any adhering material from the conveyor belt. The conveyor belt is guided back to the loading point via return rollers (11).

Drive Station

The drive station causes the conveyor belt to move via the drive pulley (1). A drive unit (2) is connected to the belt conveyor via the drive shaft (3) and is thus able to transfer its rotary movement to the belt conveyor. The drive unit can be optionally equipped with an anti- reversing mechanism. This prevents the drive unit or conveyor belt from running back- wards.

The drive shaft is mounted on two flanged bearings (4) in such a way that it can rotate. A scraper (5) cleans material contaminations from the drive pulley in order to prevent any damage to the conveyor belt.

Tensioning Station

The conveyor belt is tensioned and guided via the tensioning station. The tensioning pulley

(1) is moved in a straight line via tensioning spindles (2), thereby increasing or reducing the tension of the conveyor belt. The tensioning pulley is mounted on two pedestal bearings (4) by means of a shaft (3) and is able to rotate. A rotary survey is mounted at the end of the shaft (5).

Belt Cleaners (Pneumatic)

In the normal version, the belt conveyor is equipped with belt cleaners that are adjusted and readjusted mechanically. In the case of the reversible belt conveyor, the belt cleaner is optionally available as a pneumatically adjustable unit. In this variant of the belt conveyor, a scraper is mounted at the drive and tensioning pulleys. Depending on the direction of transport, the scraper opposite the transport direction is lowered.

Operating Modes

Automatic mode is the default mode for operation of the belt conveyor. In automatic mode, all the operating sequences of the belt conveyor run automatically, and the belt conveyor is operated in combination with other machines or system components. All current data, messages, errors and other information are displayed at the user interface.

The belt conveyor can be operated manually for maintenance purposes. In this case, man- ual operation is performed via the user interface at the control cabinet. This operating mode is intended for emergencies and for maintenance and repair work. There are no locking mechanisms in manual mode.
