Overview of the Metering Conveyor Scale

SBS-OP-136B

Revised: 2024-01-30

Purpose/Application

Describes the procedures for starting and stopping the metering conveyor scale

PPE Required

General

Bump cap/hard hat, safety glasses with foam liner, proper hearing protection, fire-resistant long sleeved shirt, safety toe boots.

Required for Specific Tasks

Respirator or PAPR in case of a toxic batch material spill.

Safety Hazards

Respirable Crystalline Silica

Crystalline silica has been classified as a human lung carcinogen. Breathing crystalline silica dust can cause silicosis, which in severe cases can be disabling, or even fatal.

Safety Controls

  • The Hazard Communication Standard (HCS) requires chemicals to have Safety Data Sheets (SDS). Use the SDS for chemicals listed in this SOP for additional hazards, first aid measures, toxicological information, accidental release measures, and handling/storage of the chemical.
  • Review the Emergency Action Plan before performing work.

Equipment Needed

Access to the metering conveyor scale control screen and power switch.

Comments

Before starting work:

  • Read this document thoroughly.
  • Complete the required training.
  • Wear the required PPE.

Safety Mechanisms and Controls

  1. Before operating the metering conveyor scale, verify that all safety mechanisms, such as the protective housings and covers, are installed and functioning properly.
  2. Note:

    Electric Shock
    The lockable main switch on the door of the metering conveyor scale control cabinet is used to switch off the power supply to the metering conveyor scale.
    • The cabinet is located on the Hydra-Mix floor (two floors up from the melter control room).
    • When the switch is switched to “Off”, the metering conveyor scale will not work and no movements will occur.
    CAUTION:

    Even after a main switch has been switched off, the following circuits remain live (if present) for the corresponding control cabinet: Lighting for control cabinet, control cabinet socket, control cabinet ventilation.

  3. Note:

    Electric Shock
    The E-Stop switch is located at the control cabinet. It shuts down all functions and movements.
    CAUTION:

    E-Stop switches do not disconnect the metering conveyor scale from the power supply. All machine parts that are live during normal operation continue to be live after an emergency stop has been triggered.

  4. Note:

    LO/TO (Lockout-Tagout)
    The metering conveyor scale also has a service switch to switch off the device.
    • The service switch does not completely cut off the metering conveyor scale from the power supply. Lines that are still live even when the service switch is off are identified in orange.
    • LO/TO the service switch before performing maintenance work.
  5. The metering conveyor scale has warning signs for:
    • Dangerous electrical voltage
    • Danger of pulling-in and crushing
  6. Hazardous materials and substances are transported on the metering conveyor scale.
    • Observe all plant requirements when operating the device.
    • Replace contaminated clothing with clean clothing immediately.

Starting and Stopping the Metering Conveyor Scale

  1. Perform the following checks before starting up the metering conveyor scale:
    • Inspect the metering conveyor scale for visible damage, and correct any problems before operating.
    • Verify that the safety mechanisms are installed and in proper condition.
    • Verify that the rotary survey is installed and functioning correctly.
    • Verify that the feed and discharge unit is connected correctly.
    • Make sure that no personnel are present in the danger area or in the areas of movement of machine parts.
  2. To turn on the metering conveyor scale:
    • Switch the lockable main switch to On.
    • Acknowledge the emergency stop using the operating screen.
  3. To turn off the metering conveyor scale:
    • Use the user interface at the control cabinet.
    • The metering conveyor scale continues to run and terminates the movements of the actuators.
    • Then switch off the metering conveyor scale using the lockable main switch.
  4. If a fault or malfunction occurs, this is indicated at the user interface.
    Note:

    For information about possible errors and malfunctions and the procedure for eliminating errors, see the electrical documentation.
