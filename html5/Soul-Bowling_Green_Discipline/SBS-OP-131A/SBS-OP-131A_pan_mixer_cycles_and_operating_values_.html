Pan Mixer Cycles and Operating Values

SBS-OP-131A

Revised: 2024-01-23

Purpose/Application

Describes the mixing cycles and operating values for the pan mixer.

PPE Required

General

Bump cap/hard hat, safety glasses with foam liner, proper hearing protection, fire-resistant long sleeved shirt, safety toe boots.

Additional PPE When Needed

Respirator or PAPR in case of a toxic batch material spill.

Safety Hazards

Respirable Crystalline Silica

Crystalline silica has been classified as a human lung carcinogen. Breathing crystalline silica dust can cause silicosis, which in severe cases can be disabling, or even fatal.

Safety Controls

  • Exposure to heat can cause illness and death. The most serious heat illness is heat stroke. Other heat illnesses include heat exhaustion, heat cramps, and heat rash. Take precautions to avoid heat illness any time temperatures are high, and the job involves physical work.
  • The Hazard Communication Standard (HCS) requires chemicals to have Safety Data Sheets (SDS). Use the SDS for chemicals listed in this SOP for additional hazards, first aid measures, toxicological information, accidental release measures, and handling/storage of the chemical.
  • If a task causes exposure to hot molten glass, wear the appropriate shaded eyewear to decrease exposure to infrared radiation.
  • Review the Emergency Action Plan before performing work.
  • Follow donning and doffing procedure.
  • Some tasks in this document require additional PPE and tools/equipment to perform tasks, and may have specific safety hazards.

Equipment Needed

Access to the mixer control panel and the SBS SCADA workstation.

Comments

Before starting work:

  • Read this document thoroughly.
  • Complete the required training.
  • Wear the required PPE.

Mixer Cycles and Operating Values

  1. The mixer is automatically operated by the SBS system. The mixing cycle has 5 stages:
    • Filling the Mixer
    • Dry Mixing
    • Water Dosing
    • Wet Mixing
    • Discharge
    Note:

    The values are set on the Formula screen for the amount of time for dry mixing, water dosing, wet mixing, and discharge. To set the values, refer to SBS-OP-031B Setting the Mixer Parameters Using the Formula Screen.

    Note:

    To manually operate the mixer by using the touch panel near the mixer, refer to SBS-OP-031C Manual Mode Using the Touch Screen Near the Mixer.

    Note:

    There is an E-Stop on the mixer control panel and on a column near the discharge side of the mixer.

  2. Filling the Mixer: When the batch receiving hopper above the mixer becomes full:
    • The system will verify that there are no mixer faults and that the lid is closed.
    • The mixing paddles and side scrappers in the mixer begin to rotate.
    • The closure at the bottom of the hopper then opens, and the hopper contents empty into the mixer.
  3. Dry Mixing: When the hopper is empty, the mixing continues for the amount of time specified for the batch formula.
  4. Water Dosing: When the dry mixing time is completed, the specified amount of water is fed into the mixer. There are two different modes for adding water, which are selected on the Formula screen.
    • Dosing by Value. The system monitors the signal from the water counter and compares it with the value in the formula. When the set point is reached, the water valve will close.
      • The PLC checks the time between the impulses from the water counter. If the water runs too slow or water dosing takes too long, the water valve will close, and a failure message is generated.
      • If dosing is interrupted because of a failure, feed the residual amount manually or abort the dosing.
      • For manual dosing, use the button from the water valve on the SCADA screen or the faceplate. The water valve closes when the set point is reached.
      • To abort the dosing, click on the blue failure reset button for the mixer.
    • Dosing by Time. If this mode is active, the water valve will open for the specified amount of time, and then the water valve closes. There is no check for the amount of water flow. This mode can be used if the water counter is damaged.
  5. Wet Mixing: After the water adding, the wet mixing time starts.
  6. Discharge: After wet mixing is completed, the fill release signal from the post hopper scale should activate.
    • The discharge slide/gate on the bottom of the mixer then opens, and discharges the batch to the post hopper scale.
    • The discharge timer begins.
    • If the weight of the batch (typically one metric ton) is not discharged from the mixer within the programmed time, the system produces a fault/error message.
    • The weight is shown on the integration control panel screen.
  7. After the material is discharged and the discharge slide/gate closes, the mixer is ready to be filled again.
