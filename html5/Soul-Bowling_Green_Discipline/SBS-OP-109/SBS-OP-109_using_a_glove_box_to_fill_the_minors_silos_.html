Using a Glove Box to Fill the Minors Silos

SBS-OP-109

Revised: 2024-02-15

Purpose/Application

Describes the procedures for filling minors silos 7 and 8 by using a glove box.

PPE Required

General

Bump cap/hard hat, safety glasses with foam liner, proper hearing protection, fire-resistant long sleeved shirt, safety toe boots.

Have available a respirator with certified cartridges, or a PAPR, in case of a toxic batch material spill.

Safety Hazards

Safety Controls

  • The Hazard Communication Standard (HCS) requires chemicals to have Safety Data Sheets (SDS). Use the SDS for chemicals listed in this SOP for additional hazards, first aid measures, toxicological information, accidental release measures, and handling/storage of the chemical.
  • Review the Emergency Action Plan before performing work.

Equipment Needed

  • Access to the SCADA SBS workstation.

Comments

Before starting work:

  • Read this document thoroughly.
  • Complete the required training.
  • Wear the required PPE.

This SOP uses Streator screens and images.

Filling Silos 7 and 8 by Using a Glove Box

  1. Select the appropriate glove box unit. When standing in front of the units:
    • The right unit handles selenium for silo 7
    • The left unit handles cobalt for silo 8.
    Note:

    Before using, confirm which unit handles which material.

  2. Place the material container on the platform for the appropriate glove box.
    • Fasten the bucket using the red strap.
  3. At the SBS workstation, display the Silofeeding Silo screen for the Minors silos:
    • Press the Silo Feeding tab.
    • Navigate to the appropriate Silofeeding Silo screen.
    • Select the silo being filled, which should match the position of the container (silo 7 or 8).
    Note:

    There is no operation mode. The filling works if the control power is on.

  4. Turn on the light inside the glove box, if desired. The switch is on the control panel on the side wall.
  5. At control panel #1 on the glove box, push the button for the container sizeâ€”either 11.25 inches or 13 inches.
    • The green light for the button will light up when the button is pushed.
    Note:

    Do not use the red Stop button.

  6. Note:

    Pinch hazard.

    Dust hazard.

    Raise the container to the glove box.

    • Remove the container lid.
    • Press and hold the Tilting Mechanism Lift buttons on both control panels at the same time. (See the arrows in the image.)
    • When the end position is reached, the lifting platform automatically stops.
    • Release both buttons.
    Note:

    Two hands are required to lift the container for safety purposes. Make sure to push the Lift button on each control panel, and continue holding them until the platform reaches its end position and stops.

    Note:

    if there is a problem, lower the container all the way down, then re-raise it.

  7. Ensure that the bag inside the container falls into the glove box.
    • Seal the opening on the unit by pulling on the pre-mounted bag and tying it shut with a zip tie.
  8. Note:

    Pinch hazard.

    Lower the empty container.

    • Press and hold the Tilting Mechanism Lower buttons on both control panels at the same time. (See the arrows in the image.)
    • When the lifting platform stops at its fully lowered position, release both buttons.
    Note:

    Two hands are required to lower the container for safety purposes. Make sure to push the Lower button on each control panel, and continue holding them until the platform reaches its lowest position and stops.

  9. Note:

    Have a respirator or PAPR available in case of a cobalt or selenium release.

    Place your hands into the gloves attached to the box.

    • Use the knife inside the glove box to slit the bag open.
    • Empty the contents from the bag.
    • Ensure that the bag is empty.
  10. Dispose of the bag through the side port.
    • Remove the rubber cover, place the bag through the opening, and replace the rubber cover.
  11. Start the vacuum conveying system to transfer the material in the glove box to the silo.

    Press the Feeding Silo Start switch on control unit #1.

  12. The silo stops filling when:
    • The Feeding Silo Stop button on control box #1 is pressed.
    • The silo is full or overfull.
    • The silo is blocked.
    Note:

    If the filling stops unexpectedly, go to the SDS control screens to determine the problem, and resolve it.

    • If the red failure light is on at the control panel on the side wall, press the failure reset button.
