Using 2D and 3D Viewer Tools

A set of tools in the document viewer enables you to view and inspect 2D and 3D drawings in detail. The options and tools depend on the type of file you open.

In general, after you select a tool, you can continue to use other tools along with it. For example, after you selectExplode Model, you can useZoomandOrbittools. To restore your drawing model to the default view, clickHome.

The following table provides information on using each of the tools in the document viewer.

Term Icon Function
Pan Drag to move the drawing or model to a different part of the viewer.
Zoom Perform any of the following options to zoom in or zoom out a drawing model:
  • Click a part of the design and double-click to zoom.
  • Click a part of the design and press CTRL + Down Arrow key to zoom in and CTRL + Up Arrow key to zoom out.
  • Click a part of the design and rotate the wheel button.
    • Forward or away from user = zoom out.
    • Backward or towards the user = zoom in.
  • Click Home to return to the default view.
Camera Interactions Click and then click the type of view you want to use.
  • Click to position the model at the center of the viewport.
  • Click to rotate the model.
  • If the file is a 3D model, then click to change the current camera focal length of the model.
Measure Click to view all the measurement options and then select the appropriate option. When completed, click Done to turn off the measurement.

In 2D designs, you can use the following measurement options:

  • Distance
  • Angle
  • Area
  • Arc
  • Calibrate

In 3D designs, you can use the following measurement options:

  • Distance
  • Angle
  • Calibrate
Document Browser To switch the view between 2D and 3D, perform the following steps:
  1. Click .

    The Document Browser dialog box is displayed.

  2. In the Tree tab, expand Model and then click the appropriate view.
    Note:
    If the document supports only 2D View , then the 3D View option is not available.
  3. To view the thumbnails of the drawing, click the Thumbnails tab.
  4. Click the appropriate page.
Layer Manager To view layers in the drawing design, perform the following steps:
  1. Click . The Layers dialog box is displayed.
  2. In the Layers dialog box, click to show or hide layers in the drawing model.

    Alternatively, in the Enter Filter Term box, click and enter the layer name to search for a layer.
Properties Click to view the properties of a selected part within the drawing.
Full Screen Click to change the viewer to full-screen view.

Press Esc or click the Full Screen toggle option to exit full-screen view.

Tools specific to 3D drawings:

Term Icon Function
Orbit Click and then click the appropriate rotate option you want to use.
  • Click to rotate the model.
  • Click to rotate and tilt the model as needed.
First Person To navigate through the 3D drawing model as if you were actually inside the model, perform the following steps:
  1. Click . The Navigate in First Person dialog box is displayed.
  2. Read the navigation instructions, and then click Ok, Got It .
  3. Navigate through the 3D model using the following options:
    • Use the directional keys on the keyboard to navigate through your model. Alternatively, you can use the W A S D keys as the directional keys.
      • ↑ or W - move forward
      • ↓ or S - move back
      • ← or A - move left
      • → or D - move right
    • Use Shift + directional keys to move faster.
    • To adjust the walk speed, use the + or - keys.
    • Click Home to return to the default view.
Section Analysis Click and then click the type of sectioning you want to use.
  • Click to section along the x-axis.
  • Click to section along the y-axis.
  • Click to section along the z-axis.
  • Click to section a specific area.

Drag the desired manipulators until the model is sectioned the way you want it. When done, click again to turn off sectioning.
Explode Model To take apart a 3D drawing design and see the parts that make up the model, perform the following steps:
  1. Click . A slider is displayed.
  2. Drag the slider forward to separate the parts of the model.
  3. Drag the slider backward to bring the model back together.
  4. Optionally, use Zoom to take a closer look at a part.
  5. Click again to go back to the default view.
Model Browser To view or hide a model in the drawing, perform the following steps:
  1. Click . The Model dialog box is displayed.
  2. In the Model dialog box, click to show or hide a complete model or the parts in the drawing design.
  3. In the Search box, click and enter the name of the part that you want to search.
