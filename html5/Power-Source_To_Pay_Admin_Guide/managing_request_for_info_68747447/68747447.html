Managing Request for Information

Request for Information (RFI) is a questionnaire prepared by the buyer to ask the questions to the suppliers.

A request for information (RFI) is a proposal requested from a potential seller or a service provider to determine what products and services are potentially available in the marketplace to meet a buyer's needs and to know the capability of a seller in terms of offerings and strengths of the seller. RFIs are commonly used on major procurements, where a requirement could potentially be met through several alternate means. An RFI, however, is not an invitation to bid, is not binding on either the buyer or sellers, and may or may not lead to an RFP or RFQ. The RFI can be closed and reopened as well. The Supplier fills the details and sends it back to the Buyer. The Buyer then scores the Suppliers on the basis of RFI received. The Stakeholders will be notified about the same. You can configure up to 1000 questions in an RFI.

We have two kinds of suppliers.

  • Portal Suppliers - The suppliers who have access to the customer portal (possess a customer portal account) or partner portal (possess a partner portal account).
  • Non - Portal Suppliers - The suppliers who do not have access to either customer portal or partner portal.

For Portal Suppliers, the RFI Survey is available on the Supplier Portal. Whereas, for Non-Portal Suppliers, the RFI Survey is available through an unauthenticated URL in the form of a Visualforce page. You can configure and customize the welcome page of RFI Survey. The Suppliers will receive the link to RFI Survey via an email. The Suppliers will then respond to the RFI Survey and submit their responses. You will receive the response under Responses > RFI tab. You can create the responses as well.

You can configure the RFI Survey page in the supplier portal. You can customize the RFI Survey page from the Email Templates in Salesforce.

  • You can sort the questions based on topics.

To create a request for information

  1. Click the Sourcing Events tab.
  2. Select the appropriate sourcing event and click Edit.
  3. Click the Preparation tab and click the RFI tab.
  4. Click Create.

To add a question to RFI

  1. Click the Sourcing Events tab.
  2. Select the appropriate sourcing event and click Edit.
  3. Click the Preparation tab and click the RFI tab.
  4. Click View.
  5. Enter the Description of RFI.
  6. Click Search & Add.
  7. Enter keywords or description or question to search for and click Search. -or-

    Click Search to display all questionnaires.

    Note:

    Note

    The results are displayed in Search Results section.

  8. Click Add next besides the question that you want to add to your RFI.
    Note:

    Note

    Do this for all the questionnaires that you want to include in your RFI.

  9. Select Response Required check box if you require the supplier to answer that question. Response Required check box is selected by default.
    Note:

    Note

    Ensure that you have selected Response Required field to be Visible. For more information, refer to Profile Settings and Security.

  10. To clone a question in RFI, click Clone.
  11. Edit the question as per requirement and click Add.
  12. To reorder any question, drag-and-drop a question to the appropriate position.
  13. Click to expand Save Questionnaire section.
    Note:

    Note

    Step 8 and Step 9 are optional. Perform these steps if you want to save the current questionnaire for future use.

  14. Select Save as a new Questionnaire and enter the Questionnaire Name and Description to save the selected questions as a new questionnaire.
  15. Click Upload to upload an attachment to the sourcing event.
    Note:

    Note

    For the Attachments in Apttus STP, you can upload any file. We allow files of the following extension types: (.PDF), (.RTF), (.RTX), (.TXT), (.JPG), (.JPEG), (.PNG), (.GIF), (.TIF), (.BMP), (.DOC), (.DOCX), (.DOCM), (.PPT), (.PPTX), (.PPTM), (.PPS), (.PPSX), (.XLS), (.XLSX), (.XLSM), (.ZIP), (.MP3), (.M4A), (.OGG), (.WAV), (.MP4), (.M4V), (.MOV), (.OGV), (.3GP), (.3G2). The application might become slow if you upload a file higher than 25 MB.

  16. Click Choose File and select the appropriate file.
  17. Enter the File Description.
  18. Select the appropriate permission from the Permissions picklist menu.

    Permission

    Description

    Public

    To make the attachment available to all the Suppliers.

    Private

    To make the attachment available to only the Buyer.

    Selective Public

    To make the attachment available to the selected Supplier(s).

    Note: At least one supplier must be selected for this option.
  19. Click Attach.
  20. To edit an attachment, click Edit.

    -or-

    To delete an attachment, click Delete.

  21. Click Download to download the RFI.
  22. Click Save.

To create a new question

  1. Click the Sourcing Events tab.
  2. Select the appropriate sourcing event and click Edit.
  3. Click the Preparation tab and click the RFI tab.
  4. Click View.
  5. Click Create New.
  6. Complete the following fields.

    Field Name

    Description

    Question

    Enter the statement of the question.

    Weight

    Enter the weight of the question. (Importance level of the question)

    Response Type

    Select the type of the response from the picklist menu. Available options are.
    • Radio Button (You can set score for each response.)
    • Picklist (You can set score for each response.)
    • Text Box (The weight is automatically set as 0. The score will be set to 0 as well.)
    • Check Box (You can not set any Score for the responses.)

    Existing Topic

    Select the topic of the question from the picklist menu. If a topic is not available then create one by adding the name of the topic.

    New Topic

    Enter the name of the new topic of the question.

    Response

    Enter each alternative of the response list.
  7. Enter Response and Score for each response.
  8. To reorder a response, click the appropriate response and set the appropriate position of the response by click Up or Down button.
  9. Click Add.
  10. Click Save.
  11. To reorder any question, drag a question to the appropriate position.
  12. Click Save.
  13. Click Download to download the RFI questionnaire.

    or

    Click Back to Sourcing Event.
