Strategic Sourcing User Roles

The user roles that are applicable to Strategic Sourcing are as follows:

  • Buyer : An Administrative user whose objective is to buy the requested product or services at the most appropriate cost for value. Also known as, Sourcing Manager. Responsibilities include administrative activities such as creating sourcing event and managing the entire event. There can be multiple sourcing managers for the same sourcing event. The buyer activates the sourcing event. The buyer creates communities for the suppliers.
  • Stakeholder : A user who is an internal member of the organization who participates in the event as an evaluator. Responsibilities include activities such as evaluating the scorecards in a neutral manner. The scorecards include all parameters to evaluate a supplier, as set by the buyer. The Stakeholders are added by the buyer.
  • Supplier : A user whose objective is to provide most competitive quotes and win the sourcing event. A supplier manages the supplier portal with the credentials he receives from the buyer. Responsibilities include activities such as accepting the invitation to the sourcing event, responding to RFIs and RFQs, and interacting with the buyer for any sourcing event-related tasks.
Tip:

Use Case

Tier 1 Mobiles, a mobile phone manufacturing company, procures the hardware from a variety of suppliers and assembles them at their manufacturing unit. Sam will play the role of buyer. Sam is responsible for all activities related to strategic sourcing - creating the sourcing event, preparing for the sourcing event, inviting the suppliers, activating the event. After the suppliers respond to the questionnaire, Sam evaluates the suppliers and selects the best supplier.

Tier 1 Mobiles procures the following products:

  • Mobile Hardware
  • LCD strips
  • Integrated Circuit Boards
  • Speakers
  • Microphones

Hence, a separate sourcing event must be created for each product as the suppliers for these products are different. Only the suppliers that were approved in the Supplier Onboarding process are invited to the sourcing event(s).

The diagram below depicts the various phases of Strategic Sourcing.

  • Preparation -Buyer prepares for sourcing event.
  • Invitation -Buyer invites suppliers to the sourcing event.
  • RFI - Buyer requests for information from suppliers.
  • RFQ - Buyer requests for quotation from shortlisted suppliers.
  • Evaluation - The system evaluates the suppliers on the basis of evaluation weightage.
  • Award - Buyer awards the best supplier the deal.

Your sourcing event can have one of the following statuses.

  • Inactive
  • Active
  • Cancelled
  • On Hold
  • Closed

The Buyer must do following tasks.

  • The buyer must put together the list of questions to be answered by suppliers.
  • The buyer must put together the list of items to be quoted by the suppliers.
  • The buyer must gather feedback from internal stakeholders on the suppliers who submitted responses.
  • The buyer must enforce a fair and precise evaluation process to select the best suppliers.
Warning:

It is mandatory to Add the Suppliers and Create the Milestones before you publish the Sourcing Event. The system will not behave as expected if these are not configured.

It is mandatory to set the Sourcing Event Type before you create a Sourcing Event.
