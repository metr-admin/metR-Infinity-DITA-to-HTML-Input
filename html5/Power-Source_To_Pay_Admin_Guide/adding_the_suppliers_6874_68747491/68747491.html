Adding the Suppliers

A Supplier is the counter-party to the buyer in a sourcing event. A supplier supplies the products or services.

A supplier participates in the sourcing event. The goal of supplier is to win a sourcing event. A supplier can accept or decline the invitation to a sourcing event. After accepting a sourcing event, supplier responds to the questions (RFI and RFQ) provided by you. The supplier provides most competitive quotes to the products and services listed in the sourcing event. The Supplier also provides alternatives to the buyer. Once the event is published, suppliers will be notified via an email about the invitation to the sourcing event.

You can select the suppliers to be invited to the sourcing event. Suppliers with and without portal access can participate in the sourcing event. Now, you can add more than 100 suppliers in a sourcing event. When you search for the suppliers, you can view if the supplier has a Partner Portal Account, Customer Portal Account, or none. You can participate in the sourcing event on behalf of suppliers. When a supplier who does not have a portal access is invited to a sourcing event, the invitation is Auto-Accepted.

Manage Emails

When you have a large number of suppliers, the emails are sent in a batch. The Manage Emails functionality enables you to manage the emails when a large number of suppliers are added in a sourcing event and the entire batch fails because of the Salesforce limit to send emails. Manage Emails functionality is enabled only if the RFI Survey is meant for Non-Portal Suppliers. You can resend the emails to specific suppliers in case the limit is about to end. Refer to Resend Emails for more information. Manage Emails button will be enabled only after the RFI phase has started.

  • To manage emails

Resend Emails

Resend Emails functionality enables you to resend the emails for RFI Survey to any specific suppliers. This feature can be utilized when a batch job fails and it is crucial to send the RFI Survey email to the supplier to respond to. Resend Emails feature is enabled only if the RFI Survey is meant for Non-Portal suppliers. Resend Emails button will be visible under the Action column after RFI phase has started.

To add a supplier

  1. Click the Sourcing Events tab.
  2. Select the sourcing event you want to edit and click Edit.
  3. Click the Preparationtab and click the Suppliers tab.
  4. Click Add.
  5. Enter the relevant Search Criteria details and click Search. or
    Click Search.
    Note:

    Note

    The suppliers that have been registered and added to the system are displayed here. For more information on onboarding suppliers to the system, refer Supplier Onboarding.

    Also, the suppliers having an existing account in the current Salesforce org are displayed.

  6. Select the relevant suppliers from the Search Results section. The Customer Portal Account and Partner Account columns indicate whether the respective supplier has the access to Customer Portal account or Partner Portal account.

    Note:

    Note

    The selected suppliers are displayed in the Selected Results section.

  7. Click Save Selection.
    Note:

    Caution

    Ensure that the suppliers are added to the Suppliers section on the Sourcing Events page. You can select the appropriate contact from the Contact Name picklist. The Portal Access? column depicts if the supplier has portal access.

  8. Under Actions column, click Remove to remove a supplier from the list of suppliers.
  9. Click Save.

To manage emails

  1. Click the Preparation tab.
  2. Click the Suppliers tab.
  3. Click Manage Emails.
    Note:

    Note

    Manage Emails button will be visible only if you have started the RFI phase.

  4. Click Resend for the batch that you want to resend the emails to.

To resend an email

  1. Click the Preparation tab.
  2. Click the Suppliers tab.
  3. Click Resend Email for the supplier that you want to resend email to.
    Note:

    Note

    Resend Emails button will be visible only if you have started the RFI phase.
