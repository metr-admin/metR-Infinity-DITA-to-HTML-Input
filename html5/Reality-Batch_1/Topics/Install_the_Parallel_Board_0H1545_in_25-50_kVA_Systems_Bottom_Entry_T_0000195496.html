Install the Parallel Board 0H-1544 in 25–50 kVA Systems (Bottom Entry)

Note: This is the bottom entry procedure. See Install the Parallel Board 0H-1544 in 25–50 kVA Systems (Top Entry) for the top entry procedure.
DANGER
hazard of electric shock, explosion, or arc flash
  • Electrical equipment must be installed, operated, serviced, and maintained only by qualified personnel.
  • Apply appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) and follow safe electrical work practices.
  • Disconnection devices for AC and DC must be provided by others, be readily accessible, and the function of the disconnect device marked for its function.
  • Turn off all power supplying the UPS system before working on or inside the equipment.
  • Before working on the UPS system, check for hazardous voltage between all terminals including the protective earth.
  • The UPS contains an internal energy source. Hazardous voltage can be present even when disconnected from the mains supply. Before installing or servicing the UPS system, ensure that the units are OFF and that mains and batteries are disconnected. Wait five minutes before opening the UPS to allow the capacitors to discharge.
  • The UPS must be properly earthed/grounded and due to a high leakage current, the earthing/grounding conductor must be connected first.
Note: This procedure is only applicable to 25–50 kVA systems.
Note: The procedure is the same for UPS1 and UPS2 in the parallel system.
  1. Remove the two indicated covers in front of and below the breakers.
  2. Slide out the low voltage assembly far enough to disconnect the cables.
  3. Note the location of the cables and disconnect the cables.
  4. Remove the indicated cover.
  5. Remove the cover in front of the connection box interface.
  6. Tilt the interface assembly outwards.
  7. Remove the indicated metal plate.
  8. Mount the 0P2558 PCB assembly to the bracket using four M4 screws and attach four wire clips to the bracket.
  9. Install the 0P2558 PCB assembly in the UPS using the M6 screw and press the assembly onto the power board flange as illustrated.
  10. Remove the terminators from the lowest power module and reconnect them into the 0P2558 PCB assembly.
    1. Remove the green, yellow and red terminators from the lowest power module.
    2. Discard the red terminator and plug the green terminator into terminal A1 (UPS1) / A2 (UPS2), and the yellow terminator into B1 (UPS1) / B2 (UPS2).
  11. Connect the 0W11666 cables to the original locations of the RJ45 terminators on the power module, and route and connect the cables to the parallel board.
    1. Connect cable P2 (red) to J2006 on the parallel board.
    2. Connect cable P3 (green) to J2002 on the parallel board.
    3. Connect cable P4 (yellow) to J2005 on the parallel board.
  12. Connect the 0W6341 cable (7000 mm long) to the remaining green and yellow terminals.
    1. Connect A2 (green) on UPS1 to A1 (green) on UPS2.
    2. Connect B2 (yellow) on UPS1 to B1 (yellow) on UPS2.
  13. Route the 0W6341 cable behind the inner side plate, out through the grommet in the low voltage channel, and out through the bottom conduit plate to the other UPS.
  14. Reinstall the removed covers, cables and plates in reversed order.
  15. Configure the settings for parallel operation, see Parallel Configuration.
