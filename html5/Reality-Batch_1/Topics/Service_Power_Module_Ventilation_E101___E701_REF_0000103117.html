Power Module Ventilation (E101/E701)

Reality EMEA

The fan module E101 (E102) cools the power module T101 (T102) by forced cooling. The fan module consists of three fans and one fan monitor board (0P2554). For one power module within the block there are only three fans mounted. For two power modules within the block there are six fans mounted. The fan monitoring board can be configured by DIP-switches to enable/disable fans that are not present.

As the fans are monitored individually, it is easy to identify and replace inoperable fans. Because of N + 1 fan redundancy, the fan function continues even if a fan becomes inoperable.

One power module requires three fans. 24/15 VDC ELV fan supply is generated by the PSU from the power module. The fan speed can be increased by increasing the fan supply voltage to 24 VDC.

24 VDC SELV voltage supplies the fan monitoring board.

Fan Monitoring Board

All fan modules are equipped with a speed monitoring board. The fan monitoring board monitors the fan speed and can supply up to six fans. The input signals generated by hall sensors on the connected fans are monitored. These are square wave signals with a duty cycle of 50%. When the fans are operable, the red status LEDs are OFF. If the frequency of an input signal falls below 46 Hz or rises above 167 Hz, the corresponding red status LED is illuminated and the optocoupler output becomes high (inactive).

There are three DIP switches placed on the fan monitoring board:

  • SW1000 consisting of two switches where the output signals of the two fan groups can be either combined or separated.

  • SW1001 and SW1002, each consisting of three switches where the individual fan signals can be either enabled or disabled.
