Palletizer Annual Maintenance

PKR-03-003

Revised: 2021-02-26

Purpose and Application

Purpose

To define a work instruction for performing annual maintenance on a palletizer.

Application

This work instruction applies to all Soul manufacturing facilities with the relevant system.

Safety

Observe all Soul standard safety requirements and procedures defined by the Global Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) organization, as well as all applicable local, regional, and national requirements. Safety equipment and instructions specific to the completion of this work instruction are detailed in the Instructions Section.

Instructions

  1. Before starting the work instruction activity, perform the following steps:
    1. Do not begin these tasks without confirmation that the personnel directly involved have completed the required training associated with the work instruction activity and have reviewed this document.
    2. Gather necessary tools and equipment as identified below in the Equipment Section.
    3. Wear and use any additional Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safety equipment required for this specific work instruction.
    4. Obey all applicable safety requirements and procedures.
    5. Notify the appropriate personnel of the activity and the estimated time needed to complete the tasks.
    6. Follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures when work instruction activity requires isolating energy in the designated area. Visually inspect and confirm that the energy is isolated before starting work.
    7. Only after all energy is verified to be isolated, can the protective guarding be removed and safety interlock gates be opened.
  2. To maintain palletizers, perform the following steps:
    1. Ensure that any outstanding maintenance identified on the monthly maintenance inspection is completed. Refer to PKR-03-001 Palletizer Monthly Maintenance.
      1. If sprockets/pinions show more than 50% tooth wear, replace them.
    2. Visually inspect all chains, sprockets/pinions, and sprocket/pinions take-ups for wear.
    3. Replace batteries in the programmable logic controller (PLC), if applicable.
    4. Measure the current draw on the main drive motor.
      1. If the draw exceeds 80% FLA (full load amps) during normal operation, investigate the cause.
    5. If any values are found to be out of specification, notify the appropriate SAP PM contact to issue a planned work order.
  3. Upon completion of tasks, perform the following steps:
    1. Replace protective guarding and close the safety interlock gates.
    2. Confirm that the designated work area is clear and communicate to personnel the intent to restart energy.
    3. After the designated work area is confirmed clear and personnel are aware of the pending restart, follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures to restart energy supplied to the designated area. Visually inspect that the energy is active.
    4. Perform basic housekeeping duties. Clean up the work space, tools, and equipment. Dispose of trash. Put tools and equipment in their assigned areas.
    5. Notify the appropriate personnel that the work instruction activity has been completed
    6. Record and report findings and results.
    7. Follow appropriate instructions for the notification of findings and results relating to specifications, targets, and/or reaction limits.

Required Training

Before performing this work instruction, applicable personnel must be trained by an authorized trainer on the required training contents listed in this document.
