HE Treatment Hood Annual Maintenance

HEP-03-002

Revised: 2021-02-12

Purpose and Application

Purpose

To define a work instruction for performing annual maintenance on a hot end treatment hood.

Application

This work instruction applies to all Soul manufacturing facilities with the relevant system.

Safety

Observe Soul standard safety requirements and procedures defined by the Global Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) organization, as well as all applicable local, regional, and national requirements. Safety equipment and instructions specific to the completion of this work instruction are detailed in the Instructions Section.

Instructions

  1. Before starting the work instruction activity, perform the following steps:
    1. Do not begin these tasks without confirmation that the personnel directly involved have completed the required training associated with the work instruction activity and have reviewed this document.
    2. Gather necessary tools and equipment as identified below in the Equipment Section.
    3. Wear and use any additional Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safety equipment required for this specific work instruction.
    4. Obey all applicable safety requirements and procedures.
    5. Notify the appropriate personnel of the activity and the estimated time needed to complete the tasks.
    6. Clearly identify, mark off, and secure the designated area defined by the work instruction activity.
    7. Follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures when work instruction activity requires isolating energy in the designated area. Verify energy is isolated before starting work.
    8. Only after energy is verified to be isolated can the protective guarding be removed and the safety interlock gates be opened.
  2. To maintain hot end treatment hoods, perform the following steps:
    1. Ensure that any outstanding maintenance identified on the weekly maintenance inspection is completed. Refer to HEP-03-001 HE Treatment Hood Weekly Maintenance.
    2. If applicable, replace seals and the pump head module.
    3. Replace tubing and fittings as required.
    4. It is recommended (but not required) to paint the exterior and interior of the hood with high-temperature paint.
    5. If applicable, calibrate the dosing pump.
    6. If applicable, inspect the radiator for leaks.
    7. If anything is found to be out of specification, notify the appropriate SAP PM contact to issue a planned work order.
  3. Upon completion of tasks, perform the following steps:
    1. Replace any protective guarding and close the safety interlock gates.
    2. Confirm that the designated work area is clear and communicate to personnel the intent to restart energy.
    3. After the designated work area is confirmed clear and all personnel are aware of the pending restart, follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures to restart the energy supplied to the designated area. Verify that the energy is active.
    4. Remove signs and markings from the designated work area.
    5. Perform basic housekeeping duties. Clean up the work space, tools, and equipment. Dispose of trash. Put tools and equipment in their assigned areas.
    6. Notify the appropriate personnel that the work instruction activity has been completed
    7. Record and report findings and results.
    8. Follow appropriate instructions for the notification of findings and results relating to specifications, targets, and/or reaction limits.

Required Training

Before performing this work instruction, applicable personnel must be trained by an authorized trainer on the required training contents listed in this document.
