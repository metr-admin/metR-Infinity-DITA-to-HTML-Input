Batch Systems Maintenance

Purpose

To define a procedure that applies to all plants for all personnel performing maintenance on a batch delivery system.

Identification

Batch systems consist of raw material collection areas, silos, bucket elevators, control system, gathering belts, weighing system, mixer, water delivery, vibratory feeder, delivery conveyors, hoppers, and batch chargers.

Procedure

  1. Each plant shall identify critical batch equipment within their factory, including but not limited to the following:
    1. Batch conveyors such as raw material conveyors, gathering conveyors, mixed batch conveyors
    2. Batch elevator such as raw material elevators or cullet conveyors
    3. Batch mixer
    4. Batch charger
    5. Batch transfer equipment such as vibratory conveyors, screw conveyors, pneumatic conveyors, diverter gates
    6. Batch control systems
    7. Batch house load cell(s)
  2. Each plant shall establish critical operational parameters according to MSDR BAT-07-041 Batch Systems Maintenance and the f raw material collection areas, silos, bucket elevators, control system, gathering belts, weighing system, mixer, water delivery, vibratory feeder, delivery conveyors, hoppers, and batch chargers.
    1. BAT-03-016 Batch Mixer Monthly Maintenance
    2. BAT-03-031 Critical Load Cell Monthly Maintenance
    3. BAT-03-032 Critical Load Cell Calibration
    4. BAT-03-038 Batch House Scale Validation
    5. FUR-03-051 Oscillating Batch Charger Monthly Maintenance
    6. FUR-03-053 Vibrating Pan Batch Charger Monthly Maintenance
    7. FUR-03-055 Pusher Batch Charger Monthly Maintenance
  3. Each plant shall establish ranges or set points for each critical operational parameter.
    1. Plant must use manufacturer’s specifications and/or history to establish ranges or set points.
    2. Ranges must be clearly documented and/or displayed.
  4. Maintenance personnel shall monitor and record current readings of the critical operational parameters at the frequency defined in MSDR BAT-07-041 Batch Systems Maintenance and the following work instructions:
    1. BAT-03-016 Batch Mixer Monthly Maintenance
    2. BAT-03-031 Critical Load Cell Monthly Maintenance
    3. BAT-03-032 Critical Load Cell Calibration
    4. BAT-03-038 Batch House Scale Validation
    5. FUR-03-051 Oscillating Batch Charger Monthly Maintenance
    6. FUR-03-053 Vibrating Pan Batch Charger Monthly Maintenance
    7. FUR-03-055 Pusher Batch Charger Monthly Maintenance
  5. Maintenance personnel shall compare current readings to prior records, looking for:
    1. Readings outside of established operational range requiring immediate action
    2. Trend of operational parameters requiring analysis and further investigation
  6. Readings outside of established operational range or noticeable trends shall be reported to Plant Engineer and/or Maintenance Leader.

  7. Maintenance personnel shall perform maintenance tasks on batch systems as specified in relevant work instructions or manufacturer’s manual and at the frequency specified in MSDR BAT-07-041 Batch Systems Maintenance and the following work instructions:

    1. BAT-03-010 Bucket Elevator Monthly Inspection
    2. BAT-03-011 Bucket Elevator Annual Maintenance
    3. BAT-03-012 Conveyor Monthly Maintenance
    4. BAT-03-013 Conveyor Annual Maintenance
    5. BAT-03-016 Batch Mixer Monthly Maintenance
    6. BAT-03-017 Batch Mixer Annual Maintenance
    7. BAT-03-031 Critical Load Cell Monthly Maintenance
    8. BAT-03-032 Critical Load Cell Calibration
    9. BAT-03-038 Batch House Scale Validation
    10. FUR-03-051 Oscillating Batch Charger Monthly Maintenance
    11. FUR-03-052 Oscillating Batch Charger Annual Maintenance
    12. FUR-03-053 Vibrating Pan Batch Charger Monthly Maintenance
    13. FUR-03-055 Pusher Batch Charger Monthly Maintenance
    14. BAT-03-018 Batch Mixer Transmission Annual Maintenance
  8. Maintenance personnel shall ensure housekeeping is performed on critical batch equipment.
  9. All personnel working on the batch system shall notify the Batch & Furnace Manager and/or their direct supervisor before performing any activities on the system or ancilllary equipment.
  10. All critical spares for critical batch equipment identified in MSDR BAT-07-041 Batch Systems Maintenance shall be maintained in inventory or a strategy established to minimize downtime.
    1. Critical spare strategies may include shared inventory with nearby plant, available for rental, functional redundancy is installed or a strategy is developed with a supplier to receive the critical spares parts in a reasonable amount of time.
    2. Each critical spare part must have a listing of corresponding SAP part numbers. If SAP part numbers are not available, the list must include the drawing number, or the make, model, and part number.
