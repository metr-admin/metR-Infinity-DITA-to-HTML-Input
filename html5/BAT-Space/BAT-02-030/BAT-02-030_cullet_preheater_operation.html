Cullet Preheater Operation

Purpose

To define a procedure that applies to all plants for proper operation of the cullet preheater system.

Procedure

  1. Plant or Third Party personnel shall follow and be familiar with Procedure FUR-02-001 Furnace Safety.
  2. Plant or Third Party personnel shall be trained according to Training Material BAT-04-030 Cullet Preheater Operation and Maintenance Manual – v6, BAT-04-031 Cullet Preheater Operation and Maintenance Manual – v6.1, or BAT-04-032 Cullet Preheater Operation and Maintenance Manual – v2.
  3. The cullet preheater shall only be operated when sufficient cullet is available, as specified in MSDR BAT-07-030 Cullet Preheater Operation Conditions.
  4. The cullet preheater shall shut down automatically if any condition specified within MSDR BAT-07-030 Cullet Preheater Operation Conditions is exceeded.
    1. Abort conditions must be set during commissioning.
  5. Personnel shall start and heat up the cullet preheater as specified in either Work Instruction BAT-03-002 Cullet Preheater Cold Startup – v2, Work Instruction BAT-03-006 Cullet Preheater Cold Startup – v6, or Work Instruction BAT-03-023 Cullet Preheater Cold Startup – v6.1 or using the automatic startup process built into the software.
    1. Personnel shall complete Form BAT-02-030-F 001 Cullet Preheater Cold Commissioning Checklist for each cullet preheater if the preheater has been off for one month or longer.
  6. Personnel shall restart the cullet preheater as specified in either Work Instruction BAT-03-001 Cullet Preheater Hot Restart – v2, Work Instruction BAT-03-005 Cullet Preheater Hot Restart – v6, or Work Instruction BAT-03-022 Cullet Preheater Hot Restart – v6.1 or using the automatic startup process built into the software.
  7. Personnel shall turn off and cool down the cullet preheater as specified in either Work Instruction BAT-03-004 Cullet Preheater Slow Shutdown – v2, or Work Instruction BAT-03-008 Cullet Preheater Slow Shutdown – v6, or Work Instruction BAT-03-025 Cullet Preheater Slow Shutdown – v6.1 or using the automatic shutdown process built into the software.
  8. In emergency situations, personnel shall turn off and cool down the cullet preheater as specified in either Work Instruction BAT-03-003 Cullet Preheater Fast Shutdown – v2, Work Instruction BAT-03-007 Cullet Preheater Fast Shutdown – v6, or Work Instruction BAT-03-024 Cullet Preheater Fast Shutdown – v6.1.
    1. During an abort shutdown, a temporary pressure increase may be experienced in the furnace and cause sting outs until the exhaust system controls stabilize.
  9. In the event of a system shutdown, personnel shall follow the following steps:
    1. Assess if the system can be restarted.
    2. Restart if possible.
    3. If the system cannot be restarted, contact Batch & Furnace Manager to perform actions required to adjust furnace setup for lower inlet batch/cullet temperature.
  10. During off-line operation, personnel shall maintain a positive pressure in the mixing chamber to ensure hot exhaust gases do not enter the cullet preheater system.
  11. All alarms shall be acknowledged and immediately addressed. If necessary, take action to resolve the issue.
  12. Inspection ports shall not be opened during on-line (hot) operation unless following the method specified in Work Instruction BAT-03-009 Cullet Preheater Cleaning.
  13. Only authorized personnel shall manually override automatic valves.
  14. Batch material shall not be run through the cullet preheater when the cullet preheater is hot.
