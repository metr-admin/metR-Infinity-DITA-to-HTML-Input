Batch House Operation

Purpose

To develop a procedure that applies to all plants for proper operation of the Batch House.

Procedure

  1. Each Plant shall designate a person responsible for the Batch House. In the majority of cases, this person is the Batch & Furnace Manager.
  2. The person responsible for the Batch House shall comply with the Roles & Responsibilities associated with the Batch House, as defined in Procedure FUR-02-022 Batch and Furnace Manager Responsibilities.
    1. The Batch & Furnace Manager shall be trained on how to properly forecast raw materials consumption and inventory control, taking under consideration production demand, seasonal requirements, transit times, etc.
  3. Batch House personnel shall comply with the Roles & Responsibilities defined in Procedure BAT-02-021 Batch House Personnel Responsibilities and be trained for the position in every activity that needs to be performed.
  4. The plant shall have an asset management plan in place for the Batch House to ensure that the preventive maintenance is up to date and the equipment is working properly
  5. The plant shall ensure:
    1. ENVIRONMENTAL, HEALTH AND SAFETY:
      1. Batch House operation shall be in compliance with Procedure BAT-02-003 Batch House Environmental, Health and Safety.
      2. All Batch House personnel shall wear the correct PPE as defined by the local EHS department.
      3. All equipment has the appropriate guarding according to Procedure EHS-02-122 Machine Guarding Program, and the area is well lit.
      4. If used, storage racks in the Batch House shall comply with local regulations for capacity.
    2. HOUSEKEEPING:
      1. Proper Housekeeping routines are in place according to Procedure BAT-02-004 Batch House Housekeeping. All equipment labeling shall be visible, and the scales and equipment shall be free of material accumulation and leaks. The housekeeping routines shall include, but not be limited to:
        1. Cleaning schedule.
        2. Installation and use of industrial vacuum (high efficiency) or similar.
        3. Dust collectors.
      2. Processes shall be in place to properly dispose of any material, such as mixed batch or raw materials (including minors) according to local regulations.
    3. BATCH SYSTEM:
      1. Raw materials silos and scales, and if applicable pneumatic receiving system, are clearly labeled and the labels match the material currently being stored/weighed that correspond to the Batch House control system/panel.
      2. The plant shall have a written procedure to define how to safely perform manual weighing which is accessible in the Batch House control room. Also, all operators in charge of the Batch House shall be trained in manual weighing and given refresher training at least once per year.
      3. All scales are in good working order (seals in good condition, free of accumulated material, fit for purpose) and maintenance is up to date.
      4. Procedures shall be in place to ensure that the correct functioning of the Batch House weighing system is verified following any upgrades or changes to the control system.
      5. All gates (raw material exits) shall be visually inspected weekly to ensure proper functioning.
      6. The plant shall have a written procedures to define how to respond to Batch House technical issues which is accessible in the Batch House control room. Also, all operators in charge of the Batch House shall be trained in responding to technical issues and given refresher training at least once per year.
      7. The response plan to Batch House technical issues should include, but it is not limited to:
        1. Scale alarms: Over/Under weighment, weigh time exceeded, tare weight off… etc.
        2. *Critical equipment blockages, breakdowns, alarms.
        3. How to restart the system after a power failure.
        4. How to discharge a wrong batch.
        5. How to use and start a backup system to feed batch to the Furnace.
        6. Silo Full alarms, rotation control alarms, belt alignment alarms, chute blockage alarms.

          *Critical equipment: Mixer, elevators, conveyors.

          All of these procedures need to address safety for the operator and for the process to avoid accidents/injuries, weighing errors, cross contaminations, damage to critical equipment, down times and interruptions of the furnace(s).

          It is not responsible to acknowledge an alarm without prior investigation that it is safe to do so, and the problem has been diagnosed. Never remove device protection sensors!

      8. Belt cleaning devices (brushes, scraper, etc.) shall be installed to avoid batch build-up on the conveyor and set correctly to ensure effective belt cleaning with no belt damage. Inspection and maintenance of these devices shall be performed weekly.
      9. When replacing a new conveyor, belt scrapers shall also be renewed to prevent immediate damage.
      10. The alarms for the Batch House weighing system as they relate to the raw material weight tolerances should be set according to the weight tolerance recommendations defined by Regional Glass Science.
      11. The plant should react proactively if any changes in the ambient conditions occur which could affect the proper functioning of Batch House weighing system (humidity, moisture in raw materials, etc.).
      12. It is recommended that a daily batch count reconciliation be performed to ensure the proper batch weight.
    4. MIXER:
      1. The plant shall have proper procedures in place to ensure the mixer is cleaned and inspected on a weekly basis by trained personnel.
      2. The mixer discharge timing shall be sufficient to remove as much material as possible from the mixer between batches.
      3. All gates that control material flow into and out of the mixer are working as intended (no leaks).
      4. For plants with at least 2 furnaces using different compositions, the purge time of the transfer equipment, after the mixer, shall be sufficient to avoid cross contamination between batches.
    5. BATCH DELIVERY:
      1. For plants with Horizontal Batch Houses, the raw materials shall be properly synchronized prior to delivery to the mixer to maximize layering (sandwiching) and mixer utilization.
      2. The homogeneity of the batch shall be visually inspected on a daily basis.
      3. The plant shall have good control of mixed batch synchronization with cullet to maximize layering (sandwiching).
      4. The Batch and Furnace manager shall ensure that the day bin level variations are kept at a minimum, and that the levels are kept as high as possible to avoid segregation and mitigate against breakdowns.
    6. RAW MATERIALS:
      1. All raw materials silos shall be capable of use (no unusable silos due to reasons such as poor silo integrity or being filled with unusable, contaminated, or obsolete materials).
      2. Raw material silos shall be measured daily as described in Work Instruction BAT-03-039 Raw Material Silo – Manual Measurement.
      3. Batch House silos shall be inspected internally and externally once per year to ensure the integrity of the structure is in good condition. This inspection shall be performed and documented according to Work Instruction BAT-03-040 Raw Material Silo Inspection.
      4. The Batch and Furnace Manager should practice the First In, First Out (FIFO) philosophy for the consumption of the minor raw materials.
      5. The plant should keep the minor raw materials dry.
