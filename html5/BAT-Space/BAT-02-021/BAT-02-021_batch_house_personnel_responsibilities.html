Batch House Personnel Responsibilities

Purpose

To define a procedure that applies to all plants to specify the responsibilities and tasks to be performed by Batch House personnel.

Procedure

  1. Batch House operations shall be organized and staffed in a manner capable of protecting the health and safety of Batch House personnel, protecting the Batch House asset, supporting production needs, producing quality mixed batch output, and respecting environmental regulations.
    1. The Plant Manager is accountable for implementing the Batch House specific organization for all Batch House personnel.
  2. Batch House personnel shall be in compliance with all requirements defined in Procedure BAT-02-003 Batch House Environmental, Health and Safety.
  3. Batch House personnel shall perform work in the following areas including, but not limited to the following:
    1. Raw material receiving
    2. Monitor batch and cullet quality (moisture testing)
    3. Raw material storage (silo measurement and inspection)
    4. Batch weighing
    5. Minors handling, storage, weighing and mixing
    6. Batch mixing
    7. Mixed batch delivery
    8. Day bin measurement and inspection
    9. Purchased Cullet Management
    10. In-House Cullet Management
    11. Monitoring housekeeping and escalating issues
  4. All Batch House personnel shall monitor and maintain the performance aspects of the Batch House operations including, but not limited to the following:
    1. Raw material receiving
      1. Verify that each load of raw material delivered to the plant:
        1. Was ordered by the plant.
        2. Is free of contamination.
      2. Perform quality tests of each raw material as described in Procedure RAW-02-002 Raw Material Inspection, Receiving and Control.
      3. Take a representative sample of each material received and retain for the time period specified in Procedure RAW-02-002 Raw Material Inspection, Receiving and Control.
    2. Raw material storage
      1. All silos/bunkers must be visibly and properly identified.
      2. Ensure that each load of raw material is unloaded in the correct silo / bunker.
      3. Ensure the use of raw materials with the First In, First Out (FIFO) philosophy where possible.
    3. Batch weighing
      1. Control batch weights to meet batch formula specifications.
      2. Validate scale calibration at the frequency specified in MSDR BAT-02-001 Batch House Scales Calibration and Validation Requirements.
    4. Minors handling, storage, weighing and mixing
      1. Store minor raw materials in clean, safe, and designated area.
      2. Ensure the use of minor raw materials with the First In, First Out (FIFO) philosophy where possible.
      3. Weigh and mix minor raw materials in a safe manner according to the batch recipes provided by Glass Science.
        1. Validate scale calibration at the frequency specified in MSDR BAT-02-001 Batch House Scales Calibration and Validation Requirements.
    5. Batch Mixing
      1. Visually inspect to ensure a homogeneous mix.
    6. Mixed Batch Delivery
      1. Ensure mixed batch delivery to furnace with minimal segregation and loss.
      2. Maintain enough mixed batch to satisfy all furnaces within the plant.
    7. Purchased Cullet Management
      1. Verify that each load of cullet delivered to the plant:
        1. Was ordered by the plant.
        2. Ensure cullet complies with specifications defined in MSDR RAW-07-001 Cullet Specification and Control.
      2. Ensure loads of cullet are separated and stored by type, color and supplier (if possible) in bunkers or silos designed for handling cullet.
      3. Ensure the use of cullet with the First In, First Out (FIFO) philosophy where possible.
      4. Ensure cullet is stored in the proper silo.
      5. Ensure cullet is weighed to the batch recipe prescribed by Glass Science.
      6. Ensure cullet and mixed batch are combined and delivered to furnace with minimal segregation and loss.
    8. In-House Cullet Management
      1. Ensure equipment to recover rejected hot bottles, gobs, streamed glass and rejected cold containers are functioning correctly. This includes, but is not limited to:
        1. Conveyors, crushers, bins, fork-lifts, quench conveyors, metallic separation, etc.
      2. Ensure in-house cullet is separated by color and from external cullet.
      3. Ensure the use of cullet with the First In, First Out (FIFO) philosophy where possible.
      4. Ensure cullet is stored in the proper silo.
      5. Ensure in-house cullet is weighed to the batch recipe prescribed by Glass Science.
      6. Ensure mixed cullet and mixed batch are combined and delivered to furnace with minimal segregation and loss.
    9. Monitor and maintain a record of the raw materials received.
    10. Track raw materials consumption, and inventories.
    11. Based on timely inspections, notify maintenance of all necessary repairs.
    12. Perform housekeeping and organizational duties for the batch house as defined in Procedure BAT-02-004 Batch House Housekeeping.
    13. Operate daily to accomplish the following requirements including, but not limited to:
      1. Execute tasks promptly and focus on most critical aspects of the operation.
        1. Notify manager of tasks that require additional assistance or guidance.
        2. Act if corrective actions are required to maintain stable operations.
      2. Complete the required training as defined in Procedure BAT-02-010 Batch House Personnel Training.
      3. Escalate needs and improvement ideas to supervisor.
  5. Batch House personnel shall actively communicate conditions, operational parameters and needs of the batch house operation to the following individuals including, but not limited to:
    1. Batch & Furnace Manager
    2. Other Batch House personnel:
      1. Activities related to prior shift
        1. Review previous shift data in the daily log book for reported problems.
        2. Significant problems experienced.
      2. Tasks specifically related to the beginning of the shift.
  6. Batch House personnel shall be familiar with the Global Manufacturing Fundamentals as it relates to the batch and furnace area including, but not limited to:
    1. Policies
    2. Procedures
    3. Work Instructions
    4. Training Materials and Videos
    5. Master Specification and Design Requirement (MSDR)
    6. Forms
