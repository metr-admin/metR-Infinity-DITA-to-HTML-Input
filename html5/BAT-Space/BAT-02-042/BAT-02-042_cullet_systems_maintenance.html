Cullet Systems Maintenance

Purpose

To define a procedure that applies to all plants for all personnel performing maintenance on a cullet system.

Identification

Cullet systems consist of raw material collection areas, cullet crusher, silos, bucket elevators, control system, gathering belts, weighting system, mixer, vibratory feeder, and delivery conveyors.

Procedure

  1. Each plant shall identify critical cullet system equipment within their factory, including but not limited to the following: Bucket elevators, processing conveyors, crushers, basement cullet removal systems, cullet hoppers, cullet chutes and vibratory feeders.
  2. Maintenance personnel shall perform maintenance tasks on cullet systems as specified in relevant work instructions or manufacturer’s manual and at the frequency specified in MSDR BAT-07-042 Cullet Systems Maintenance and the following work instructions:
    1. BAT-03-010 Bucket Elevator Monthly Inspection
    2. BAT-03-011 Bucket Elevator Annual Maintenance
    3. BAT-03-012 Conveyor Monthly Maintenance
    4. BAT-03-013 Conveyor Annual Maintenance
    5. BAT-03-014 Cullet Processing Crusher Monthly Maintenance
    6. BAT-03-015 Cullet Processing Crusher Annual Maintenance
    7. BAT-03-031 Critical Load Cells Monthly Maintenance
    8. BAT-03-032 Critical Load Cells Calibration
    9. BAT-03-033 Cullet Magnet Daily Maintenance
    10. BAT-03-034 Cullet Magnet Annual Maintenance
  3. Maintenance personnel shall ensure housekeeping is performed on critical cullet equipment.
  4. Maintenance personnel shall perform maintenance task on scraper conveyor, if the plant have them, as specified in relevant work instructions or manufacturer’s manual and at the frequency specified in MSDR BAT-07-042 Cullet Systems Maintenance and the following work instructions:
    1. BAT-03-035 Scraper Conveyor Monthly Maintenance
    2. BAT-03-036 Scraper Conveyor Six Month Maintenance
    3. BAT-03-037 Scraper Conveyor Gear Box Oil Change
  5. All personnel working on the cullet system shall notify their direct supervisor before performing any activities on the system or ancillary equipment.
  6. All critical spares for critical electrical equipment identified in MSDR BAT-07-042 Cullet Systems Maintenance shall be maintained in inventory or a strategy established to minimize downtime.
    1. Critical spare strategies may include shared inventory with nearby plant, available for rental, functional redundancy is installed or a strategy is developed with a supplier to receive the critical spares parts in a reasonable amount of time.
    2. Each critical spare part must have a listing of corresponding SAP part numbers. If SAP part numbers are not available, the list must include the drawing number, or the make, model, and part number.
