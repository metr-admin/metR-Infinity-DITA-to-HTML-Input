Collaborating Using PowerPoint

Working in PowerPoint, you can add comments to your Chatter feed or share entire presentations.

Parent topic: Sharing Options

To Collaborate Using PowerPoint

  1. Create a new presentation or open an existing presentation in Word.
  2. Connect to X-Author Chatter as described in Connecting to X-Author Chatter Online.
  3. Select who you want to collaborate with in your Chatter feed by clicking one of the icons:

    /Chatter icon/

    Your audience will be everyone in your organization with a Chatter account.

    /Group icon/

    Your audience will be all the members of the group you select.

    /Individual icon/

    Your audience will be the single individual you select.
  4. Make changes to the presentation using PowerPoint and then choose what content to share.

    /screenshot of chatter window and drop-down/

    Post

    Comments you provide in Chatter are included, but no part of the presentation is included.

    Selected Content

    The content highlighted in your presentation is inserted into the Chatter feed, as unformatted text. There is a maximum of 1000 characters. If you select images or multiple objects, it is inserted as a .png file.

    (Not currently supported. Choosing this option shares your Comments to the feed and the entire presentation is attached to the Chatter feed.)

    Selected Slides

    You can select any whole slides in any sequence from the Slides tab in PowerPoint and they are attached to the feed entry as a single .ppt file. The slides are placed in the order in which you select them. The feed entry indicates which slides were included in the .ppt file. The selected slides will be exactly like they are in the original .ppt, except auto-numbering may be different.

    (Not currently supported. Choosing this option shares your Comments to the feed and the entire presentation is attached to the Chatter feed.)

    Entire Presentation

    The whole PowerPoint presentation is attached to the feed.
  5. Click Share to share your content to the Chatter feed.
