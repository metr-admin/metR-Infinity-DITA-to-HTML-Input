Working at Heights Rescue Plan

Purpose

  1. The purpose of this document is to provide guidance on retrieval of employees in the event a fall has occurred when working at heights (e.g., roofs, inside buildings, etc.).
  2. After a fall, the worker remains suspended in the air and will need to be rescued by others. It is important to the safety of the employee for a quick retrieval to occur to prevent any further injury to the employee. Leaving a worker suspended for a long period of time can be dangerous to their health and safety. In other situations, the employee could have injuries that require first-aid or medical assistance. A rescue plan will detail how to return fallen workers to a place of safety while keeping rescuers safe.

Definitions

Fallen employee
Employee suspended in the air after a fall from height.
Rescuer
Individual having the skills, knowledge and training to perform a rescue.
Ground worker
Employee coordinating the rescue with a rescuer, emergency service and the fallen employee.
Rescue equipment
Any equipment used to evacuate a fallen employee being suspended.
Rescue kit
A kit designed to rescue a person left suspended after a fall from height, without the need for extensive training.
Suspension trauma
The natural physiological response to the human body being held motionless in a vertical position for a period of time, resulting in presyncopal symptoms or loss of consciousness. Prolonged effects of suspension trauma:
  1. Leg circulation (straps impede blood remuscle venous pump fails, blood becomes toxic and highly acidic).
  2. Heart circulation (anxiety and onset of shock, heart rate increases, cardiac irritability).
  3. Brain circulation (diminished blood flow, blocked airway, cardiac arrest or brain damage, death).

Develop Risk Assessment and Rescue Plan for Working at Heights Tasks

  1. During preparation phase for Work at Height and risk assessment, a specific rescue plan must be defined and appropriate work permits issued as per EHS-02-103 F003 Working at Height Permit.
  2. For the purpose of Work at Height Rescue risk assessment, the below elements have to be taken into consideration:
    1. Type and extent of the hazards for the people involved in the rescue.
    2. Definition of the required properties of the rescue equipment.
    3. Determination of the appropriate rescue procedure:
      1. Active Rescue (Rescuer person can be lowered together with the Fallen Employee).
      2. Passive Rescue (Rescuer stands on a safe level).
    4. Determination of the appropriate rescue equipment (belts, loops, lifting devices, descenders, connecting elements, anchor devices, cherry pickers, etc.).
    5. Determination of the rescue routes.
  3. Assessment and selection of rescue equipment shall involve:
    1. Suitability for the given conditions.
    2. Suitability according to ergonomic requirements.
  4. Ensure that the rescue concept is understood and agreed with: area managers/superiors and teammates as well as the plant fire brigade if applicable.
  5. Make sure there are contact numbers for outside rescue services (e.g., Local Fire Department) available and known.
  6. Confirm that Rescuer(s) are physically fit and have extensive knowledge of the equipment and practical experience.

Review of Rescue Training

  1. Verify the Rescuer(s) have received theoretical and practical instruction of the rescuing procedure.
  2. Designated Rescuers must be adequately trained and have easy access to all the equipment they need to effectively rescue others safely and as quickly as possible.
  3. Employees shall work in teams: if they work alone, it will not be possible for a rescue procedure to be carried out on time. Make sure that someone is always in the condition to call the emergency contact during the entire duration of the work.

Review of Rescue Equipment Requirements and Inspections

  1. Preparation is key: establish suitable attachment points, prevent unintentional loosening, verify that there is rescue equipment and/or rescue kit at the point of work prior to the work commencing. Failure to stage the equipment prior to the task beginning, could result in further injury to the employee. It is critical to have the equipment ready in order to promptly begin the rescue efforts.
  2. Use of approved rescue equipment which has to be present at the site:
    1. Relevant CE/ANSI marking
    2. Declaration of Conformity
    3. Instructions for use and maintenance

Review of Types of Rescue

  1. There are 4 types of rescue:
    1. Lowering a remote casualty
    2. Raising a remote casualty
    3. Self-evacuation by descent
    4. Rescue another in descent
  2. It is preferable that the Rescuer does not descend and is not suspended while rescuing someone else, so that further complications are avoided. The Rescuer should not endanger themselves when carrying out a rescue procedure.
  3. Consider all limitations of the rescue plan for adverse weather (wind, lightning, snow etc.). In any case, Working at Heights should be avoided in case of strong wing, rain, ice and snow.

Review of Rescue Plan and Actions to be Taken

  1. When a fall happens, another employee (Rescuer who has been previously trained in rescue procedures and rescue equipment handling) needs to assess the situation and make contact with the fallen worker, determining their status to consider if the fallen employee can self-rescue or not. If not, they check if the fallen person is conscious or not. If not conscious, they call the external Emergency service before starting the rescuing procedure.
    1. WARNING: If a person hangs motionless for longer periods (10-20 minutes) in the full body harness, health risks can arise (hanging trauma: orthostatic shock, danger to life).
  2. In the meantime, the emergency services will need to be contacted and the company alerted about the accident.
  3. Rescuer performs a visual and functional check of rescue equipment before use.
  4. Rescuer or another person has to clear and secure work areas where the rescue will take place, while they remain safe or if any ongoing work would obstruct a rescue. 
  5. The next step is to immediately implement the rescue procedure in accordance with the company policy and safety guidelines.
  6. Consider usage of rescue kits which for example allow you to either lower the person to the ground or lift them up, depending on the situation. Make sure that the rescue kit is present at the point of work prior to the work commencing.
  7. If someone has fallen over an edge, Rescuer needs to consider the additional friction encountered when trying to raise the fallen worker, the anchor line being at a risk of cutting and generally be aware of the edge interfering with the rescue equipment.
  8. If an immediate rescue solution is not available, then try to support the fallen worker’s feet to make the restraint a bit more bearable.

Rescue Equipment

  1. Depending on the type of work that is carried out, as well as the height of the building/room or site specific circumstances, you might need to use rescue equipment as simple as a ladder or as complicated as a crane. In some situations, you may have to consider MEWPs (mobile elevating work platforms), man-riding baskets for cranes or proprietary rescue systems.
  2. In any case, ensure that the equipment that you will use for a rescue are properly serviced before being put to use. Do not use the equipment for purposes other than which it was intended.
  3. All the rescue equipment shall be inspected at least once a year by competent person.
  4. The site will also need to keep records of previous inspections, maintenance and equipment history.

Review of First Aid Actions to be Taken

  1. Check symptoms of hanging trauma by a trained First Aid Responder:
    1. Paleness
    2. Sweating
    3. Shortness of breath
    4. Increase in pulse and blood pressure
    5. Visual impairment
    6. Dizziness
    7. Nausea
    8. Later drop in pulse and blood pressure
  2. If there is no massive bleeding, the person is not unconscious and there is no respiratory arrest, the patient should first be positioned with the upper body raised and legs drawn up in a squatting or crouching position.
  3. First move slowly into flat storage.
  4. If necessary, provide medical treatment.
  5. If there are no signs of hanging trauma after the rescue, the normal first aid measures should be carried out.

Post Incident Review

  1. After each Rescue, perform the analysis and prepare the Rescue Report as per EHS-02-004-F001 Working at Heights Rescue Procedure Report.
  2. Rescue equipment that is damaged or loaded by a fall must be withdrawn from use unless a competent person (or manufacturer instructions indicate to remove from service) has given permission for further use.
  3. Review and amend the rescue plan on a regular schedule, after relevant changes to the worksite, and after all rescues or related incidents.
  4. Perform refresher training for a rescue procedure once a year.

Annual Training Requirements

  1. Everyone involved in working at height and rescue procedures should be trained on fall protection, rescue equipment and procedures:
    1. How to inspect, anchor, assemble and use fall protection and rescue equipment safely.
    2. How to recognise fall hazards.
    3. How to implement fall hazard control methods.
    4. How to conduct fall protection and rescue procedures.
    5. How to inspect equipment and systems before use.
  2. The authorized Rescuer must be trained by a competent rescuer trainer and then retrained should the nature of his work change, or if there are other changes in circumstances. The Rescuer must also be evaluated by a competent trainer at least once annually.
  3. All training needs to be well documented; documents need to be archived for at least 3 years, depending on local regulations.
