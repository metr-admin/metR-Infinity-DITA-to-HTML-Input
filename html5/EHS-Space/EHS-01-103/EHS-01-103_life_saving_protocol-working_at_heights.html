Life Saving Protocol - Working at Heights

Purpose

Space has developed a set of mandatory Life Saving Protocols that set a minimum standard and minimum performance expectations for managing high-level hazards common to the glass industry.

Scope

  1. This protocol applies to all Space employees, contractors and visitors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities, technical centers, offices & administrative buildings. All sites in all regions are expected to develop site specific procedures to comply with this protocol.
  2. Requirements of this protocol will apply to the control of risks associated with working at heights. Working at heights can include tasks where activity is carried out on any structure or plant being constructed or installed, demolished or dismantled, inspected, tested, repaired or cleaned, on a fragile surface(roofs), on a potentially unstable surface, using equipment to work at the elevated level (elevating work platforms or portable ladders) on a sloping or slippery surface where it is difficult for people to maintain their balance, near an unprotected open edge (near incomplete stairwells), and near a hole, shaft(trenches, service pits).

Process Requirements

  1. A formal and documented risk assessment must be completed prior to undertaking any work from heights. Working at heights should be avoided and only conducted if it is the only practicable option.
  2. Guard rails and toe boards shall be installed as per the local legislative requirements on all walkways and platforms.
  3. Where the potential exists for an employee to fall at an unprotected height according to regional legislative requirements, personal fall arrest devices must be used.
  4. A formal training program for all fall protection and fall arrest devices are required.
  5. Employees using personal lifting devices (e.g. a manlift, scissor lift, mobile elevated platform) shall be suitably trained and shall tie off at all times in such devices.
  6. A maintenance and inspection program is implemented at the site for ladders, scaffolding, mobile elevated work platform, and all fall protection and fall arrest equipment.
  7. Working from heights permit documentation must be completed and signed off by a trained person prior to any work at heights activity being undertaken.
  8. All floor openings (pits, sumps, wells, etc.) must be protected with a standard railing unless covered with permanently-installed cover plates or gratings.
  9. Any person walking on a roof containing fragile roofing materials must either be supported by a walkway or wear suitable fall arrest equipment.
