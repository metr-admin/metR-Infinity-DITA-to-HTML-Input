Lockout Tagout Tryout

Purpose

The purpose of this procedure is to establish a process for the protection of personnel in, on, or around machines or equipment during repair, maintenance, operation, and associated activities, from injury due to unexpected energization, start-up, or release of stored energy from the machines, equipment, or process.

Application

This procedure applies to all Space employees, contractors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities, technical centers, offices, and administrative buildings where work activities expose employees to the dangers of energized equipment during routine operation, maintenance, or repair.

Scope

  1. This procedure, based on Policy EHS-01-107 Life Saving Protocol - Lockout Tagout Tryout, establishes requirements for identifying hazardous energy sources and their control points, isolating those sources of energy, and by affixing appropriate Lockout Tagout devices to the energy control points to prevent the inadvertent release of hazardous energy.
  2. In locations where more stringent state or provincial standards or requirements exist, the more stringent requirements shall be followed.

Procedure

  1. Employees performing Lockout Tagout Tryout shall be aware of all manufacturer and in-plant instructions prior to performing work.
  2. A machine profile shall be developed for each type of machinery or equipment covered by this program to identify the proper Lockout Tagout Tryout procedures for that type of equipment using Form EHS-02-107-F 001 Lockout Tagout Machine Profile or equivalent and shall be available at all times to authorized and affected employees.
    1. An equivalent form may be used that provides the following information:
      1. Identity of the machinery or equipment
      2. Type and magnitude of energy
      3. Machine Shutdown
      4. Local Energy Isolations
      5. Release of any stored energy
      6. Lockout Tagout of all energy sources
      7. Testing (“Tryout”)
      8. Restart
  3. Before shutdown, the authorized employee shall notify all affected employees of the lockout.
  4. The authorized employee shall identify and isolate all local energy feed locations for the types of the energy to be controlled.
  5. Lockout Tagout Tryout shall be followed according to Work Instruction EHS-03-008 Lockout Tagout Tryout or equivalent.
  6. Lockout Tagout Tryout shall only be performed by authorized employees or contractors.
  7. Lockout Tagout Tryout shall use both locks to isolate energy and tags to identify the system has been isolated.
    1. Tagout alone may be used on a device which is incapable of being locked out. When tagout alone is used, a double tagout method shall be implemented. The tags must be located as close as safely possible to the energy isolating device as well as the main energy feed (panel), in a position that must be immediately obvious to anyone attempting to operate the device.
      1. When Tagout alone is used, the facility must consider additional means to ensure full employee protection through the implementation of additional safety measures such as the removal of an isolating circuit element, blocking of a controlling switch, opening an extra disconnecting device, or the removal of a valve handle to reduce the likelihood of inadvertent energization.

Lock and Tag Specifications

  1. Lock Specifications
    1. Locks shall be substantial enough to prevent removal without the use of excessive force or unusual techniques.
    2. Locks shall be capable of withstanding the environment to which they are exposed.
    3. Locks must be easily identified as devices used only for controlling energy, and not be used for other purposes.
    4. Lockout and Tagout devices should be standardized within the facility.
    5. Lockout devices shall be capable of holding the energy isolation devices in a “safe” or “off” position.
  2. Tag Specifications
    1. Tags, including their means of attachment, shall be substantial enough to prevent inadvertent or accidental removal.
    2. Tags shall be constructed so that exposure to weather conditions must not cause the tag to deteriorate or the message on the tag to become illegible.
    3. Tags must warn against hazardous conditions and include wording such as: DO NOT START; DO NOT ENERGIZE; DO NOT OPERATE.
    4. The lock or tag shall indicate the identity of the employee applying the device(s).
  3. Tryout Specifications
    1. Equipment de-energization shall be verified.
    2. In order to assure that all hazardous energy has been isolated, the authorized employee shall perform the appropriate check, for example:
      1. Push the start function button to verify that electric power has been removed, or
      2. Adjust the temperature cycle thermostat to check that all electrical energy has been shut off, or
      3. Crack the steam inlet and discharge line outlet valves, etc.

Multiple Lockout Tagout (Process Involving More Than One Person)

Each authorized employee shall affix a personal lockout device to the multiple lockout device (i.e. hasp), lockbox, or comparable mechanism, and shall remove those devices when work on the machine or equipment being serviced is finished by all authorized employees.

Shift or Personnel Changes

  1. If an authorized employee working on a machine or equipment leaves the job before it is completed, such as job re-assignment or shift change, the next employee shall install a personal lock and tag to the machine’s energy isolation device before the first employee removes his/her original lock and tag.
    1. If not possible, the first employee must remove his/her lock and tag, allowing the next employee to immediately place his/her lock and tag on the machine’s energy isolation device.
  2. Where there is no overlap of workers, but lockout must be maintained, the affected department supervisor’s lock and tag shall be applied before the person leaving the job assignment or piece of equipment removes their lock and tag.

Contractors

  1. When outside contractors are performing Lockout Tagout Tryout, the Plant Supervisor or designated person and the outside contractor shall inform each other of their respective Lockout Tagout process.
  2. The Plant Supervisor or designated person shall ensure that affected Space employees understand and respect the outside contractor’s Lockout Tagout process.
  3. In instances where both Space employees and outside contractors are working together on the same machinery or equipment requiring Lockout Tagout, the Space Lockout Tagout Tryout process shall be followed.

Annual Inspection And Certification

  1. At least annually, each authorized employee must be observed performing a Lockout Tagout Tryout process in order to document that the requirements of this program are being followed. This includes:
    1. Demonstrating proficiency by making use of locks, tags, and related lockout equipment.
    2. Properly following all steps as outlined in the manufacturer and in-plant instructions.
  2. The Plant Supervisor or other designated person must perform this inspection and certification using the Form EHS-02-107-F 003 Lockout Tagout Annual Inspection and Certification Audit Checklist or equivalent.
  3. A record of this observation must be maintained and documented using the form EHS-02-107-F 004 Lockout Tagout Annual Inspection and Certification Audit Log or equivalent.
    1. Completed Checklists and Logs shall be submitted to EHS for filing annually.
      1. Records shall be retained by EHS for a period of at least three (3) full calendar years.

Exceptions to Lockout Tagout Tryout

  1. Maintenance and servicing during normal production operations are not covered by this Lockout Tagout Tryout program unless an employee is required to:
    1. Remove or bypass a guard or other safety devices, or
    2. Place any part of his/her body into an area on a machine at a point of operation or when an associated danger zone exists during a machine operating cycle.
  2. Minor tool changes, adjustments, job changes, and other minor servicing activities which take place during normal production operations are not covered by this Lockout Tagout Tryout program if:
    1. They are routine, repetitive, and integral to the use of the equipment for production.
    2. The work is performed using alternative measures which provide effective protection.
  3. The Lockout Tagout Tryout process does not apply to work on cord and plug connected electric equipment if:
    1. The hazard of unexpected energization or start-up of the equipment is controlled by the unplugging of the equipment, and
    2. The plug is under the exclusive control of the employee performing the servicing or maintenance.
  4. The Lockout Tagout Tryout program does not apply to equipment that has been placed in an “out of service” status for business or production purposes (e.g., poor efficiency, recycled, sold, etc.) and not related to servicing or maintenance.
    1. When equipment is placed in an “out of service” status it shall be affixed with a tag stating “Out Of Service” and shall not be affixed with a lockout-specific tag.
  5. In order to evaluate equipment and/or machines to determine if alternative methods can be implemented other than the locking and tagging out of energy, use the Alternatives to Lockout Tagout Tryout section within Form EHS-02-107-F 001 Lockout Tagout Machine Profile or equivalent.

Removing a Lockout Tagout Device

  1. Before lockout devices are removed and energy is restored to the machine or equipment, the authorized employee will verify the equipment is fully reassembled, emergency stops reactivated, and guards replaced.
  2. Lockout and Tagout devices shall be removed by the authorized employee who installed them.
  3. Whenever an authorized employee who applied a Lockout Tagout device is not available to remove such device, it may only be removed by a supervisor or designee, only after it has been verified that the authorized employee who attached the Lockout Tagout device is no longer at the facility, using Form EHS-02-107-F 005 Abandoned Lock Removal Form or equivalent.
  4. The supervisor or designee must inspect the equipment thoroughly to ensure it can be operated safely before removing the Lockout Tagout device.
  5. The authorized employee who installed the original Lockout Tagout device shall be contacted and informed that the Lockout Tagout device has been removed during his/her absence before resuming their next work shift.
