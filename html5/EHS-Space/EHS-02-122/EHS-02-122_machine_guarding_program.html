Machine Guarding Program

EHS-02-122

Approved: 2016-07-31

Purpose

The purpose of this procedure is to establish a process for the safeguarding of personnel who may require that some part of the their body be placed in the path of machine movement, or within the vicinity of moving parts.

Application

  1. This procedure applies to all Space employees, contractors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities, technical centers, offices, and administrative buildings where work activities expose employees to moving parts which require machine safeguarding.
  2. This procedure applies to all machines used by Space during routine and non-routine activities, operated by Space employees, contractors or visitors.

Scope

  1. This procedure establishes requirements for safeguarding machine parts, functions or processes which may cause injury.
    1. For purposes of this procedure, a safeguard is a shield or device covering hazardous areas of a machine to prevent contact with body parts or to control hazards like flying chips or particles from exiting the machine.
  2. In locations where more stringent country, state or provincial standards or requirements exist, the more stringent requirements shall be followed.

Reference

  • EHS-02-122-F Machine Guarding Hazard Assessment

Procedure

  1. Hierarchy of Guarding
    1. Machine guarding and control decisions shall be made in the following order of preference:
      1. Design out or eliminate the hazard,
      2. Physically “engineer out” the exposure to the hazard,
      3. Guard the hazard – require personal protective equipment,
      4. Use warning devices, or make the danger clearing apparent,
      5. Use warning signs,
      6. Use safe working practices and procedures.
    2. Equipment guarding and other controls shall be considered in the design and construction of equipment to prevent the potential for contact with moving mechanical parts. Guards and other controls must be designed by an individual competent in machine guarding to prevent reaching over, under, around or through openings to reach moving parts or hazardous energy sources.
  2. Hazard Assessment
    1. When the operation of a machine, equipment or accidental contact with it could injure the operator or others in the vicinity, the hazards shall either be controlled or eliminated.
    2. Any machine or equipment which presents hazards that must be safeguarded against shall be assessed using Form EHS-02-122-F Machine Guarding Hazard Assessment, or equivalent.
      1. New installations or modifications to existing equipment must be assessed prior to install or modification.
      2. Existing equipment must be assessed systematically based on: current perception of risk, near-miss and accident data, local regulations, etc.
    3. Upon installation, machine guards and other safety controls shall be evaluated for proper function and to ensure all hazards were identified and that associated risks have been eliminated or reduced to an acceptable level prior to the release of the equipment for use.
    4. Machines that require guarding shall be inspected regularly. Based on the results of these inspections, maintenance or replacement of guards shall be conducted as necessary.
  3. Emergency Stop Controls
    1. Emergency stop controls shall be provided on equipment for use in interrupting power in emergency situations to avert impending or actual danger.
    2. Emergency stop controls shall be accessible to all potential machine operators, supervisors, and co-workers; labeled in the language understood by employees at the site; and consistently color-coded to ensure visibility to equipment operators.
    3. Emergency stop controls shall cease the hazardous process without creating additional hazards or increased risk.
    4. When necessary, emergency stop controls shall trigger or permit the triggering of safeguards.
    5. Disengaging emergency stops shall not restart machinery, but only permit it to be restarted (use of a reset is advisable).
    6. The emergency stop function shall be available and operational regardless of the operating mode.
    7. Emergency stop controls shall not be modified in any way that reduces the effectiveness of the emergency stop in an emergency condition.
    8. Emergency stop devices shall be considered a back-up safety mechanism to other machine safeguards and not a substitute for them.
  4. Electrical Power/Controls
    1. Each machine shall be equipped with a master switch that can be locked and tagged during repair or maintenance operations.
    2. Power controls and operating controls shall be located within easy reach of the operator at his/her regular workstation. Controls shall be brightly marked and easily identified allowing the operator to cut off power at the point of operation.
    3. Each machine shall be provided with an appropriate electrical ground.
    4. A trip device, or circuit breaker, shall be provided on machinery where injury might result if motors were to restart after power failures, preventing the machine from operating when electricity is restored.
  5. Guards
    1. All guards shall be appropriate for the hazards involved, secured in place, constructed of substantial material, and have surfaces free of hazardous projections.
    2. Appropriate guards shall be provided to protect the operator and others in the area from hazards such as exposed belts, pulleys, sheaves, drive shafts, drive couplings, chains, rotating parts, flying chips and sparks.
    3. No employee shall operate and/or cause to be operated any machinery without proper protective devices in place.
    4. The operator shall never perform layout, assembly, or set-up work on the table/work area when the machine is running.
    5. Guards shall be secured in place unless equipped with an interlock that stops all moving parts when opened.
  6. Preventative Maintenance
    1. All emergency stop controls, guarding, and related hazard control equipment (i.e., light curtains, safety interlocks, etc.) must be functionally checked and maintained at periodic intervals in keeping with the manufacturer’s specifications, but no less than annually. A one-year documentation history of preventive maintenance and functional checks must be kept in facility files.
    2. Preventive maintenance frequency shall be set on the basis of the manufacturer’s recommendation, risk assessment, and/or local regulations.
  7. Training and Signage
    1. Affected employees shall be trained on the correct method of working in areas where guards or other controls are in place.
    2. Signage shall be affixed near the point of operation to warn of potential hazards.
