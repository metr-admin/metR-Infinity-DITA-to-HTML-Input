Life Saving Protocol - Hot End Hazards

Purpose

Space has developed a set of mandatory Life Saving Protocols that set a minimum standard and minimum performance expectations for managing high-level hazards common to the glass industry.

Scope

  1. This protocol applies to all Space employees, contractors and visitors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities and technical centers. All sites in all regions are expected to develop site specific procedures to comply with this protocol.
  2. Requirements of this protocol will apply to the control of risks associated with work being conducted in the forming areas and hot end.

Process Requirements

The following actions are required to ensure that the risks and hazards associated with Hot End operations are appropriately controlled:

  1. General Hot End requirements
    1. Adherence to the required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).
    2. Regular housekeeping inspection and monitoring programs are in place.
    3. Risk assessments and job safety analysis have been completed.
  2. Furnace Area
    1. Ensure that procedures are in place to control the risk of accessing the regenerator and melter crowns.
    2. Ensure that procedures are in place to control the risks associated with work on fuel train/gas train (fuel supply lines and regulators).
    3. Ensure that procedures are in place to control risks associated with work on electrical boost equipment.
    4. Ensure that procedures are in place for activities that involve maintenance and change of refractory parts (i.e. Orifice Bowl).
    5. Entry procedures and permits are in place for access and egress from the furnace melting chamber or regenerator that is above ambient air temperature.
  3. Forming Area
    1. Required safety equipment identified in relevant procedures are in place and in use.
    2. The machine is equipped with Blank Side Barriers or Anti-deflection bracket covers.
    3. All forming machines should be fitted with sprinkler systems consistent with Space Property Protection Manual â€“ Forming Machine Fire Protection.
    4. A formal machine cleaning program is in place.
    5. During maintenance activity, Lock Out/Tag Out procedures are implemented for all areas of the machine.
    6. Sectional work takes place using procedures including isolation of adjacent sections as appropriate
    7. Ensure that procedures are in place so that the machine can be stopped in the event of an emergency.
    8. Job change safety procedures must be in place and specify safety requirements.
    9. Procedures must be in place for safe clearing of machine jam-ups
    10. Suitable lifting equipment must be in place around the machine to reduce manual handling risks.
    11. Suitable PPE must be worn in the Hot End according to local risk assessments and procedures.
    12. A heat stress management processes should be in place for all workers in the Hot End.

Training

  1. Competency based training programs must be in place for employees that work in the Hot End and should include; Risk assessments, JHA, procedures and work element sheets/instructions.
  2. All forming employees receive annual machine fire prevention/deluge system and response training.
  3. All relevant employees must receive glass leak training.
