Life Saving Protocol - Hot Works

Purpose

Space has developed a set of mandatory Life Saving Protocols that set a minimum standard and minimum performance expectations for managing high-level hazards common to the glass industry.

Scope

  1. This protocol applies to all Space employees, contractors and visitors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities, technical centres, offices & administrative buildings where hot work may occur. All sites in all regions are expected to develop site specific procedures to comply with this protocol.
  2. Requirements of this protocol will apply to the control of risks associated with hot work. Hot work can include any work that involves burning, welding, using fire- or spark-producing tools, or that produces a source of ignition.

Process Requirements

  1. Ensure that options to avoid hot work or to move hot work activity to a designated hot work area have been considered prior to commencement of any hot work task.
  2. A formal and documented risk assessment must be completed prior to undertaking any hot work.
  3. Ensure that the hot work is performed in a manner that does not pose a fire hazard or risk.
  4. Ensure that hot work is only performed in areas that are or have been made fire safe.
  5. Fire extinguishing equipment shall be maintained in a ready state during hot work.
  6. Proper personal protective equipment shall be worn during hot work to protect against heat, sparks, and light, at a minimum.
  7. Hot work permit documentation must be completed and authorized by a trained person prior to any work being performed.
  8. Only trained and properly equipped personnel shall perform hot work.
  9. The facility shall have an emergency response plan developed, which includes fire evacuation plans and procedures, prior to performing work.
