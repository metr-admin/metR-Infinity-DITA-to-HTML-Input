Lifting and Supporting Loads Procedure

Purpose

To protect personnel and prevent injuries by ensuring that all affected employees are aware of the potential hazards of lifting operations and are provided with the knowledge they need to protect themselves from the potential hazards associated with lifting operations and by establishing safe work practices for all affected employees.

Scope

This procedure, based on Policy EHS-01-105 Life Saving Protocol – Lifting and Supporting Loads.

Application

  1. This procedure applies to all Space employees, contractors and visitors, and all operations including standard and non-standard (i.e., construction projects) activities within manufacturing facilities, technical centers, offices, and administrative buildings where lifting activities may occur.
  2. Requirements of this procedure will apply to the control of risks associated with lifting loads using lifting devices such as cranes, hoists, and winches. Specifically, this protocol relates to the risks associated with working in and around loads being lifted above the ground.

Reference

  • EHS-02-105-F 001 Lifting and Supporting Loads Pre-Use Inspection Checklist
  • EHS-02-105-F 002 Lifting and Supporting Loads Inventory
  • EHS-03-007 Lifting and Supporting Loads

Procedure

  1. Alternatives to Lifting and Supporting Loads
    1. Prior to planning a lift, consideration shall be given to alternative methods for the task at hand. If it is possible to complete the task without using lifting equipment or the need for a lift can be designed out, then preference shall be given to these alternative methods.
  2. Estimating the Weight of Loads
    1. Do not lift until the load weights have been determined. When estimating load weights, operators will stay within 50% of the crane’s rated capacity or manufacturer recommendation. If there are ever discrepancies, the lowest determined weight shall be used. Never attempt a load lift based solely on a guess.
    2. The following methods may be used to estimate the weight of loads:
      1. Check equipment classification/load plates.
      2. Check shipping papers.
      3. Consult with the equipment manufacturer.
      4. Estimate weight using weights of similar loads.
      5. Use a dynamometer.
      6. Use industry standard tables or charts.
  3. Crane and Lifting Equipment Operations
    1. While operating cranes and lifting equipment, personnel shall perform safe operating practices using Work Instruction EHS-03-007 Lifting and Supporting Loads or equivalent.
  4. Crane and Equipment Inspections
    1. Pre-use inspections. Cranes and equipment (hoists, slings, hooks, etc.) shall be inspected by the operator or designated trained employee or contractor before each use according to Form EHS-02-105-F 001 Lifting and Supporting Loads Pre-Use Inspection Checklist, or equivalent.
    2. Periodic inspections. Supervisors will determine and schedule additional inspections periodically to be completed by a third party contractor, where service conditions warrant. A thorough periodic inspection shall be made on a regular basis, to be determined on the basis of the following:
      1. Frequency of crane use
      2. Severity of service conditions
      3. Nature of lifts being made
      4. Experience gained on the service life of cranes used in similar circumstances, as well as consensus standards and manufacturer’s recommendations
    3. Damaged/unserviceable cranes. Cranes and lifting equipment found to be damaged or unserviceable shall be immediately removed from service and tagged with a visual identifier to indicate status.
  5. Crane Inventory
    1. A running inventory list of all crane and lifting equipment shall be maintained using Form EHS-02-105-F 002 Lifting and Supporting Loads Inventory, or equivalent.
