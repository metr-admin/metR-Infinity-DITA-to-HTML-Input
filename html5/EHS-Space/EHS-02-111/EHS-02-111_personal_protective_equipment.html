Personal Protective Equipment

Purpose

  1. To protect human life and prevent injuries by establishing a system of hazard assessment, Personal Protective Equipment selection and use, and employee training.
  2. This procedure establishes the minimum requirements of the Space Personal Protective Equipment Procedure. This procedure applies to all Space employees, contractors, and visitors of Space facilities.

Application

  1. Space protects their employees from workplace hazards through the use of engineering or work practice controls. If the machine or work environment can be physically changed to prevent employee exposure to the potential hazard, then the hazard shall be eliminated with an engineering control. If employees can be removed from exposure to the potential hazard by changing the way they do their jobs, then the hazard shall be eliminated with a work practice control. When these controls are not feasible or do not provide sufficient protection, the use of personal protective equipment (PPE) is required. A Job Hazard Analysis is required to assess the area and to determine if hazards are present, or are likely to be present, that would require the use of PPE.
  2. This procedure applies to all Personal Protective Equipment with the exception of Fall Protection (covered in Fall Protection Procedure), Hearing Protection (covered in Hearing Conservation Procedure), Respiratory Protection (covered in Respiratory Protection Procedure) and Electrical Protection (covered in Electrical Safety - Related Work Practices Procedure and Arc Flash procedure).

Reference

  • EHS-03-001 Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment
  • EHS-03-001-F Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment
  • EHS-07-011 Global PPE Matrix

Procedure

  1. Methods of Control
    1. When a potential hazard is identified, engineering controls shall be implemented to eliminate or reduce hazards, if feasible.
    2. If this is not feasible, the process shall be isolated or the hazard shall be guarded.
    3. If other controls are not possible, PPE shall be used.
  2. Safety Equipment
    1. Personal Protective Equipment (safety glasses, face shields, respirators, welding protection, safety shoes, etc.) as specified for certain jobs shall be worn by employees where their use is required.
  3. Hazard Assessment
    1. Each job function shall be assessed to determine which potential hazards are likely to be present that require the use of Personal Protective Equipment. For each job that has been evaluated, Form EHS-03-001-F Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment, or equivalent, shall be completed.
      1. Work Instruction EHS-03-001 Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment may be used to properly enter information into EHS-03-001-F Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment.
  4. PPE Selection
    1. Eye and Face Protection
      1. The facility shall require that affected employees use appropriate eye or face protection when exposed to eye or face hazards from flying particles, molten metal, liquid chemicals, acids, caustic liquids, chemical gases or vapors, or potentially dangerous light radiation.
        1. The use of contact lenses should be avoided where the likelihood exists for harmful contact with chemicals.
      2. Where applicable, all safety glasses, goggles, and face shields shall meet the test requirements referenced in Reference Material EHS-07-011 Global PPE Matrix, or equivalent.
    2. Foot Protection
      1. The facility shall require that affected employees use proper footwear when working in areas where there is a danger of foot injuries due to falling or rolling objects or objects piercing the sole, or where such employee’s feet are exposed to electrical hazards.
    3. Hand Protection
      1. The facility shall require that affected employees use appropriate gloves when employees’ hands are exposed to potential hazards such as those from skin absorption of harmful substances, severe cuts or lacerations, severe abrasions, punctures, chemical burns, thermal burns, and harmful temperature extremes.
      2. The facility shall base the selection of the appropriate glove on an evaluation of the performance characteristics of the glove relative to the task(s) to be performed, conditions present, duration of use, and hazards and potential hazards identified.
    4. Head Protection
      1. The facility shall require that affected employees wear a protective helmet when working in areas where there is a potential for injury to the head from falling objects or from the employee bumping his/her head.
      2. Where applicable, all hard hats or bump caps shall meet the test requirements of local legislation.
    5. Protective Clothing
      1. The facility shall require that each affected employee use appropriate protective clothing when required.
      2. Protective clothing shall be selected giving consideration to the potential hazard(s) for which it is worn.
  5. General Safety Practices
    1. Safety warning signs, such as “Eye Protection Required,” shall be installed at all entrances where that particular PPE is required and adhered to.
    2. Only PPE equipment authorized by the supervisor shall be used.
    3. Long hair (shoulder length) must be safely controlled when working around moving equipment, chemicals, and open flames.
    4. Loose clothing and/or jewelry shall not be worn around moving equipment.
    5. Personal headsets and/or earphone-style radios shall not be worn or used in the manufacturing area.
    6. Wear all necessary PPE according to the requirements of the work area or specific operation.
    7. Open-toed shoes, sandals, and high heels represent inappropriate footwear in manufacturing areas and are prohibited.
  6. Documentation and Recordkeeping
    1. The following records shall be maintained:
      1. Training records.
      2. Hazard assessment records.
        1. Use Form EHS-03-001-F Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment, or equivalent, to document the completion of hazard assessments for PPE usage.
      3. Additional forms should be completed if available Personal Protective Equipment or facility conditions change.
