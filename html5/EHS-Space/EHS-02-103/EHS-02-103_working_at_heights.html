Working at Heights

Purpose

The purpose of this procedure is to prevent injuries by establishing a system and guidelines to protect all personnel and contractors engaged in work activities that expose them to potential falls from elevations. This establishes the minimum requirements to ensure safety when working at heights.

Application

  1. This procedure applies to all Space personnel, contractors and visitors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities, technical centers, offices and administrative buildings where work activities expose personnel to falls from heights.
  2. Working At Heights is applicable any time an Space employee is working at unprotected elevations as specified by local regulation. The Working At Heights process does not apply to work performed on ladders or approved scaffolding.

Scope

This procedure, based on Policy EHS-01-103 Life Saving Protocol- Working at Heights establishes requirements for performing work on potentially unstable surface, using equipment to work at the elevated level (elevating work platforms) on a sloping or slippery surface where it is difficult for people to maintain their balance, near an unprotected open edge (near incomplete stairwells), near a hole or shaft (trenches, service pits) or on a fragile surface(roofs).Working at heights can include tasks where activity is carried out on any structure or plant being constructed or installed, demolished or dismantled, inspected, tested, repaired, or cleaned.

Procedure

  1. Hazard Assessment/Identification
    1. A hazard assessment of the job task or work area is required to determine if fall hazards are present, or likely to be present. For purposes of the Working At Heights program, a fall hazard may exist if (but is not limited to):
      1. Any time an employee is working at unprotected heights of no more than 2 meters (6 feet) or as specified by local regulation.
      2. When working above areas that present extraordinary hazards, such as,
        1. Dangerous machinery
        2. Impalement hazards
        3. Floor / Roof openings
        4. Open floors, access ways for hoists, skylights, vents
        5. Leading edge hazards
        6. Unprotected edges
      3. Steep roof or surface.
      4. Machine loading / crating for shipment.
      5. Building maintenance work.
      6. Aerial boom style lifts and Scissor lifts.
      7. Walking on or working in an elevated pipe rack or electrical trays.
      8. When hazards cannot be engineered out or the work procedures cannot be modified to eliminate the hazard.
    2. If the fall hazard cannot be eliminated, then personal fall arrest or fall prevention equipment shall be used.
  2. A Permit shall be issued for any work outside of a permanent platform or while using mobile equipment (For example, EWP, any roof work, ect.) using Form "EHS-02-103-F 003 Working at Heights Permit" or equivalent.
  3. Equipment Inspection
    1. Fall arrest and fall protection equipment shall be inspected, maintained, and documented before each use according to Form EHS-02-103-F 001 Working at Heights Equipment Checklist Form or equivalent.
    2. Complete the Working at Heights Inspection Checklist Form by using Work Instruction "EHS-03-005 Working at Heights Equipment Inspection Checklist".
  4. Equipment Use (Tying Off)
    1. Employees shall use appropriate fall arrest equipment when performing tasks at unprotected elevations of 2 meters (6ft), or as specified by local legislation.
    2. Appropriate fall arrest equipment includes, at a minimum:
      1. A full body harness that properly fits the user according to Work Instruction EHS-03-006 Wearing Harness Equipment.
      2. A lanyard that is appropriate to the task being conducted
        1. Shock absorbing lanyards shall only be utilized when the fall distance from the connection point, to a lower level or objects, exceeds 5.3 meters (17 ½ ft.)
      3. An anchorage point
    3. Once the appropriate harness and lanyard has been donned, employees shall select and connect to an anchorage point.
      1. Anchorage points shall be selected to ensure there would not be contact with a lower level or objects should a fall occur
      2. Prior to use, anchorage points and devices shall be visually inspected to determine that they are of substantial strength and undamaged
      3. Anchorage points shall be attached to substantial structures capable of supporting 5000lbs (2268kg) per employee attached
      4. Anchorage points shall be placed directly above or behind the work area to avoid potential swing fall hazards and away from sharp edges or other objects
    4. When employees are finished with the task, they shall ensure that the work area is clear and safe (i.e. holes/openings are covered) prior to unhooking from the anchorage point
  5. Posting of Permits for working at heights.
    1. All working at heights permits shall be posted.
    2. Fall Arrest Equipment shall be available at the plant according to the Fall Arrest section of the MSDR EHS-07-111 PPE Equipment Register.
    3. Personnel shall become familiar with the equipment’s visual appearance by performing a visual inspection on a frequent basis in order to identify damage or wear.
    4. A third party contractor shall inspect all fall arrest equipment every six months in addition to user pre-use inspection.
    5. The manufacturer's directions and recommendations for the inspection requirements of the equipment in use shall be followed.
      1. Any fall arrest equipment that is found to be defective or has been subject to a fall shall be removed from service immediately and brought to the attention of EHS or supervisor.
  6. An emergency response and rescue plan for working at heights of shall be implemented and documented.
  7. Cleaning and Storage of Equipment
    1. Storage areas shall be clean, dry, and free of exposure to fumes or corrosive elements.
    2. Equipment shall be cleaned according to manufacturer’s instructions.
    3. When not in use, the harness shall be stored hanging by the back D-ring. This allows the harness to retain its original shape.
    4. The equipment shall be stored in a cool, dry place until needed. If materials appear to be faded or tags and labels are illegible, consult a trained person to determine if replacement is necessary.
  8. Alternative Methods
    1. When performing tasks conducted at elevations of 2 meters (6 feet) or as specified by local legislations, preference should be given to alternative methods, such as fixed platforms or guardrails which eliminate the fall hazards.
    2. Personal fall arrest equipment, such as harnesses and lanyards, shall be used when the fall hazards cannot be eliminated through engineering controls or through modification of work procedures.
  9. Personnel training shall be document using Form EHS-07-103 Working at Heights Qualified Employee List.
