Life Saving Protocol - Mobile Equipment

Purpose

Space has developed a set of mandatory Life Saving Protocols that set a minimum standard and minimum performance expectations for managing high-level hazards common to the glass industry.

Scope

  1. This protocol applies to all Space employees, contractors and visitors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities, technical centers, offices & administrative buildings. All sites in all regions where mobile equipment may be used are expected to develop site specific procedures to comply with this protocol.
  2. Requirements of this protocol will apply to the control of risks associated with operating, loading, and travelling with mobile equipment such as forklifts, boom and scissor-type manlifts, wheel/steer loaders, sweepers, rail car movers, personnel/burden carriers/tuggers. Specifically, this protocol relates to the risks associated with operating, loading, and travelling with mobile equipment.

Equipment Management

  1. All mobile equipment including attachments (side shift devices, jibs, extension tines, etc.) must be formally documented in a register.
  2. Ensure that mobile equipment is registered, inspected, and certified in accordance with local legislative requirements.
  3. Ensure that the safe working load tag/rating plate is displayed on all mobile equipment and confirm compliance with periodic inspections.
  4. Undertake periodic inspections, testing, and maintenance of mobile equipment using a competent person. Inspections must be documented and include structural and mechanical elements, refer to relevant standards for guidance.
  5. Undertake pre-operation inspections to ensure equipment is safe to use before operating. Follow tag-out procedures and remove equipment from service if damaged, unsafe, or does not meet compliance specifications.

Risk Assessments and Competence

  1. A documented risk assessment and/or a job hazard analysis must be completed by a competent person prior to using any mobile equipment.
  2. Operators must be trained and competent in the operation of mobile equipment. Training must be documented and available upon request.
  3. Where required, operators of mobile equipment must have a current license, or be under instruction (e.g. Logbook) to obtain a license.

Operating, Loading, and Traveling

  1. Charging or refueling of mobile equipment must be performed by a competent person and in an area designated for that purpose.
  2. Applicable safety equipment must be worn, such as seat belts, when operating forklifts and wheel/steer loaders as well as body harnesses with tie-offs when operating boom and scissor type manlifts.
  3. Operators must not use personal mobile/handheld communication devices whilst operating mobile equipment.
  4. Operators shall report all hazards, near misses, and incidents regardless of fault or severity.
  5. Operators must be aware of overhead clearance and overhead objects such as ceilings, cable trays, pipe work, supporting beams, electrical cabling, lights, and fire suppression systems.
  6. Passengers are not to ride the mobile equipment at any time, unless the equipment is designed to carry more than one person.
  7. Mobile equipment must be clearly visible and audible by persons on foot. Operators must alert employees in the area especially when entering or exiting a building, turning, or reversing by means of reverse alarm, horn, headlights, and flashing beacons.
  8. Always travel with platforms lowered, or so that a load may not obstruct the view from the operatorâ€™s seat.
  9. Site speed limits must be adhered to at all times.
  10. Mobile equipment must not be left unattended by the operator without engaging the parking brake, lowering the load, and shutting off the engine and removing the key.
  11. All mobile equipment cabs must be fitted with suitable protection to protect drivers from falling objects and materials.
  12. Operators of mobile equipment must always give priority to pedestrians and stop at crossings, respecting the Space property at all times.
  13. Operators must always look in the direction of travel when driving mobile equipment (e.g. if reversing, operators must look over their shoulder).
  14. Operators must not drive whilst taking a course of medication that could impair their judgement. Operators must inform their direct supervisor of any medication they have been prescribed so an assessment can be conducted.

Traffic Management

  1. All sites must develop a formal Traffic Management Plan with suitable pedestrian/vehicle segregation.
  2. The plan shall include rules for safe designating parking, refueling/charging, repairing, and moving of disable vehicles.
  3. The plan shall include the provision of signs, speed limits, barriers, bollards, walk ways, etc.
