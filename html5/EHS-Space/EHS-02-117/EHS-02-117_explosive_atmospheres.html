Explosive Atmospheres

Purpose

The purpose of this procedure is to protect personnel by helping individuals recognize and avoid dangerous substances in explosive atmospheres. The goal is to reduce and manage the risk of fires and explosions from dangerous substances in potentially explosive atmospheres.

Application

This procedure applies to all Space employees, contractors and visitors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities, technical centers, offices, and administrative buildings where explosive atmospheres could exist.

Scope

  1. Explosive atmospheres can be caused by flammable gases, mists or vapors or by combustible dusts. If there is enough of the substance mixed with air, then only an ignition source is required to cause an explosion.
  2. Explosive atmospheres that may be found within Space are:
    1. Storage vessels/areas that contain dangerous substances
    2. Storage vessels/areas that have contained dangerous substances
    3. Storage of flammable wastes
    4. Deliveries from road tankers such as diesel, liquid oxygen, and flammable substance such as acetylene

Requirements

  1. Fire and explosion risks that may be caused by dangerous substances shall be assessed. This shall be an identification and careful examination of:
    1. The dangerous substances on the site
    2. The work activities involving these substances
    3. The ways in which those substances and work activities could harm people

Procedure

  1. Risk Assessment
    1. Prior to carrying out work, a formal and documented risk assessment shall be completed. The risk assessments shall examine:
      1. The hazardous properties of the dangerous substance involved;
      2. The amount of dangerous substances involved;
      3. The work processes, and their interactions, including any cleaning, repair or maintenance activities that will be carried out;
      4. The temperatures and pressures at which the dangerous substances will be handled;
      5. The containment system and controls provided to prevent liquids, gases, vapors or dusts escaping into the general atmosphere of the workplace;
      6. The likelihood that explosive atmospheres may occur or persist;
      7. The likelihood that ignition sources, including electrostatic discharges, can arise and become active and effective;
      8. Any explosive atmosphere formed within an enclosed plant or storage vessel;
      9. Any measure provided to ensure that any explosive atmosphere does not persist for an extended time, e.g. ventilation.
    2. Preventing Risks & Control Measures
      1. Control measures shall be put in place to eliminate risks from dangerous substances, or reduce them as far as is reasonably practicable. Where it is not possible to eliminate the risk completely, measures shall be taken to control the risks and reduce the severity (mitigate the effects) of any harmful event.
      2. If it is feasible, dangerous substances shall be replaced with lower risk substances, or a different work process shall be used.
      3. Where the risk cannot be eliminated, control measures shall be applied in the following priority order:
        1. Reducing or minimizing the quantity of dangerous substances
        2. Avoiding or minimizing releases of dangerous substances
        3. Controlling releases at the source
        4. Preventing the formation of dangerous atmospheres
        5. Collecting, containing, and removing releases to a safe place (e.g., ventilation)
        6. Avoiding ignition sources (e.g., electrostatic discharge from clothing, sparking tools, etc.)
        7. Avoiding adverse conditions (i.e., exceeding the limits of temperature or control settings) that could lead to danger
        8. Keeping incompatible substances apart
      4. Control measures shall be consistent with the risk assessment and appropriate to the nature of the activity or operation.
    3. Mitigation
      1. Mitigation measures shall be put in place and shall be consistent with the risk assessment and appropriate to the nature of the activity or operation and include:
        1. Reducing the number of employees exposed to the risk
        2. Providing a plant that is explosion resistant
        3. Providing a plant that is corrosion resistant
        4. Providing explosion suppression or explosion relief equipment
        5. Taking measures to control or minimize the spread of fires or explosions
        6. Providing suitable personal protective equipment
    4. Emergency Plans and Procedures
      1. Arrangements shall be made to deal with emergencies. These plans and procedures shall cover safety drills and suitable communication and warning systems and shall be in proportion to the risks.
      2. If an emergency occurs, employees tasked with carrying out repairs or other necessary work shall be provided with the appropriate equipment to allow them to carry out this work safely.
      3. The information in the emergency plans and procedures shall be made available to the emergency services to allow them to develop their own plans if necessary.
    5. Providing Information, Instruction and Training for Employees
      1. Employees shall be informed of:
        1. The dangerous substances in the workplace and the risks they present
        2. Relevant safety data sheets (SDSs)
        3. Information on any other legislation that applies to the dangerous substance
        4. Findings of the risk assessment
        5. The control measures put in place, their purpose, and how to follow and use
        6. The emergency procedures
      2. Information, instruction, and training shall only be provided to visitors or contractor where it is required to ensure their safety.
