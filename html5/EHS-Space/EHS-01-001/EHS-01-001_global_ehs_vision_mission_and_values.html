Global EHS Vision, Mission and Values

Vision

Zero incidents

  1. Space is committed to achieve a global workplace with zero environment, occupational health or safety incidents.
  2. EHS incidents include all injuries and environmental non-compliance issues.

Mission

  1. Empower our people and engage our communities to achieve zero incidents. Through leading practices, we implement global, sustainable and innovative collaborative solutions. We enable learning, recognize achievements, and continuously improve.
  2. We are committed to achieve a zero incident workplace through our people â€“ by providing them with the resources, tools and leadership support to see and solve problems that could lead to an EHS incident.
  3. We focus on applying leading practices by collaborating with local, regional and national communities across regions, and using internal and external ideas to create sustainable, collaborative, innovative solutions.
  4. We are committed to being a learning organization; to recognize and reward EHS employees and our partners in the functions and businesses; and to improve our processes and results every day.

Five EHS Principles

These principles describe how we will deliver a zero EHS incidents workplace. They drive our culture, strategic planning, EHS processes, talent management, and metrics.

All incidents are preventable

All EHS incidents are preventable. We focus on root causes to find solutions to solve current issues and prevent a repeat incident anywhere in the world.

Everyone is responsible

Preventing EHS incidents is the responsibility of every one of our 21,500 employees. While employees in different roles have different responsibilities, all employees are expected to help us reach zero EHS incidents.

We identify and reduce risk

EHS incidents start with risk and end in an incident. We work to identify risks or problems before they become an incident, and identify sustainable solutions through risk reduction.

A proactive no blame approach

When there is an incident, leadership and the global EHS team are committed to solve the problem, share the solution globally, and avoid laying blame for the past.

Never put production ahead of EHS

Employees often must make decisions that could risk an EHS incident in order to meet business needs. Space leadership is committed to create a culture where these situations are openly discussed, where alternative actions are assessed, and where economic considerations do not automatically come before EHS.
