Job Hazard Analysis

Procedure

  1. This procedure is to ensure that hazards associated with the business operations are identified in a uniform manner, the risks associated with those hazards are assessed and appropriate control measures are put in place.
  2. Job Hazard Analysis is a form of risk assessment and shall be considered the same for the purpose of this procedure although the documentation used may differ.

Reference

  • EHS-02-112-F 001 Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment
  • EHS-02-112-F 002 Job Hazard Analysis Risk Register
  • EHS-02-112-F 003 EHS Risk Matrix
  • EHS-03-001 Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment

Responsibility & Authority

Management shall ensure this process is conducted for all operational aspects of the business processes within the workplace where there is a hazard present. Ensuring control measures are appropriate and do not introduce alternate hazard sources is an integral part of the process.

Process

  1. The JHA/Risk Assessment process shall consist of four phases:-
    1. Hazard Identification
      1. Hazards shall be identified when:
        1. an accident / incident investigation takes place or review of previous injury / incident records or hazard notifications take place.
        2. a hazard or near miss notification is received.
        3. a workplace inspection is conducted.
        4. a new plant, equipment, processes or chemical / substance is introduced.
        5. modifications to plant, equipment, processes, substances are made.
        6. new information which becomes available which affects health, safety of the environment is introduced.
        7. assessments are required by legislation.
    2. Risk Assessment
      1. The Plant / Department Manager shall ensure that risks associated with each identified hazard are assessed in consultation with appropriate personnel. The appropriate personnel may include team members, EHS personnel, EHS representative, engineers, supervisors, and persons who identified a hazards, and any person involved in an incident.
      2. The risk assessment shall consider:
        1. the likelihood of the hazard causing harm and
        2. the severity of the harm of the hazardous event or situation.
      3. The level of risk shall be determined as Low, Moderate, High or Extreme using EHS-02-112-F 003 EHS Risk Matrix and will be dependent on the detail of the hazard entered into risk assessment EHS-03-001-F Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment.
        1. Work Instruction EHS-03-001 Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment may be used to properly enter information into EHS-03-001-F Job Hazard Analysis and PPE Assessment.
    3. Risk Control Measures
      1. The Plant / Department Manger shall ensure the appropriate control measures are determined. Appropriate personnel or contractor must be consulted as required in the determination of control measures.
      2. The control measures to be implemented shall be in accordance with the hierarchy of controls as follows:
        1. elimination
        2. substitution
        3. engineering
        4. administrative controls
        5. personal protective equipment
    4. Review of JHA/Risk Assessment & Control Measures
      1. Upon the completion of a JHA/Risk Assessment where control measures have been implemented, those measures shall be assessed after implementation to ensure the measures are operating as required to determine if those measures have achieved an acceptable level of control for the hazard identified.
      2. All JHA/risk assessments must be reviewed in alignment with the frequency of the work instruction it applies to, when a new task is identified or where the process, machinery & equipment or substances used in the process change. This will address any hazards introduced as a result of those changes.
      3. Global JHAs shall be completed by each GNL and fundamental team for identification of specific hazards and PPE requirements for each work instructions. Approval of each JHA/Risk Assessment should be completed by Global EHS and referenced in the corresponding work instruction.
      4. All high risk Hazards shall be documented using EHS-02-112-F 002 Job Hazard Analysis Risk Register for further control measures to be worked on.
