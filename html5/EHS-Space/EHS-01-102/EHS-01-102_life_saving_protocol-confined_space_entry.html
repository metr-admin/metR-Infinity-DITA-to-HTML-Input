Life Saving Protocol - Confined Space Entry

Purpose

Space has developed a set of mandatory Life Saving Protocols that set a minimum standard and minimum performance expectations for managing high-level hazards common to the glass industry.

Application

This procedure applies to all manufacturing facilities and suppliers to Space.

Scope

  1. This protocol applies to all Space employees, contractors and visitors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities, technical centers, offices, and administrative buildings. All sites in all regions are expected to develop site specific procedures to comply with this protocol.
  2. Requirements of this protocol will apply to the control of risks associated with confined spaces in workplaces. Confined spaces include spaces such as those in a vat, tank, pit, pipe, duct, flue, oven, chimney, silo, reaction vessel, container, receptacle, underground sewer, well, shaft, trench, tunnel, or other similar enclosed or partially enclosed structure, which meet certain conditions.

Policy

  1. A formal and documented risk assessment must be completed prior to undertaking any work in a confined space. Work in a confined space must be avoided and only conducted if all other options have been exhausted.
  2. The hazards of the confined space must be abated/mitigated prior to entry. Examples of abatement include: atmospheric monitoring, ventilation, permit, PPE, signage, and Lockout Tagout Tryout.
  3. All Confined Spaces at the site shall be identified, labeled, and documented in a register.
  4. Documented entry procedures shall be created that include the potential hazards present in the confined space and the abatement/mitigation actions to render the space safe for human entry.
  5. Confined space entry permit documentation must be completed and authorized by a trained person prior to any work being undertaken in a confined space.
  6. Only competently trained and properly equipped personnel shall enter a confined space.
  7. Ensure that an adequate communications system is in place to enable communication between people inside and outside the confined space and to summon help in an emergency.
  8. An emergency response and rescue plan shall be established prior to entry and equipment required to execute that plan shall be present before entry begins.
  9. Access and egress points must be in place and clear at all times.
  10. A standby person shall be assigned to remain outside of the entrance to a confined space at all times a person is working within.
