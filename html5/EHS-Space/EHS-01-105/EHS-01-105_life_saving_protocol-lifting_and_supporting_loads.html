Life Saving Protocol - Lifting and Supporting Loads

Purpose

Space has developed a set of mandatory Life Saving Protocols that set a minimum standard and minimum performance expectations for managing high-level hazards common to the glass industry.

Scope

  1. This protocol applies to all Space employees, contractors and visitors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities, technical centers, offices, and administrative buildings. All sites in all regions are expected to develop site specific procedures to comply with this protocol.
  2. Requirements of this protocol will apply to the control of risks associated with lifting loads using lifting devices such as cranes, hoists and winches. Specifically, this protocol relates to the risks associated with working in and around loads being lifted above the ground.

Equipment Management

  1. All lifting equipment, including slings and attachments, must be formally documented in a register.
  2. Ensure that lifting equipment is registered, inspected, and certified in accordance with local legislative requirements.
  3. Ensure that the safe working load tag/rating plate is displayed on all lifting equipment and confirm compliance with periodic inspections.
  4. Undertake periodic inspections, testing, and maintenance of lifting equipment using a trained person. Inspections must include structural and mechanical elements and refer to relevant standards for guidance.
  5. Undertake pre-operation inspections to check condition before use and remove from service if damaged.

Risk Assessments and Competence

  1. A documented risk assessment and/or a job safety analysis must be completed by a trained person prior to using any lifting device.
  2. Operators must be trained on the lifting equipment or cranes being operated. Training must be documented and available upon request.

Lift Preparation and Execution for complex lifts

  1. Develop clear criteria for what constitutes a complex lift and require such lifts to have a documented risk assessment and lift plan completed prior to each complex lifting activity.
  2. Ensure that a pre-start checklist/inspection is completed prior to operating the lifting equipment.
  3. Verify the ground is suitable for safe operation of cranes and, where they are fitted, deploy outriggers.
  4. Ensure that the area of the lifting operation is adequately illuminated and external conditions are assessed and controlled.
  5. Only operate cranes, hoists, and lifting equipment within the specified lifting rating. Ensure that the rated load capacity of a crane's bridge, individual hoist, or any sling or fitting is not exceeded. Know the weight of the object being lifted.
  6. Engage loads properly on hooks with hook latch in lock position where fitted.
  7. Isolate the work area and define and manage the operational area using barricades and spotters to prevent the risk of injury from the equipment or the load falling or striking people.
  8. Persons must not under any circumstances enter an area under a suspended load, and never leave suspended loads unattended.
  9. Complex lifts must be supervised and tag lines must be used where a load requires steadying or guidance.
