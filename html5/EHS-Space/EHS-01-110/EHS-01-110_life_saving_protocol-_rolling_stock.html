Life Saving Protocol- Rolling Stock

Purpose

Space has developed a set of mandatory Life Saving Protocols that set a minimum standard and minimum performance expectations for managing high-level hazards common to the glass industry.

Scope

  1. This protocol applies to all Space employees, contractors and visitors, and all operations including manufacturing facilities, recycling centers, and mines. All sites in all regions with rolling stock are expected to develop site specific procedures to comply with this protocol.
  2. Requirements of this protocol will apply to the control of risks associated with working on or around rolling stock.

Equipment Management

  1. Ensure that all rolling stock is inspected and certified in accordance with local legislative requirements.
  2. Undertake pre-operation inspections to check condition before operating. Remove from service if damaged or malfunctioning.
  3. Undertake periodic inspections, testing, and maintenance of rolling stock to maintain the highest standards of operation. Inspections shall include structural and mechanical elements and refer to relevant standards for guidance.
  4. All maintenance shall be performed to the manufacturer’s specifications.
  5. Equipment shall not be used to exceed manufacturer’s performance, such as overloading capacity of the equipment to move or stop.
  6. Do not disable any safety devices (such as horns, bells, etc.) on the equipment.

Risk Assessments and Competence

  1. A documented risk assessment and/or job hazard analysis shall be completed prior to operation of rolling stock.
  2. Operators shall be trained in the operation of rolling stock. Training must be documented and available upon request.
  3. Personnel involved in the movement and loading/unloading of rolling stock shall be properly trained to manage or avoid risks associated with such activities.

Working On or Around Rolling Stock

  1. Remain alert and aware of surroundings. Locomotives, railcars, and other rolling stock equipment may move without warning on any track in either direction.
  2. Never sit, walk, stand, or place any objects on the rails, ties, switch gear, guardrails, or other parts of the track structure. Be aware that tracks can move.
  3. When two parties are operating on the same track at the same time, a written operating procedure shall be developed in conjunction with the local servicing railroad and utilized at the location by the operators.
  4. Any time work is being performed on rolling stock while it is still on the tracks, a visible flag and/or “Men Working” sign shall be clearly displayed.
  5. A communication system shall be established to ensure that all employees know where others are working in and around rolling stock. This system may be radio contact, air horns, hand signals, or other forms of communication.
  6. When loading, unloading, and when dock boards or bridge plates are in place, hand brakes shall be set. Wheel stops and other recognized positive protection shall be used to prevent the rolling stock from moving.
    1. Dock boards or bridge plates must be sufficient to carry the imposed load.
    2. Dock boards or bridge plates must be secured in position with anchoring or equipped with devices which will prevent their slippage.
    3. Powered dock boards shall be designed to appropriate commercial standards.
    4. Handholds shall be provided on portable dock boards to assist with moving.
  7. Mobile equipment shall not be used to open or close rail car doors.
  8. Mobile equipment shall not be driven up to anyone standing within the rail car.
  9. Working on or accessing the top side of a rail car or other rolling stock will often constitute Working at Heights and will require equipment for fall protection. Please refer to EHS-02-103 Working at Heights Procedure.
  10. Workers loading/unloading materials shall be instructed on safe procedures appropriate to the material they handle.
  11. The rolling stock operator shall be aware of the maximum number of empty and loaded railcars that can be attached based on the size of the locomotive.
  12. During any movement and when approaching crossings, the locomotive or towing equipment shall sound its horn, or another form of alert if local legislation restricts noise.
  13. The locomotive or other power equipment shall be operated at a safe speed at all times.

Railway Clearances

  1. Safe clearance vertically and horizontally is required around railway right of ways. This means no structures, abutments, or obstructions along the track where either rail or Space employees may need access. This includes unloading shed access ways or other structures along tracks.
  2. Railway clearances shall be in accordance to local legislation and communicated to all affected employees.
  3. Any areas not meeting these clearance requirements shall be posted with regional railroad standard “NO CLEARANCE” signs.
