Electronic Chute Control II System Testing

Purpose and Application

Purpose

To define a work instruction for testing the electronic chute control II system. This document is designed to prevent future failures.

Application

This work instruction applies to Space manufacturing facilities.

Safety

Observe Space standard safety requirements and procedures defined by the Global Environmental Health , andamp; Safety (EH, andamp;S) organization, as well as all applicable local, regional, and national requirements. Safety equipment and instructions specific to the completion of this work instruction are detailed in the Instructions Section.

Instructions

  1. Before starting the work instruction activity, perform the following steps:
    1. Do not begin these tasks without confirmation that personnel directly involved have completed the required training associated with the work instruction activity and have reviewed this document.
    2. Gather necessary tools and equipment as identified below in the Equipment Section.
    3. Wear and use additional Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safety equipment required for this specific work instruction
    4. Obey all applicable safety requirements and procedures
    5. Follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures when work instruction activity requires isolating energy in the designated area. Verify energy is isolated before starting work.
  2. To ensure the functionality of the chute control system, perform the following steps:
    Note:

    These steps should be performed during preventative maintenance, shutdowns, job changes, and/or production down time.

    Refer to drawings OIS-03002, OIS-03394, OIS-03429, and SCS-5-40.

    Setup parameters for the servo controller, and a maintenance record, have been provided in the Appendix to aid in logging operating parameters and work performed on the pneumatic chute control system.

    1. Verify that the chute air supply is high pressure, dry (instrument) air.
      Note:

      On old installation drawings, 3.4 bar (50 psi) was specified. The new standard is to supply 6.9 bar (100 psi) for chute air.

    2. Verify that the air pressure switch tubing is connected before the check valve and receiver tank. (Reference the “L-P” airline on the above mentioned drawings.)
      1. Calibrate and set the pressure switch to 3.1 bar (45 psi).
      2. Adjust the regulator to operate at 3.8 to 4.1 Bar (55 to 60 psi).
    3. Verify that the air hoses are not cut or damaged.
      Note:

      Use braided stainless steel supply hoses, not rubber hoses.

    4. Repair or replace the following solenoid valves, pressure switches, and proximity switches once per year. The quantity and part number for each component are shown below.
      1. 1 shear lockout solenoid valve 115 VAC (HF-3556)
      2. 4 pressure switches 24 VDC (chute, machine “A”, machine “B”, plunger cool) (OIS-204-665)
      3. 1 pilot air pressure switch 24 VDC (SCS-3-329)
      4. 1 gob reject solenoid valve 24 VDC (OIS-204-3159)
      5. 1 guide funnel solenoid valve 24 VDC (OIS-204-3108)
      6. 1 chute solenoid valve 24 VDC (OIS-204-3108)
      7. 1 gob accelerator solenoid valve 24 VDC (OIS-204-3108)
      8. 1 upper funnel solenoid valve 24 VDC (OIS-204-3108)
      9. 1 chute water flow solenoid valve 24 VDC (OIS-204-3178)
      10. 3 proximity switches 24 VDC (OIS-204-3133)
    5. On the MICC 5480, MICC Laser, Flexdrive II, and Flexdrive III, there are two ammeter relays: one for the feeder, and one for the gob distributor. There is a high current trip, and a low current trip.
      1. Approximate guidelines for the feeder: The high current setpoint should be at 3 amps above the operating current, and the low current setpoint should be at 3 amps below the operating current.
      2. Approximate guidelines for the gob distributor: The high current setpoint should be at 2 amps above the operating current, and the low current setpoint should be at 2 amps below the operating current.
      3. These setpoints can be adjusted to be closer to, or farther from, the operating current depending on the mechanical cam and the motor current variation.
      4. The operating current should be observed and the setpoints set, based on the amount of fluctuation in the current.
      5. Adjustments may be required due to shear cam changes during job changes.
    6. If Servo Needles, Servo Shears, and/or Servo Gob Distributor are installed, there is a chute enable relay on the SDFL Interface board (SCS-0314). This relay enables the chute to go up if the Needles, Shears, or Gob Distributor stop for any reason.
      1. There is also a pressure switch (OIS-204-665) used to monitor the air supplied to the lockout solenoid valve. Calibrate test this switch, and then set it at 3.1 bar (45 psi).
  3. To test the chute control system, perform the following steps:
    1. Lower the tube down to shut off the glass flow. Then plug the orifice to ensure that glass will not flow.
    2. Start the feeder, shears, and gob distributor.
    3. Ensure that setup/operating parameters are correct. Refer to the ECCSII manual, section 4.
    4. Place the chute control system in operating mode. To operate the chute control system in operating mode, perform the following sequence
      1. Bring the funnels into the glass.
      2. Bring the reject cylinder into the glass.
      3. Bring the chute down.
      4. Bring the gob accelerator into the glass (if applicable).
    5. Stop the feeder. When the feeder stops, the gob accelerator will retract, the chute will come up, the reject cylinder will retract, and the guide funnels will retract.
      1. Restart the feeder, shears, and gob distributor.
      2. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 6 below
    6. Stop the shears. When the shears stop, the gob accelerator will retract, the chute will come up, the reject cylinder will retract, and the guide funnels will retract.
      1. Restart the shears.
      2. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 7 below
    7. With the gob distributor run/setup switch in the run position, stop the gob distributor. When the gob distributor stops, the gob accelerator will retract, the chute will come up, the reject cylinder will retract, and the guide funnels will retract
      1. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 8 below
    8. With the gob distributor run/setup switch in the setup position, start the gob distributor. Stop the gob distributor. When it stops the chute should not come up. Remember to put the switch back in the run position when loading glass.
      1. Restart the gob distributor.
      2. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 9 below
    9. Push the chute-up button on the main panel. This will cause the gob accelerator to retract, the chute to come up, and the funnels and reject cylinders to retract.
      1. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 10 below.
    10. Push the button on the main panel that retracts the reject cylinder out of glass. This will cause the gob accelerator to retract, the chute to come up, the reject cylinder to retract, and the guide funnels to retract.
      1. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 11 below.
    11. Push the emergency chute-up button on the main panel. This will cause the gob accelerator to retract, the chute to come up, the reject cylinder to retract, and the guide funnels to retract
      1. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 12 below.
    12. Push the button on the remote panel that retracts the reject cylinder out of glass. The gob accelerator will retract, the chute will come up, the reject cylinder will retract, and the funnels will retract.
      1. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 13 below.
    13. Push the chute-up button on the remote panel. The gob accelerator will retract, the chute will come up, and the funnel and reject cylinders will retract.
      1. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 14 below.
    14. Push the emergency chute-up stand-alone button. This will cause the gob accelerator to retract, the chute will come up, the reject cylinder will retract, and the guide funnels will retract.
      1. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 15 below.
    15. Individually test and calibrate the chute air, machine “A” air, machine “B” air, plunger cooling, and pilot air pressure switches.
      1. Rotate the dial to a setpoint higher than the operating pressure.
      2. When the set point on the dial goes above the operating pressure, the gob accelerator will retract, the chute will come up, the reject cylinder will retract, and the guide funnels will retract.
      3. At the point that the pressure switch trips, pull off the dial and set it at the proper position determined by the chute air pressure gauge.
      4. Then adjust the dial to be 0.34-0.69 bar (5-10 psi) below the operating pressure.
        Note:

        For machines that change processes, the pressure switches must be set for the proper pressure.

        Note:

        To save production time, pre-calibrate the spare pressure switches before use. This can be done by using a calibrated air source.

      5. Repeat step 4 above, then go to step 16 below.
    16. Test the water flow solenoid (if present) by moving the chute to the up position. Water flow should automatically increase.
      1. Next, move the chute down and press the glass switch to reject gobs.
      2. If the number for the gob count is greater than the parameter setpoint in the servo system, the water flow will increase
        1. The parameter is adjustable on the Servo Display Station.
        2. For example, if the parameter for number of rejects in one machine cycle before high water flow is set at two, then, when two sections are rejected in one machine cycle, the water flow should increase.
      3. For the high water flow to reduce to low water flow, if the reject count is below the Rejects Before High Flow parameter, the count begins for the Cycles Before Low Flow parameter.
      4. The water flow will automatically decrease when the reject count is below the Rejects Before High Flow parameter for the specified number of cycles, which ranges from 1 to 8 machine cycles.
      5. Refer to the Electronic Chute Control II manual, section 4.
    17. Adjust the speed screws, located on the solenoid valves (which are used for regulating how quickly the funnel cylinder, reject cylinder, gob accelerator cylinder, and chute cylinder move), to a proper setting.
      1. The mechanisms should extend and retract fast enough to provide adequate safety conditions, yet not so fast so that the mechanisms are slamming. Slamming causes the cylinders to stick in an unsafe condition.
    18. Refer to Appendix A for the setup parameter information for the servo controller.
    19. Refer to Appendix B for s a setup and maintenance record for the plant engineer and/or machine repair supervisor to insure that proper testing, repair, and operation of the chute control are completed for each machine.
  4. Upon completion of tasks, perform the following steps:
    1. After the designated work area is confirmed clear and personnel are aware of the pending restart, follow all Lockout/Tagout procedures to restart energy supplied to the designated area. Verify energy is active.
    2. Notify appropriate personnel that the work instruction activity has been completed.
    3. Record and report findings and results.
    4. Follow appropriate instructions for notification of findings and results relating to specifications, targets and/or reaction limits.

Required Training

Before performing this work instruction, applicable personnel must be trained by an authorized trainer on the required training contents listed in this document.
